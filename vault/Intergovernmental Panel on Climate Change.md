# Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change
#organization

### AF 2021-08-11 0858
(00:02:03) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] discussed a new report from the IPCC, described as the most important assessment since 2013, which concluded that climate change is unequivocally caused by human activity and is preventable through global government intervention.

(00:03:05) The IPCC report advocated for reduced carbon footprints through measures like using public transportation, living in smaller apartments, and eating less meat to prevent catastrophic temperature changes and reduce natural disasters.
(00:11:52) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that the IPCC report was commissioned to catalyze action by global governments and consumers to change behaviors and prevent worsening climate change.
(00:15:03) A [[BBC]] summary of the IPCC report stated that humanity's damaging impact on the climate is a "statement of fact," with ongoing emissions risking the breach of a key temperature limit in just over a decade and a potential two-meter sea-level rise by 2100.
(00:16:12) [[United Nations]] Secretary-General [[António Guterres]] described the IPCC Working Group I report as a "code red for humanity," calling for combined forces to avert climate catastrophe and for government leaders to ensure the success of the upcoming [[COP26]] summit in [[Glasgow]].
(00:20:17) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that the IPCC report, released during the [[Joe Biden|Biden]] administration after an eight-year hiatus since the [[Obama]] administration, used arbitrary metrics and alarmist rhetoric to push for reduced greenhouse gas emissions.
(00:22:06) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] claimed the IPCC had made 135 failed predictions over 25 years, with models consistently overestimating warming and its effects, such as incorrectly forecasting that [[New York]] would be underwater by specific dates.
(00:33:36) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] cited the IPCC report's claim that the past 50 years have seen the fastest warming in 2000 years, but dismissed this as cherry-picked data given the [[Earth]]'s 4.6-billion-year history, expressing skepticism towards expert alarmism.
(00:53:58) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] concluded that the IPCC report explicitly calls for global government, prison-like population control, and the eradication of human freedom and prosperity under the guise of preventing climate catastrophe.
