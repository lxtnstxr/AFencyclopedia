# Shadow Inc.
#organization

### AF 2020-02-05 0540
(01:03:20) The 2020 Iowa [[Democratic Party|Democratic]] caucus results were significantly delayed due to a malfunction in a reporting app developed by the company Shadow Inc., leaving only 87% of precincts reporting days after the event.

(01:14:38) The [[Democratic Party|Iowa Democratic Party]] paid Shadow Inc. $63,000 to develop the caucus reporting app that later malfunctioned, with the company having ties to veterans of [[Hillary Clinton]]'s 2016 presidential campaign.
(01:14:58) Shadow Inc. was a subsidiary of the nonprofit [[Acronym]], founded by political strategist [[Tara McGowan]], [[who]] is married to [[Michael Hale]], a senior strategist for the [[Peter Buttigieg|Pete Buttigieg]] presidential campaign.
(01:15:20) The [[Peter Buttigieg|Pete Buttigieg]] presidential campaign paid Shadow Inc. $42,500 for software, creating a financial link between the campaign and the company responsible for the malfunctioning Iowa caucus reporting app.
(01:52:58) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that the company Acronym owns the voting app company Shadow Inc..
