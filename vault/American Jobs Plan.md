### AF 2021-04-28 0801
(00:15:19) [[Joe Biden]] proposed the American Jobs Plan, which he described as a once-in-a-generation investment and the largest jobs plan since [[World War II]].

(00:15:41) The American Jobs Plan aimed to create jobs modernizing roads, bridges, highways, ports, airports, and rail corridors.
(00:16:37) The American Jobs Plan aimed to create jobs connecting every [[United States|American]] with high-speed internet, including 35% of rural [[United States|America]].
(00:17:03) The American Jobs Plan included building a modern power grid to address vulnerabilities exposed by winter storms in [[State of Texas|Texas]].
(00:19:17) The American Jobs Plan aimed to put engineers and construction workers to work building energy-efficient buildings and electrical workers installing 500,000 electric vehicle charging stations.
(00:19:42) The American Jobs Plan included paying farmers to plant cover crops to reduce carbon dioxide in the air.
(00:20:45) [[Joe Biden]]'s American Jobs Plan was guided by a "Buy [[United States|American]]" principle, a slogan [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] noted was previously used by [[Donald Trump]].
(00:22:27) [[Joe Biden]] stated that nearly 90% of the infrastructure jobs in the American Jobs Plan did not require a college degree, and 75% did not require an associate's degree.
