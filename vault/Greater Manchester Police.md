### AF 2020-01-16 0526
(00:25:40) A report by [[The Independent]] revealed that the Greater Manchester Police failed to act on dozens of cases where teenage girls were groomed and abused by gangs of men from Asian backgrounds, due to fears of upsetting race relations.

(00:29:20) Operation Augusta, launched in 2004 after the death of a 15-year-old girl, identified at least 57 victims and 97 suspects involved in grooming across the [[Manchester]] [[region]], but was under-resourced and shut down by the Greater Manchester Police and [[Manchester City Council]].
(00:30:40) The Greater Manchester Police secured only three convictions out of 97 identified suspects in the grooming gang scandal, with the report concluding that perpetrators were allowed to continue their activities.
