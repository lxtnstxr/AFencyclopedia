# Walter Wallace Jr.
#person

### AF 2020-10-28 0711
(01:42:48) [[City of Philadelphia|Philadelphia]] imposed a citywide curfew from 9 p.m. to 6 a.m. following two nights of riots and looting that resulted in 81 arrests, 297 reports of looting, 23 injured officers, and widespread vandalism, sparked by the police shooting of Walter Wallace Jr..

(01:50:20) Walter Wallace Jr., whose shooting by police sparked riots in [[City of Philadelphia|Philadelphia]], was an aspiring rapper with a criminal record including a guilty plea for assaulting a police officer in 2013 and a 2017 guilty plea for robbery, assault, and putting a gun to a woman's head after kicking down her door; he was awaiting trial for allegedly threatening to shoot up another woman's [[house]] at the [[time]] of his death.
(01:56:28) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that a segment of the population, including violent repeat offenders like Walter Wallace Jr., is a "net negative" that cannot be reformed and must be permanently managed through incarceration or institutionalization rather than accommodated by changing societal laws or police practices.
