## Summary

The October 7th Hamas attack on Israel was analyzed as a secret, independent operation intended to disrupt the normalization of relations between Israel and Saudi Arabia, which it successfully stalled. In response, Hezbollah mobilized but, following a speech by Hassan Nasrallah, calibrated its actions to avoid a full-scale regional war, allowing Israel to proceed with a ground invasion of Gaza. Discussions regarding Gaza's future administration involved potential roles for Abraham Accords countries, while one commentator characterized the situation as a strategic victory for Israel that would advance its regional integration.
