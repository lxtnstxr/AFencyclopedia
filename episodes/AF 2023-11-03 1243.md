## Summary

Hezbollah leader Hassan Nasrallah delivered a highly anticipated speech in which he did not declare war on Israel, signaling a decision against full-scale escalation despite the ongoing conflict. Concurrently, U.S. Secretary of State Antony Blinken affirmed support for Israel and discussed post-conflict plans for Gaza, involving potential administration by a coalition of Arab states. Commentator Nicholas J. Fuentes argued the conflict had cooled to Israel's strategic advantage, allowing its military operation in Gaza to proceed without triggering a broader regional war.
