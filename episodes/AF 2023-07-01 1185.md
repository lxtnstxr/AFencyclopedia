## Summary

Widespread riots, described as the most violent in France since 2005, erupted following the police shooting of a 17-year-old, Nahel M., during a traffic stop in Nanterre, leading to mass arrests and a major security deployment. Concurrently, the U.S. Supreme Court issued several landmark rulings, ending the consideration of race in college admissions, striking down President Joe Biden's student loan forgiveness plan, and ruling in favor of a web designer's right to refuse service for same-sex weddings. Political commentator Nicholas J. Fuentes also promoted an upcoming rally and argued that former President Donald Trump possessed a unique political will to use federal power for issues like immigration.
