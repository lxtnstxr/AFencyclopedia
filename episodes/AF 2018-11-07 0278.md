## Summary

The Republican Party gained seats in the United States Senate but lost control of the United States House of Representatives to the Democratic Party in the 2018 midterm elections. Nicholas J. Fuentes attributed Republican legislative failures to congressional leadership and credited Donald Trump with mobilizing the party's base. Exit polls showed significant demographic voting disparities, with majorities of women, black, Hispanic, and Asian voters supporting the Democratic Party.

---

(00:00:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] hosted a monologue analyzing the results of the 2018 United States midterm elections.

(00:01:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] had predicted the [[Republican Party]] would gain between three and five seats in the [[United States Senate]] and face a difficult fight for control of the [[United States House of Representatives]].

(00:03:38) In the 2018 midterms, the [[Republican Party]] flipped [[United States Senate]] seats in [[North Dakota]], [[Indiana]], and [[Missouri]], defeating incumbents including [[Claire McCaskill]].

(00:04:36) [[Rick Scott]] held a lead in the [[Florida]] [[United States Senate]] race with all votes counted, while the [[Republican Party]] held the [[Arizona]] Senate seat.

(00:05:39) [[Mitt Romney]] won election to the [[United States Senate]] from [[Utah]], replacing several retiring Republican senators.

(00:05:59) The [[Republican Party]] lost [[United States Senate]] races in [[West Virginia]] and [[New Jersey]], with [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] attributing the West Virginia loss to a Libertarian candidate splitting the vote.

(00:06:34) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that Republican Senate candidates in [[Ohio]] and [[Michigan]] who did not embrace [[Donald Trump]] lost their elections.

(00:07:08) The [[Democratic Party]] won control of the [[United States House of Representatives]] in the 2018 midterms, securing 222 seats to the [[Republican Party]]'s 196, a net gain of 27 seats for Democrats.

(00:09:39) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] contrasted the 2018 midterm results, where [[Donald Trump]]'s party lost 27 House seats, with the 2010 midterms where [[Barack Obama]]'s party lost 63 House seats.

(00:10:25) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that historically, a president's party typically loses seats in the first midterm election, making the [[Republican Party]]'s Senate gains and modest House losses an atypical outcome.

(00:14:14) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] attributed the [[Republican Party]]'s failure to repeal the Affordable Care Act in 2017 to congressional leadership, citing [[John McCain]]'s decisive vote against the legislation.

(00:16:02) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] blamed [[Paul Ryan]] and [[Mitch McConnell]] for passing a large omnibus spending bill in early 2018 that did not fund [[Donald Trump]]'s border wall.
