## Summary

Milo Yiannopoulos planned a "Free Speech Week" at the University of California, Berkeley for September 23-25, 2017, but the event collapsed after most invited speakers withdrew and the student organizing group failed to complete required paperwork. Nicholas J. Fuentes and James Allsup planned a college tour opposing immigration under the slogan "you will not replace white people," while Fuentes advocated for a white identity movement based on demographic concerns. At the United Nations, Justin Trudeau described Canada as a "work in progress" and apologized for its treatment of indigenous peoples, while Donald Trump delivered a speech characterized by an aggressive tone.
