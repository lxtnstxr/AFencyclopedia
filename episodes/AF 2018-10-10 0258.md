## Summary

The organization Canary Mission compiles dossiers on students and activists critical of Israel to harm their job prospects. An investigation identified the Jewish Community Federation of San Francisco, through its Helen Diller Family Foundation, as a primary donor to Canary Mission. Donald Trump authored a USA Today op-ed characterizing the Democratic Party as a radical socialist party promoting "open border socialism." Nicholas J. Fuentes contested Ben Shapiro's claim that accusing a Jew of being "Israel First" is anti-Semitic.

---

(00:02:00) The organization [[Canary Mission]] maintains a website that compiles dossiers on students and activists who criticize [[Israel]], with the stated purpose of harming their job prospects and which have reportedly been used in interrogations by Israeli security officials.

(00:07:00) [[Donald Trump]] authored an op-ed in [[USA Today]] titled "Democrats’ ‘Medicare for All’ plan will demolish promises to seniors," in which he characterized the [[Democratic Party]] as a radical socialist party that seeks to model the [[United States]] economy after [[Venezuela]].

(00:08:20) In his op-ed, [[Donald Trump]] argued that the [[Democratic Party]]'s support for open borders, combined with proposals for government-run healthcare, would allow millions of illegal immigrants to take advantage of benefits paid for by American taxpayers.

(00:12:40) [[Donald Trump]]'s op-ed strategy sought to exploit a rift within the [[Democratic Party]] between its progressive, socialist wing and its older, more pragmatic establishment, aiming to force Democrats to either alienate their base or prove his characterization of them as radicals.

(00:16:20) [[Donald Trump]] linked the policy concept of "open border socialism" in his op-ed, a rhetorical move that connected concerns about the size of government with immigration, a strategy previously discussed by economist [[Milton Friedman]].

(00:30:20) An investigation by Israeli newspaper [[Haaretz]] and [[The Forward]] identified the [[Jewish Community Federation of San Francisco]], a major Jewish charity with over $100 million in annual budget, as a primary donor to the [[Canary Mission]] organization through a supporting foundation.

(00:35:40) The [[Helen Diller Family Foundation]], a supporting charity of the [[Jewish Community Federation of San Francisco]] whose board members include former [[Bank of America]] chairman [[Richard Rosenberg]], was found to have earmarked $100,000 for [[Canary Mission]].

(00:37:20) Funds from the [[Helen Diller Family Foundation]] were directed to [[Canary Mission]] via the [[Central Fund of Israel]] and an Israeli public benefit corporation called [[Megamot Shalom]], which was established to ensure "the national image and strength of the state of Israel" through information dissemination.

(00:40:40) The [[Helen Diller Family Foundation]] also provided funding to [[Project Veritas]], run by [[James O'Keefe]], the [[American Freedom Defense Initiative]] founded by [[Robert Spencer]] and [[Pamela Geller]], and a foundation initiated by Dutch politician [[Geert Wilders]].

(00:45:40) [[Ben Shapiro]] stated on Twitter that accusing a Jew of being "Israel First" is a sign of anti-Semitism, a claim [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] contested as unpatriotic rather than hateful.

(00:47:20) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] claimed that a secretly recorded video of him saying "Jews hurt me in my daily existence" was circulated by commentator [[Ben Shapiro]] and [[Benny Politic]] of the [[Reagan Battalion]] in an attempt to ruin his reputation.

(00:54:20) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] criticized commentator [[Jack Posobiec]] as uncharismatic and unremarkable, citing an appearance on [[One America News]] as evidence of a lack of meritocracy in conservative media.

(00:58:50) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated he would be willing to debate white nationalist writer [[Greg Johnson]] on the topic of paganism versus Christianity, but not on his own show due to Johnson's controversial phrasing.
