## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes cited New York Times and Vox.com articles discussing demographic changes in New Hampshire and the concept of a "white threat." He highlighted numerous tweets from verified users, including Andy Cohen, a BuzzFeed reporter, and others, expressing anti-white sentiment or calls for violence against Donald Trump. Fuentes argued that demographic projections showed white populations declining significantly in America, Canada, and globally by 2100. He claimed the African National Congress amended South Africa's constitution to allow land expropriation without compensation following the killings of thousands of white farmers. Fuentes also referenced the "Clean Break Memo," a strategy document by pro-Israel thinkers advocating for the destruction of Iraq and Syria to secure Israel's borders.
