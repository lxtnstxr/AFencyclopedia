## Summary

Iran shot down an unmanned American spy drone, with Donald Trump and Iran disputing its location. Trump called off a retaliatory airstrike on Iran ten minutes before execution and detailed the decision on Twitter. U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement planned raids targeting 2,000 illegal immigrants across ten cities. The United Nations promoted insect-based foods as sustainable dietary alternatives aligned with the Paris Agreement.

---

(00:01:32) [[Iran]] shot down an unmanned American spy drone, which [[Donald Trump]] stated was flying in international waters, though [[Iran]] claimed it was in its airspace.

(00:01:45) [[Donald Trump]] called off a planned retaliatory airstrike on [[Iran]] ten minutes before it was to commence, which he stated would have killed approximately 150 people at three different sites.

(00:02:11) [[Donald Trump]] posted a detailed account of the canceled strike on [[Iran]] to his Twitter account, framing the decision as a rejection of a disproportionate response.

(00:02:38) [[John Bolton]], the National Security Advisor, and [[Mike Pompeo]], the Secretary of State, presented the military strike option to [[Donald Trump]].

(00:03:47) U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement ([[ICE]]) planned raids targeting 2,000 illegal immigrants with court-ordered removals across ten cities, with the potential for additional "collateral" arrests.

(00:05:37) The anime series *Neon Genesis Evangelion* became available for streaming on [[Netflix]].

(00:05:52) [[Google]] published and promoted an article featuring a drag queen persona named "Anna Lytical," created by its engineer [[Billy Jacobson]], who uses the character to teach coding on [[YouTube]].

(00:08:50) [[Google]], [[Facebook]], [[Goldman Sachs]], and major Hollywood production companies were identified as powerful entities promoting drag culture and related content.

(00:09:39) [[Chips Ahoy!]] featured a drag queen in a Mother's Day advertisement.

(00:11:44) The [[United Nations]] promoted insect-based foods like mealworms and cricket bars as sustainable dietary alternatives to reduce emissions, in line with the goals of the Paris Agreement.

(00:12:35) A transgender individual shot up a school in [[Colorado]], with reports indicating the motive was classmates questioning the shooter's gender identity.

(00:17:10) Rumors indicated that the upcoming [[ICE]] raids would target 2,000 individuals, a number [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] contrasted with the 100,000 illegal immigrants apprehended at the border in May.

(00:17:48) Deportation numbers under the [[Donald Trump]] administration were lower than those under the administrations of [[Barack Obama]] and [[George W. Bush]].

(00:24:58) [[Shinzo Abe]], the Prime Minister of [[Japan]], was in [[Tehran]] for a diplomatic visit at the time of the tanker incidents in the Strait of Hormuz.

(00:25:21) The [[United States]] deployed 1,500 troops and an additional aircraft carrier to the Middle East following the tanker incidents, which it blamed on [[Iran]].

(00:29:32) [[Ben Shapiro]] tweeted a thread advocating for a "credible threat of disproportionate force" as a deterrence strategy against [[Iran]], arguing that "confusion and vacillation undermine deterrence."

(00:30:29) Commentators [[Bret Stephens]], [[Ben Shapiro]], and [[Bill Kristol]] were described as repeating the mantra that "no one wants war with [[Iran]]."

(00:31:14) [[Saudi Arabia]], the [[United Arab Emirates]], [[Israel]], the Israel lobby, the military-industrial complex, [[Mike Pompeo]], and [[John Bolton]] were all identified as entities that want war with [[Iran]].

(00:32:28) [[Donald Trump]] maintained residual military forces in [[Iraq]], [[Afghanistan]], and [[Syria]], contrary to campaign promises to withdraw.

(00:33:22) Under [[Donald Trump]], the [[United States]] explicitly abandoned the goal of regime change in [[Syria]], as announced by then-UN Ambassador [[Nikki Haley]] and Secretary of State [[Rex Tillerson]].

(00:47:47) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that an [[NBC]] producer contacted him to appear on a panel to discuss the Charlottesville rally and a mosque shooting, seeking a "white nationalist" perspective, but he declined the invitation.
