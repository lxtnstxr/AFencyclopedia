## Summary

The Bashar al-Assad government, with Russia and Iran, planned a military offensive in Idlib province in northern Syria. Turkey opposed the Idlib offensive due to concerns over a refugee crisis, while the United States opposed it as a strategic defeat for its policy of Assad's removal. Russia maintained military bases in Tartus and Latakia, Syria, and sought Assad's victory to protect these assets.
