## Summary

Ruth Bader Ginsburg missed Supreme Court oral arguments for the first time after surgery for lung cancer. The United States government entered the 16th day of a partial shutdown with no congressional agreement on border wall funding. Donald Trump floated declaring a national emergency to build a border wall, an option Nicholas J. Fuentes argued lacked cabinet support. Nicholas J. Fuentes stated that pro-Israel interests prevented the Trump administration from executing a withdrawal from Syria.

---

(00:06:50) [[Ruth Bader Ginsburg]] missed oral arguments in the [[Supreme Court of the United States|Supreme Court]] for the first time in her career while recuperating from surgery to remove cancerous tumors on her lungs, a condition discovered after a fall in November that broke her ribs.

(00:08:09) [[Ruth Bader Ginsburg]] declined to retire during the presidency of [[Barack Obama]] when the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]] controlled both the Senate and the House of Representatives, a decision that left her as a pivotal figure on the court regarding abortion.

(00:11:45) The [[United States federal government|United States government]] entered the 16th day of a partial shutdown, approaching the longest shutdown in American history, with no agreement in Congress on funding for a [[Trump border wall|border wall]].

(00:13:44) A negotiating team consisting of [[Mike Pence]], [[Mick Mulvaney]], and [[Jared Kushner]] had delivered no substantive "America First" policy victories to the [[Donald Trump|Trump]] administration, according to [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]].

(00:15:38) [[Donald Trump]] announced a nationally televised address from the [[White House]] to make the case for a border wall, followed by a trip to the U.S.-Mexico border to meet with [[United States Border Patrol|Border Patrol]] agents.

(00:20:13) [[Donald Trump]] publicly floated the possibility of declaring a national emergency to build a border wall, an option [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued was not feasible due to a lack of support within the cabinet and legal challenges.

(00:22:20) Key [[Donald Trump|Trump]] administration officials, including [[Mick Mulvaney]], former chief of staff [[John F. Kelly|John Kelly]], former Secretary of Homeland Security [[Kirstjen Nielsen]], and former Secretary of Defense [[James Mattis|James Mattis]], were not supportive of building a border wall, according to [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]].

(00:29:38) [[Donald Trump]] had announced a withdrawal of all 2,000 U.S. troops from [[Syria]] within 30 days in December, but the timeline was later extended to four months and then abandoned, with no withdrawal occurring.

(00:31:31) [[John Bolton]], the National Security Advisor, traveled to [[Israel]] to meet with [[Benjamin Netanyahu|Bibi Netanyahu]], after which [[Netanyahu]] called on the [[United States]] to recognize the [[Golan Heights]] as legitimate Israeli territory.

(00:32:29) [[Benjamin Netanyahu|Bibi Netanyahu]] requested that the [[United States]] recognize the [[Golan Heights]], a territory of [[Syria]] seized by [[Israel]] in 1967 and annexed in 1981, as legitimate Israeli territory.

(00:35:06) The planned U.S. withdrawal from [[Syria]] was effectively vetoed by [[John Bolton]], [[Mike Pompeo]], the [[United States Department of Defense|Pentagon]], and [[Israel]], according to [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]].

(00:36:45) [[American Israel Public Affairs Committee|AIPAC]], [[Sheldon Adelson]], and other pro-Israel interests were cited by [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] as forces that prevented the [[Donald Trump|Trump]] administration from executing a withdrawal from [[Syria]].

(00:40:43) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that the [[United States]] maintained a military presence in [[Syria]] primarily to destabilize the country in service of [[Israel]]'s national interest, specifically to prevent [[Iran]] from establishing a foothold there.

(00:42:41) [[American Israel Public Affairs Committee|AIPAC]] gives money to U.S. elected officials, which [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued was a key mechanism for [[Israel]] to influence American foreign policy, as the cost of funding campaigns is minor compared to the financial and military support the U.S. provides to Israel.

(00:45:52) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that major conservative media outlets like [[CRTV]], [[The Daily Wire]], and [[Breitbart News|Breitbart]] are run by Zionists, specifically naming [[Mark Levin]], [[Ben Shapiro]], and the late [[Andrew Breitbart]].

(00:46:43) The "Trump revolution" was described by [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] as a three-pronged fight for national sovereignty over trade policy (against [[China]] and the [[European Union]]), immigration policy (against [[Mexico]] and Central America), and foreign policy (against [[Israel]] and [[Saudi Arabia]]).

(00:50:45) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] expressed anxiety that his criticism of [[Israel]] could lead to people being sent to his house to threaten him or his family, stating, "They will send people to threaten me and say, stop talking about Israel or we will kill your family."
