## Summary

Project Veritas released a report alleging bias at Google, which YouTube subsequently removed. Jared Kushner presented a $50 billion Middle East peace plan in Bahrain that proposed a transportation corridor connecting the Gaza Strip and the West Bank. Nicholas J. Fuentes cited rumors that Jared Kushner controlled access to Donald Trump and argued that Google's algorithmic control constitutes a significant threat.

(00:00:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] began his broadcast by announcing a discussion of a new report by [[Project Veritas]] and a peace plan proposed by [[Jared Kushner]].

(00:00:23) [[Project Veritas]], an organization run by [[James O'Keefe]], released a report alleging bias at [[Google]], which was subsequently removed from [[YouTube]].

(00:00:53) [[Jared Kushner]] presented a $50 billion Middle East peace plan, titled "Peace to Prosperity," at an international conference in [[Bahrain]].

(00:01:53) The "Peace to Prosperity" plan included a global investment fund and a $5 billion transportation corridor to connect the [[Gaza Strip]] and the [[West Bank]].

(00:02:32) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced plans to provide live commentary for the first Democratic primary debates on [[DLive]] and post-debate analysis on [[YouTube]].

(00:05:44) The first Democratic primary debate featured [[Cory Booker]], [[Beto O'Rourke]], and [[Elizabeth Warren]], while the second featured [[Bernie Sanders]], [[Joe Biden]], [[Pete Buttigieg]], and [[Kamala Harris]].

(00:08:35) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] acknowledged the death of YouTuber [[Etika]], a video game content creator.

(00:10:49) The White House's "Peace to Prosperity" plan proposed spending over half of its $50 billion in the Palestinian territories, with the remainder allocated to [[Egypt]], [[Lebanon]], and [[Jordan]].

(00:13:48) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] questioned a potential conflict of interest for [[Jared Kushner]], a Jewish Zionist, in leading Middle East peace efforts while being a close personal friend of [[Benjamin Netanyahu]].

(00:16:29) [[Jared Kushner]] was credited with designing the Trump administration's criminal justice reform package alongside [[Ivanka Trump]].

(00:16:45) [[Jared Kushner]] brokered a deal to end a government shutdown that included provisions on immigration enforcement.

(00:17:13) [[Jared Kushner]] was the architect of the Trump administration's immigration reform proposal to shift to a merit-based system.

(00:18:51) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] cited rumors from White House sources that [[Jared Kushner]] approved all hires and controlled access to [[Donald Trump]]'s desk.

(00:21:16) The creation of the State of [[Israel]] in 1948 was described as the defining geopolitical event for the [[Middle East]] and a primary source of anti-American sentiment in the region.

(00:24:07) The [[United States]] sought regime change against [[Bashar al-Assad]] in [[Syria]] due to his government's material support for [[Hezbollah]].
