## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes returned to broadcasting "America First" as an independent program after a hiatus. Fuentes alleged the Reagan Battalion Twitter account, which he linked to Benny Policek and a Washington, D.C. establishment group, targeted him and received private videos from Cassie Dillon. North Korea launched a ballistic missile over Japan, its first such launch in nearly two decades, which Fuentes described as a rational deterrent strategy. At the MTV Video Music Awards, Robert E. Lee IV condemned his ancestor and announced the Heather Heyer Foundation.

(00:12:46) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] returned to broadcasting his show "America First" after a hiatus following the Charlottesville rally, announcing the program was now independent and free from previous network constraints.

(00:17:78) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that his show had aired on [[Right Side Broadcasting Network]] for five days a week for three months starting in February before taking a break and returning with a three-times-weekly schedule.
