## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes hosts his program from Chicago and traveled to Arizona to visit associates. The United States entered its 27th day of a partial government shutdown, which became the longest in history, over a $5.7 billion funding impasse for a U.S.-Mexico border wall between Donald Trump and congressional Democrats. An ISIS suicide attack in Manbij, Syria, killed several Americans shortly after Trump announced a U.S. troop withdrawal, a decision he linked to a conversation with Turkish President Recep Tayyip Erdoğan. Trump canceled a congressional delegation trip for Nancy Pelosi after she suggested he postpone his State of the Union address.
