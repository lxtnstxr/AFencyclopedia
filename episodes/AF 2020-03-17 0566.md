## Summary

The White House and United States Congress prepared a fiscal stimulus package featuring direct cash payments, with proposals ranging from $1,000 to $4,500 per American. Joe Biden won the Democratic primaries in Florida and Illinois, while Bernie Sanders remained in the race to accumulate delegate leverage. Thirty-eight U.S. states ordered statewide K-12 school closures, and numerous states, including New York and California, closed restaurants and bars or considered shelter-in-place orders. Treasury Secretary Steve Mnuchin and President Donald Trump advocated for direct payments over a payroll tax cut for faster economic relief during the coronavirus pandemic.
