## Summary

Christine Blasey Ford testified before the Senate Judiciary Committee, alleging Brett Kavanaugh sexually assaulted her at a house party in 1982. Republican committee members hired prosecutor Rachel Mitchell to question Ford, while Democratic senators like Kamala Harris and Cory Booker praised Ford's courage. Brett Kavanaugh delivered an emotive opening statement to the committee, denying the allegations and framing them as a partisan political attack. The confirmation of Brett Kavanaugh hinged on the decisions of Republican Senators Lisa Murkowski, Susan Collins, Jeff Flake, and Bob Corker.
