## Summary

Susan Collins and Joe Manchin declared their support for Brett Kavanaugh's Supreme Court confirmation, securing a 51-vote majority. Nicholas J. Fuentes argued Brett Kavanaugh had been radicalized by the confirmation process and would rule conservatively on economic nationalism and immigration. Fuentes stated he avoided coverage of the Donald Trump-Russia narrative, considering it a purely partisan story. In a discussion on drug policy, Fuentes rejected Milton Friedman's legalization arguments, citing the opioid epidemic and the Philippines' severe penalties as a better model.
