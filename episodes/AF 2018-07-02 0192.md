(00:10:40) [[Kate Steinle]] was fatally shot on July 1, 2015, while walking on a pier in [[San Francisco]] with her father.

(00:10:50) [[Jose Zarate]], an undocumented immigrant from [[Mexico]] who had been deported five times, fired the gun that killed [[Kate Steinle]].

(00:11:50) [[Jose Zarate]] was initially acquitted of murder charges in a [[San Francisco]] court in 2017 but was later found guilty in federal court.

(00:13:50) [[Donald Trump]] was elected president in 2016 on a mandate to secure the [[United States]] border and reform the immigration system.

(00:15:00) [[Paul Ryan]], then Speaker of the House, supported a 2018 compromise immigration bill that proposed funding for a border wall in future administrations and offered a path to citizenship for certain DACA recipients.

(00:16:50) [[Bill de Blasio]], the Mayor of [[New York City]], and [[Cynthia Nixon]], a candidate for Governor of [[New York]], publicly supported abolishing U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) in 2018.

(00:17:10) [[Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez]], who primaryed Representative [[Joe Crowley]] in New York's 14th district in 2018, also advocated for abolishing ICE and supported open border policies.

(00:19:40) A 2018 Harvard-Harris poll found that 73% of swing voters and nearly 70% of all Americans opposed abolishing ICE.

(00:25:20) A Reuters poll indicated that support for the [[Republican Party]] among young white voters increased from 33% in 2016 to 39% in 2018, while support for the [[Democratic Party]] among the same group decreased from 47% to 39%.

(00:27:10) [[Andrés Manuel López Obrador]] was elected president of [[Mexico]] in 2018 with over 50% of the vote, representing a major victory for his left-wing party.

(00:28:50) Following his election, [[Andrés Manuel López Obrador]] called [[Donald Trump]] and proposed that [[Mexico]] could reduce northward migration in exchange for economic investment from the [[United States]].

(00:36:30) A reporter for *The Republican* newspaper in [[Massachusetts]] was fired in 2018 after falsely tweeting that the shooter in the Annapolis newspaper attack dropped a "MAGA" hat at the scene.

(00:37:00) [[Brian Ross]], a reporter for [[ABC News]], was fired in 2018, seven months after he incorrectly reported that [[Michael Flynn]] would testify that [[Donald Trump]] directed him to contact Russians during the presidential transition.
