## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes hosted Paul Nehlen, who joined from Wisconsin. Paul Nehlen cited his role in defeating the Trans-Pacific Partnership and supporting Donald Trump's election as key accomplishments, while identifying former Speaker Paul Ryan as a proponent of the border adjustment tax he opposed. Nehlen introduced a "Shall Not Censor" legislative proposal to extend First Amendment protections to social media platforms and criticized figures like Ben Shapiro and Steven Crowder for opposing such regulation. He also called for a presidential pardon for Clint Lorance, a lieutenant convicted of murder in Afghanistan, and contrasted this with Donald Trump's commutation for Sholom Rubashkin.
