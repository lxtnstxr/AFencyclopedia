## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes announced he would travel to Washington, D.C., for a rally on January 6th during Congress's Electoral College vote count. The COVID-19 stimulus bill passed that day included $600 direct payments and foreign aid, including $500 million for Israel and $700 million for Sudan. Madison Cawthorn and Mo Brooks committed to objecting to the Electoral College count, while finding a senator to join the objection remained a challenge. The Turning Point USA SAS Conference in West Palm Beach, Florida, was reported to have featured a cash cannon and widespread reports of attendee misconduct.
