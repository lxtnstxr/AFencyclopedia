## Summary

North Korea tested a new tactical guided weapon overseen by Kim Jong-un. The Robert Mueller investigation found no criminal conspiracy between Donald Trump's campaign and Russia. Nicholas J. Fuentes argued that the media's credibility was damaged by its coverage of the Mueller investigation.

---

(00:26:00) [[North Korea]] tested a new tactical guided weapon, overseen by [[Kim Jong-un]], which analysts believed could be launched from land, sea, or air.

(00:56:00) [[North Korea]] demanded the removal of [[Mike Pompeo]] from nuclear talks, accusing him of being reckless after he described [[Kim Jong-un]] as a tyrant during a U.S. Senate subcommittee hearing.

(01:58:00) [[John Bolton]] and others within the [[United States]] administration were building a case for war with [[Iran]], a factor [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued influenced [[North Korea]]'s renewed provocations.

(02:15:00) [[Barack Obama]] sought warrants from the [[FISA]] courts in June 2016 to place wiretaps on candidate [[Donald Trump]], initiating an investigation that preceded the [[Robert Mueller]] probe.

(02:23:00) The [[Robert Mueller]] investigation, a 22-month probe into Russian interference in the 2016 election, resulted in a 448-page redacted report released on April 18, 2019.

(02:30:00) The [[Robert Mueller]] report found no criminal conspiracy between [[Donald Trump]]'s campaign and [[Russia]], but did not reach a conclusion on whether [[Donald Trump]] obstructed justice.

(02:42:00) [[Robert Mueller]]'s team considered charging [[Donald Trump Jr.]] and [[Jared Kushner]] but determined they could not meet the [[Department of Justice]]'s burden of proof for willfully breaking the law.

(03:11:00) [[James Comey]]'s refusal to carry out orders was cited in the [[Robert Mueller]] report as a reason potential obstruction of justice by [[Donald Trump]] failed.

(03:21:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that the media's credibility was damaged by its coverage of the [[Robert Mueller]] investigation, which he viewed as a delegitimizing event comparable to false claims about WMDs in [[Iraq]].
