## Summary

The Supreme Court of the United States denied emergency requests for a religious exemption from New York's COVID-19 vaccine mandate for healthcare workers, with Justices Clarence Thomas, Samuel Alito, and Neil Gorsuch dissenting. Donald Trump appointed Amy Coney Barrett to the Supreme Court in 2020, and she was rapidly confirmed by a United States Senate led by Mitch McConnell. Nicholas J. Fuentes argued that institutions like the Federalist Society, the Republican Party, and figures such as Mitch McConnell and Kevin McCarthy are not allies of his political movement. New York Governor Kathy Hochul reinstated a statewide indoor mask mandate, a policy also adopted by states including Washington, Oregon, and Illinois.
