## Summary

The United States House of Representatives passed the Fairness for All Act with the support of 21 Republican representatives, including Chris Stewart, Elise Stefanik, and John Curtis. Nicholas J. Fuentes characterized this support as part of a "Trumpism without Trump" wing of the Republican Party and connected it to policy concessions following Glenn Youngkin's election in Virginia. Fuentes argued that white people, Christians, and conservatives face the most discrimination in the United States, citing COVID-19 relief program racial prioritization and corporate diversity quotas. He also claimed a crime wave involving organized retail theft in San Francisco and Chicago was primarily conducted by black people due to a lack of prosecution.
