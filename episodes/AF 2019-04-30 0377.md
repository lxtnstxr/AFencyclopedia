## Summary

Juan Guaidó called on Venezuela's military to join a revolution against Nicolás Maduro's government. The United States dispatched two aircraft carriers toward the Caribbean, and National Security Advisor John Bolton stated all options, including military intervention, were on the table. The Florida State Senate unanimously passed a bill defining various forms of anti-Semitism, including applying a double standard to Israel or denying its right to exist.

---

(00:29:57) [[Juan Guaidó]], the opposition leader of the [[Venezuela|Venezuelan]] National Assembly, called on the military to join him in a final phase of revolution against the government of [[Nicolás Maduro]].

(00:44:49) [[Nicolás Maduro]] blamed [[Cuba]] for deploying paramilitary and defense personnel to shore up his government during the unrest in [[Venezuela]].

(00:53:53) [[John Bolton]], the National Security Advisor of the [[United States]], stated that foreign intervention by countries like [[Russia]] and [[Cuba]] prevented members of [[Nicolás Maduro]]'s inner circle from supporting the opposition and bolstered the administration militarily.

(00:58:11) The [[United States]] dispatched two aircraft carriers towards the Caribbean in response to the situation in [[Venezuela]].

(00:58:21) [[John Bolton]] stated that all options, including military intervention, were on the table regarding [[Venezuela]].

(01:11:29) [[Donald Trump]] withdrew the [[United States]] from the Iranian nuclear deal and later imposed sanctions that included waivers for [[Iran|Iranian]] oil sales, which were set to expire on May 2nd.

(01:34:13) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that intervention in [[Venezuela]] could be justified under the [[Monroe Doctrine]] and as a matter of national interest, particularly to counter the influence of [[Russia]] and [[China]] in the Western Hemisphere.

(01:45:51) [[Bernie Sanders]] argued that [[Venezuela]] had democratic components, a position [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] contrasted with his own support for potential U.S. intervention.

(02:04:53) The [[Florida]] State Senate unanimously passed a bill to address anti-Semitism in public schools following a shooting at a synagogue in [[California]].

(02:12:59) The [[Florida]] anti-Semitism bill included a definition that considered it anti-Semitic to accuse Jewish citizens of being more loyal to [[Israel]] or worldwide Jewish priorities than to their own nations' interests.

(02:17:47) The [[Florida]] anti-Semitism bill defined accusing Jews or [[Israel]] of inventing or exaggerating the Holocaust as an example of anti-Semitic behavior.

(02:21:37) The [[Florida]] anti-Semitism bill included in its definition applying a double standard to [[Israel]] by requiring behavior not expected of other democratic nations or focusing human rights investigations only on [[Israel]].

(02:27:43) The [[Florida]] anti-Semitism bill defined delegitimizing [[Israel]] by denying the Jewish people's right to self-determination and [[Israel]]'s right to exist as anti-Semitic, while stipulating that criticism similar to that leveled at other countries was permissible.

(02:32:83) The [[Florida]] anti-Semitism bill defined making mendacious, dehumanizing, or stereotypical allegations about Jews as a collective, including claims of a world Jewish conspiracy or Jews controlling media, economy, or government, as classic anti-Semitism.

(03:07:27) [[Ronald S. Lauder]], head of the [[World Jewish Congress]], and [[David Rothschild]], chief executive of the Rothschild Bank in [[France]], were cited by [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] as Jewish leaders supporting legislation like the [[Florida]] anti-Semitism bill.

(03:16:49) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] cited the [[Jewish Federation of San Francisco]], the [[Canary Mission]], the [[Jewish Federation of Winnipeg]], the [[World Jewish Congress]], and the [[State of Israel]] as institutions working to combat anti-Semitism.
