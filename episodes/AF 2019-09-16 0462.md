## Summary

A missile strike removed 5.7 million barrels of daily oil production from a Saudi Arabian facility, causing the largest global oil price spike since 1991. The United States State Department and Mike Pompeo blamed Iran for the attack, which Iran rejected, while Donald Trump stated the U.S. was ready to respond. U.S. Special Representative Brian Hook told congressional staffers the attack was definitely not carried out by Houthi rebels and had not changed Trump's strategy of re-engagement with Iran. Benjamin Netanyahu faced a critical re-election in Israel, having recently called for the annexation of the entire Jordan Valley.
