## Summary

Silicon Valley Bank failed in March 2023 after a bank run triggered by losses on its mortgage-backed securities following Federal Reserve interest rate hikes. The Biden administration and the FDIC intervened to insure all deposits at Silicon Valley Bank, Signature Bank, and First Republic Bank beyond the standard limit. Nicholas J. Fuentes argued the bank's collapse signaled broader economic weaknesses from two decades of easy money, creating a national dilemma between inflation and deflation. Fuentes also contrasted the U.S. financialized economy with the resource-based economies of Russia and China, which he stated were aligning into a strategic bloc.
