## Summary

Ilhan Omar sparked a political controversy with tweets criticizing financial influence regarding support for Israel, leading to a condemnation letter from Democratic House leadership including Nancy Pelosi and Steny Hoyer. Omar later apologized for her tweets while reaffirming criticism of lobbies like AIPAC, which leads a network of pro-Israel PACs and whose subsidiary, the American Israel Education Foundation, funded trips for lawmakers to Israel. The U.S. Senate passed the AIPAC-sponsored Combating BDS Act with a supermajority, and figures like Ted Cruz and Marco Rubio publicly condemned Omar's statements as anti-Semitic. Nicholas J. Fuentes cited the book *The Israel Lobby and U.S. Foreign Policy* and listed several alleged actions by Israel against U.S. interests, including the 1967 attack on the USS Liberty.
