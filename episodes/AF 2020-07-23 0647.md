## Summary

The United States Congress passed a defense authorization bill requiring the Pentagon to rename bases named after Confederate generals. President Donald Trump threatened to veto the bill over that provision. The Republican National Committee canceled its large-scale nominating convention in Jacksonville, Florida, due to the coronavirus pandemic. Justice Department Inspector General Michael Horowitz and the Department of Homeland Security's Inspector General initiated investigations into federal police conduct in Portland, Oregon.

(00:07:35) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced the featured story for the evening would be an update on a defense authorization bill concerning the renaming of military bases named after Confederate generals.

(00:18:11) The [[United States Congress]] passed a defense authorization bill in both the [[United States House of Representatives|House]] and the [[United States Senate|Senate]] with an amendment requiring the [[United States Department of Defense|Pentagon]] to rename all military bases named after Confederate generals and remove related monuments.

(00:29:33) The [[United States Senate|Senate]] passed the National Defense Authorization Act with a veto-proof majority of 86 to 14, which included the provision to rename Confederate-named military bases.

(00:37:59) [[Donald Trump|President Trump]] threatened to veto the National Defense Authorization Act over its inclusion of the plan to rename bases named after Confederate figures.
