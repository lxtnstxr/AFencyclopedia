## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes was banned from multiple online platforms including Twitter and had his Bank of America account frozen by the United States Department of Justice as part of an FBI investigation into his presence at the January 6 Capitol rally. Fuentes was placed on the federal no-fly list, preventing travel to a Florida press conference with Michelle Malkin and Laura Loomer, and premiered a documentary series detailing these government actions. He argued the Biden administration's Department of Homeland Security categorizes Trump supporters and anti-vaccine activists as domestic violent extremists. Fuentes also cited the exile of Edward Snowden in Russia and stated 19 Republican senators voted for the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act.
