## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes announced a premium podcast analyzing the Syrian Civil War and historical figures like David Ben-Gurion and neoconservative Richard Perle. Donald Trump met with Japan's Shinzo Abe at Mar-a-Lago and made a revised trade deal with South Korea contingent on progress with North Korea. Trump and Kim Jong-un planned a summit for May, while South Korea and North Korea prepared a statement to end the Korean War. The Supreme Court's Neil Gorsuch sided with liberal justices to strike down a deportation statute for ambiguous language.

---

(00:05:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced the publication of a premium podcast episode analyzing the [[Syria|Syrian]] Civil War from an [[Israel|Israeli]] perspective, referencing the 1996 "Clean Break Memo" written by [[Richard Perle]] for [[Benjamin Netanyahu|Bibi]].

(00:05:50) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that the premium podcast would examine historical figures including [[David Ben-Gurion]] and Oded Yinon, as well as neoconservative architects of the [[Iraq]] War like [[Richard Perle]] and [[Douglas Feith]].

(00:06:10) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] reported that [[Donald Trump|Trump]] met with [[Shinzo Abe]] of [[Japan]] at the Mar-a-Lago resort to discuss [[North Korea]] and trade.

(00:07:40) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that the foreign policy focus of the [[Donald Trump|Trump]] administration had shifted from the Middle East to the Pacific, specifically [[North Korea]] and [[China]], contrasting it with the Middle East-centric approaches of [[George W. Bush|Bush]] and [[Barack Obama|Obama]].

(00:11:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] claimed that the strategic importance of Middle Eastern energy for the [[United States]] was declining due to increased domestic production, reducing the prominence of countries like [[Saudi Arabia]] and [[Iran]].

(00:14:10) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that [[Donald Trump|Trump]]'s tariffs on steel and aluminum, his renegotiation of a trade deal with [[South Korea]], and the [[Syria]] strikes were all tactics directed at influencing [[North Korea]] and, by extension, [[China]].

(00:15:40) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that [[Donald Trump|Trump]] made the revised trade deal with [[South Korea]] contingent on progress toward peace with [[North Korea]].

(00:18:50) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] reported that [[Donald Trump|Trump]] and [[Shinzo Abe]] held a meeting where [[Donald Trump|Trump]] confirmed high-level discussions were occurring directly between the [[United States]] and [[North Korea]].

(00:19:50) [[Donald Trump|Trump]] took credit for the success of the [[South Korea|South Korean]] Olympics and gave his blessing for [[North Korea]] and [[South Korea]] to end the Korean War, according to [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]].

(00:20:50) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] reported that an unnamed [[South Korea|South Korean]] official stated both Koreas were preparing an official statement to bring an end to the Korean War, which has continued under an armistice since 1953.

(00:21:50) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that a summit between [[North Korea]] and [[South Korea]] was scheduled for April 28th, and a summit between [[Donald Trump|Trump]] and [[Kim Jong-un]] was planned for May, with five potential locations under consideration.

(00:24:50) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that past agreements with rogue regimes like [[Iran]], [[Syria]], and [[Libya]] had failed because they involved incremental, synchronized reforms that the regimes later reneged on.

(00:25:50) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that the problem with the [[Iran]] nuclear deal negotiated by the [[Barack Obama|Obama]] administration was that it lifted sanctions and provided [[Iran]] with access to $150 billion in frozen assets without enforceable guarantees of compliance.

(00:30:30) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] predicted that [[Donald Trump|Trump]] would pursue a renegotiation of the [[Iran]] nuclear deal using a similar strategy of pressure and diplomacy as he was employing with [[North Korea]].

(00:32:50) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] reported that, according to Vox, [[John Bolton]] had proposed extensive air raids to destroy the [[Syria|Syrian]] government's war-making capability in response to a chemical weapons attack, but [[Donald Trump|Trump]] rejected this in favor of a limited strike on three empty facilities.

(00:34:30) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] reported that, according to the *Wall Street Journal*, [[Donald Trump|Trump]] wanted an Arab coalition from [[Egypt]], the [[United Arab Emirates]], [[Qatar]], and [[Saudi Arabia]] to replace the [[United States]] military presence in [[Syria]].

(00:38:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that [[Donald Trump|Trump]] still desired to meet with [[Vladimir Putin|Putin]] and was reportedly furious when the [[United States]] expelled Russian diplomats in response to the assassination of a former Russian spy in the [[United Kingdom]].

(00:43:20) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] reported that Supreme Court Justice [[Neil Gorsuch]] sided with liberal justices in striking down a federal statute mandating deportation for immigrants who commit a "crime of violence," ruling the language was too ambiguous.

(00:58:30) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that [[Israel]] was a "preeminent rogue state" in the Middle East with significant influence in the [[United States]] government, but that [[Iran]] also pursued its interests aggressively in [[Iraq]], [[Syria]], [[Yemen]], and [[Bahrain]].

(01:00:50) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] criticized figures like [[Richard Spencer]] and [[Alex Jones]] for their pro-Russia views, noting [[Richard Spencer]]'s friendship with Kremlin-linked philosopher [[Alexander Dugin]] and favorable coverage of [[Alex Jones]] on state-funded outlet [[Russia Today]].
