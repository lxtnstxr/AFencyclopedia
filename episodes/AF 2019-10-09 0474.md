## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes was banned from payment processors PayPal and Authorize.Net for his website subscriptions. A University of California, Berkeley study reported by Business Insider found billionaires paid a lower effective tax rate than the average American in 2018. Donald Trump announced a withdrawal of U.S. troops from the Syria-Turkey border, after which Turkey launched a military offensive into northeastern Syria. Trump stated the U.S. had spent $8 trillion in the Middle East and criticized the military-industrial complex for profiting from endless wars.

(00:10:40) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] recorded a podcast with [[Dave Smith]] earlier in the day, which was scheduled to be uploaded the following day.

(00:11:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] was banned from the payment processor [[PayPal]], which he had been using for premium membership subscriptions on his website.

(00:11:40) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] was subsequently banned from a second payment processor, [[Authorize.Net]], after only a week or two of use, which he attributed to being flagged as a high-risk merchant.

(00:18:20) A study by [[University of California, Berkeley]] economists [[Emmanuel Saez]] and [[Gabriel Zuckman]], reported by [[Business Insider]], found that in 2018, billionaires paid a 23% effective tax rate on their income, lower than the 28% rate paid by the average American.

(00:19:20) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that the U.S. tax system is designed to reward wealthy individuals through mechanisms like low capital gains taxes while punishing working people with higher payroll and sales taxes relative to their income.

(00:19:40) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] cited [[Andrew Yang]]'s talking point that [[Amazon]] paid zero dollars in federal taxes for 2018.

(00:25:40) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] characterized the U.S. socioeconomic conflict as a class war where the middle class pays the majority of taxes and receives deteriorating public services and consumer products, while both the wealthy and the poor pay little to nothing.

(00:28:40) [[Donald Trump]] announced the withdrawal of U.S. troops from the border between [[Syria]] and [[Turkey]], allowing [[Turkey]] to establish a safe zone in the region, a decision that created friction with Kurdish-led forces.

(00:30:40) [[Turkey]], under President [[Recep Tayyip Erdoğan]], launched a ground and air offensive into northeastern [[Syria]] to create a safe zone cleared of Kurdish militias, which it considers terrorists linked to the [[Kurdistan Workers' Party]].
