## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes launched a campaign to reinstate John Schnatter as the leader of Papa John's and scheduled interviews with Mr. Mediker, Ali Akbar, and Mike Ma. Donald Trump announced a $12 billion USDA subsidy for farmers and proposed levying an additional $200 billion in tariffs on China. WWE announced its first all-women's pay-per-view event, Evolution, presented by Stephanie McMahon.

---

(00:05:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] launched a campaign to reinstate [[John Schnatter]] as the leader of [[Papa John's]] using the hashtag #BringBackPapa and distributing flyers online and in person.

(00:35:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] scheduled interviews with [[Mr. Mediker]] for Wednesday, [[Ali Akbar]] for Thursday, and [[Mike Ma]] for Friday to discuss [[Mike Ma]]'s recent suspension from Twitter and the book *The Bronze Age Mindset*.

(00:55:00) [[World Wrestling Entertainment|WWE]] announced its first all-women's pay-per-view event, Evolution, scheduled for October 28th, which was presented as an empowerment initiative by [[Stephanie McMahon]].

(01:05:00) [[Donald Trump]] announced a $12 billion subsidy program through the [[United States Department of Agriculture]] to support American soybean, dairy, and pork producers affected by tariffs in the ongoing trade war with [[China]].

(01:15:00) [[Justin Roiland]], co-creator of *Rick and Morty* with [[Dan Harmon]], faced criticism for a drawing depicting a nude 11-year-old [[Barron Trump]] and for tweets lamenting online harassment culture.

(01:25:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] cited the case of an engineer who attended the Charlottesville rally and later died by suicide after media harassment, and the case of Millennial Matt who was doxed on [[CNN]] and fled to [[Japan]], as examples of severe consequences for political speech.

(01:45:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that figures like [[Ben Shapiro]] and institutions like [[National Review]] represent a class of people who claim to be conservative but support mass immigration and international capitalism, which he views as antithetical to traditional conservatism.

(02:05:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] endorsed the tactics of [[Mike Cernovich]], stating he "collects scalps" and "understands the nature of the conflict" in going after political opponents online.

(02:25:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that personnel in major corporations, media, and government, who are predominantly university-educated, drive cultural changes like the WWE's all-women's event, motivated by ideology rather than profit or public demand.

(02:55:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] predicted that the WWE Evolution pay-per-view would be the lowest-grossing WWE event in about a decade, citing it as an example of products created despite a lack of public demand.

(03:15:00) [[Donald Trump]] proposed levying an additional $200 billion in tariffs on [[China]], with total proposed tariffs reaching $500 billion when combined with potential duties on foreign-made cars.

(03:45:00) [[China]] recorded a deficit in its current account for the first quarter of 2018, its first since joining the [[World Trade Organization]] in 2001, indicating it was importing more services than it was exporting.

(04:05:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] identified think tanks including the [[Cato Institute]], [[American Enterprise Institute]], and [[Heritage Foundation]] as proponents of free trade policies he opposes.

(04:25:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] was scheduled to debate anarcho-capitalist [[Adam Kokish]] on the Chadcast podcast hosted by [[Alfonso Drill]].

(04:45:00) The city of [[Chicago]] created a municipal identification card usable for public services and welfare, which [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated did not require proof of citizenship.

(05:05:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] recommended *The American Conservative* magazine as a source for what he described as a renaissance of classical, traditionalist conservatism in the tradition of [[Russell Kirk]].

(05:25:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that [[Ben Shapiro]]'s business partner, [[Benny Polacek]], had searched for terms like "how to buy a baby" and "molest porn" online, and had designed logos for [[CRTV]] and [[The Daily Wire]].

(05:45:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] declined a physical fight challenge from [[Patrick Little]], whom he described as a "mentally ill person" who needed help and was being enabled by malicious influences.

(06:05:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated he preferred to pronounce "Iran" as "Eye-ran" instead of the correct pronunciation, arguing that Americans are not obligated to adopt foreign pronunciations.

(06:25:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] speculated that sealed indictments and a slow investigation into the [[Obama administration]] and intelligence community could be due to the unprecedented difficulty of prosecuting the entire [[FBI]], rather than a lack of will.

(06:45:00) [[Tencent]], a [[China|Chinese]] company, owns a 40% stake in [[Epic Games]], the creator of the video game *Fortnite*.

(07:05:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] expressed skepticism about applying the death penalty for rape due to the prevalence of false accusations, but supported it for pedophiles, and questioned the modern concept of "marital rape" within the traditional contractual understanding of marriage.

(07:25:00) Journalist [[Jared Holt]] wrote an article stating [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] declined to affirm whether he believed the Holocaust happened during a livestream with [[Patrick Little]]; Fuentes called this a smear and stated he "would never deny it."

(07:45:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]]'s favorite WWE superstars from his childhood included [[John Cena]], [[The Undertaker]], [[Randy Orton]], and [[Chris Jericho]], and he recalled attending a live event in [[Chicago]] where [[Triple H]] gave an elbow pad to a nearby child.
