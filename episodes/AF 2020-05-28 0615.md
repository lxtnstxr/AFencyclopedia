## Summary

Donald Trump signed an executive order on May 28, 2020, targeting Section 230 liability protections for social media companies and directing the Federal Trade Commission to develop a report on online censorship. The death of George Floyd after being detained by a Minneapolis police officer on May 25, 2020, sparked riots in that city which resulted in widespread arson, looting, and property damage at businesses including Target, an Apple Store, and a five-story housing complex. Senator Josh Hawley announced he would draft legislation addressing Section 230 following Trump's order, while Minneapolis Mayor Jacob Frey stated the protests stemmed from historical anger in the black community. Nicholas J. Fuentes argued the riots represented opportunism and a pattern of behavior, advocated for martial law in high-crime areas, and called for a halt to immigration to prevent further destabilization.
