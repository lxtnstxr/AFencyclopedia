## Summary

Israel ordered 38,000 African migrants from Sudan and Eritrea to leave or face jail, classifying them as economic infiltrators. The 2017-2018 protests in Iran drew calls for U.S. support from figures like John McCain and Hillary Clinton, while media reported anti-government chants. Nikki Haley anticipated a North Korean ballistic missile test, prompting Donald Trump's aggressive rhetorical and strategic response.

---

(00:00:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced plans to discuss [[Iran]], [[North Korea]], and a story from the [[BBC]] about African migrants in [[Israel]].

(00:04:03) The [[BBC]] reported that [[Israel]] ordered 38,000 illegal African migrants, predominantly from [[Sudan]] and [[Eritrea]], to leave the country by April or face jail.

(00:05:43) The [[Israeli government]] stated that elderly migrants, children, and victims of human trafficking could stay, but classified the majority as economic migrants seeking to "parasite off of the Israeli economy."

(00:08:06) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] contrasted [[Israel]]'s policy of labeling migrants as "infiltrators" and deporting them with the immigration approaches of [[Germany]], [[France]], the [[United Kingdom]], and the [[United States]].

(00:11:32) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that [[Israel]] is identified by the [[United States]] foreign policy establishment and media as a Western country and a "shining beacon" of Western values in the [[Middle East]].

(00:12:57) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that [[Israel]] maintains a constitutional identity as a Jewish state and homeland for the Jewish people, which he characterized as a "noble" mentality rooted in a belief in being "God's chosen people."

(00:17:05) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] reported that protests in [[Iran]] had escalated, with protesters and members of the [[Iranian Revolutionary Guard Corps]] being killed and police stations set on fire.

(00:18:23) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] noted that the 2009 Green Movement protests in [[Tehran]] involved over one million people, while the current 2017-2018 protests involved less than a thousand people in any given city.

(00:18:44) Figures including [[Bill Kristol]], [[John McCain]], [[Mitt Romney]], and [[Hillary Clinton]] called for the [[United States]] to support the Iranian protesters, with [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] suggesting they hinted at regime change.

(00:20:48) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that the [[Iran|Iranian]] government in 2017-2018 was more unified and purged of reformists compared to the divided government during the 2009 protests, making it more stable.

(00:22:55) Media outlets including [[BBC]], the [[Washington Post]], and [[Fox News]] reported that Iranian protesters were chanting "No Gaza, no Lebanon, no Syria, I give my life for Iran," a slogan [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued parroted [[United States]] and [[Israel|Israeli]] foreign policy concerns.

(00:25:02) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] suggested the [[Central Intelligence Agency|CIA]], [[Saudi Arabia|KSA]], and [[Israel]] likely had involvement in the Iranian protests, but argued a full-scale war with [[Iran]] was not imminent.

(00:26:11) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that [[Donald Trump]] was ideologically, politically, and personally disinclined to pursue a major ground war in [[Iran]], which would require 200,000-300,000 troops and cost over a trillion dollars.

(00:33:50) [[Nikki Haley]], the [[United States]] ambassador to the [[United Nations]], announced that the [[United States]] expected [[North Korea]] to launch a ballistic missile test sometime during the week.

(00:34:34) [[Donald Trump]] responded to [[Kim Jong-un]]'s claim of having a nuclear button by stating he had a "much more powerful" one.

(00:35:05) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] outlined [[Donald Trump]]'s aggressive strategy against [[North Korea]], which included missile launches, sanctions, [[United Nations Security Council]] pressure, and urging [[China]] to cut off oil supplies.

(00:38:35) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] theorized that the [[United States]]'s heightened rhetoric and actions regarding [[Iran]] might be a form of theater intended to pressure [[North Korea]] and [[China]] by demonstrating the consequences of defying American demands.

(00:43:33) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that the real problem with Senator [[Orrin Hatch]] resigning was the potential succession by [[Mitt Romney]], who he argued would use a Senate platform to oppose [[Donald Trump]] and possibly launch a presidential bid.

(00:48:52) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that [[North Korea]] under [[Kim Jong-un]] represented a legitimate strategic and existential nuclear threat to the [[United States]], distinct from conflicts driven by financial interests like those involving the [[International Monetary Fund|IMF]].
