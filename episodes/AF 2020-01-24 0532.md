## Summary

The coronavirus, originating in China, spread to France, the United States, Japan, and several other nations, with Chinese authorities constructing a new hospital in Wuhan within six days. Goldman Sachs CEO David Solomon announced a policy to not underwrite IPOs for U.S. and European companies without at least one diverse board member, while BlackRock and State Street Global Advisors were voting against directors of companies without female directors. Health officials in China reported 41 deaths in Hubei province and 1,287 confirmed cases nationwide, and French Health Minister Agnes Buzyn confirmed three cases in France, including one in Bordeaux.

(00:04:20) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced the featured story for the episode would be the global spread of the coronavirus originating in [[China]].

(00:09:40) The first cases of the coronavirus were reported in [[France]] and a second case was reported in [[Chicago]], [[United States]].

(00:29:20) Authorities in [[China]] were constructing a new hospital in [[Wuhan]] within six days to accommodate patients infected with the coronavirus.

(00:53:40) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] reported that the coronavirus had spread to [[Japan]], [[Macau]], [[Hong Kong]], [[Thailand]], [[Vietnam]], [[Singapore]], the [[United States]], [[Saudi Arabia]], and [[France]].

(01:12:40) [[Goldman Sachs]] CEO [[David Solomon]], speaking from [[Davos]], announced the firm would no longer underwrite initial public offerings for companies in the [[United States]] and [[Europe]] without at least one "diverse" board member, defined as a woman or a person of color.

(01:17:00) [[Goldman Sachs]] stated its new board diversity mandate would not initially apply to companies in [[Asia]].

(01:25:40) [[BlackRock]] and [[State Street Global Advisors]] were voting against directors of companies without a female director, and [[California]] had enacted a law fining public companies with all-male boards $100,000.

(01:33:40) [[Goldman Sachs]] planned to raise its diversity requirement the following year to include two diverse directors, with diversity defined to include sexual orientation and gender identity.

(01:41:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that race- and gender-based policies in both the public and private sectors of the [[United States]] and [[Europe]] were systematically designed to benefit non-whites and women at the expense of white men.

(02:10:40) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] observed that global initiatives on climate, refugee resettlement, and corporate diversity disproportionately targeted and imposed mandates on white-majority countries like those in [[Europe]] and the [[United States]], while exempting nations like [[China]], [[India]], and [[Saudi Arabia]].

(02:24:40) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] contended that if women were inherently superior business leaders as often portrayed in media, competitive companies would hire them voluntarily without needing mandates and penalties from institutions like [[Goldman Sachs]].

(02:50:40) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] asserted that female competence at high levels of leadership, innovation, and power was a fictional archetype promoted in film and television with no basis in biological reality or historical precedent.

(03:07:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] claimed the modern education system and workforce were being restructured to reward feminine traits like compliance, submission, and agreeableness over masculine virtues like competition and aggression, leading to the "empowerment of women" as a societal disaster.

(03:17:40) Health officials in [[China]] reported the coronavirus had killed 41 people in [[Hubei]] province, with 1,287 confirmed cases nationwide.

(03:24:40) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] cited a report that a new 1,000-bed hospital built with prefabricated materials in [[Wuhan]] could be completed within six days using 35 diggers and 10 bulldozers to address a shortage of medical resources.

(03:38:20) [[Agnes Buzyn]], the French health minister, confirmed three cases of coronavirus in [[France]], including a 48-year-old man of Chinese origin hospitalized in [[Bordeaux]] who had visited [[Wuhan]].

(03:44:20) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] expressed skepticism that the official Chinese figure of approximately 1,300 coronavirus cases warranted the extreme response of quarantining 40 million people, building emergency hospitals, and causing global panic, suggesting the true scale of the outbreak was being underreported.

(04:16:20) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] noted that [[China]]'s command economy and powerful state enabled it to undertake drastic measures like quarantining 40 million people and rapidly building a hospital, capabilities he considered inconceivable in the [[United States]].

(04:28:40) [[Nicholas J.
