## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes reported on the Georgia Secretary of State's announcement of a statewide signature match audit to address election disinformation. Fuentes outlined the January 5th Georgia Senate runoff involving Republicans David Perdue and Kelly Loeffler, noting a Democratic victory would give the party Senate control via Kamala Harris's tie-breaking vote. He cited two healthcare workers in Alaska who were hospitalized after receiving the Pfizer COVID-19 vaccine and argued against voting for Perdue and Loeffler in the runoff.
