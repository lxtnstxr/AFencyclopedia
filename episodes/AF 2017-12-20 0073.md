## Summary

The Tax Cuts and Jobs Act reduced the corporate tax rate from 35% to 21% and cut several individual income tax rates. The legislation repealed the Affordable Care Act's individual mandate penalty and raised the child tax credit to $2,000. Donald Trump threatened to cut off aid to countries voting against U.S. recognition of Jerusalem as Israel's capital. Osama bin Laden cited U.S. support for Israel as a primary grievance in his declaration of war against the United States.

(00:37:54) [[Donald Trump]]'s administration passed the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act, a sweeping overhaul of the tax code representing the first major reform since 1986.

(00:43:80) The Tax Cuts and Jobs Act cut the corporate tax rate from 35% to 21%, moving the [[United States]] from one of the highest rates in the industrialized world to a more competitive level.

(00:54:86) The tax reform maintained seven income tax brackets but cut rates for five of them, reducing the top marginal rate from 39.6% to 37%.

(01:04:28) The legislation repealed the individual mandate penalty from the Affordable Care Act, a key provision of the healthcare law often referred to as Obamacare.

(01:09:11) Chief Justice [[John Roberts|Justice Roberts]] of the [[Supreme Court of the United States|Supreme Court]] had previously ruled the individual mandate was constitutional because it functioned as a tax.

(01:36:84) The tax reform raised the child tax credit to $2,000 for every child under the age of 17 and doubled the exemption threshold for the federal estate tax.

(01:54:58) The legislation capped the state and local tax (SALT) deduction at $10,000, a provision that primarily affected taxpayers in high-tax states and major coastal cities.

(02:14:46) To pass the bill with a simple majority under Senate reconciliation rules, Republican lawmakers had to sunset the individual income tax cuts after ten years to avoid raising the long-term deficit beyond permitted limits.

(02:50:24) Following the passage of the tax bill, several major corporations, including [[AT&T]], [[Fifth Third Bank]], [[Boeing]], [[Wells Fargo]], and [[Comcast]], announced employee bonuses, wage increases, and new capital investment plans within hours.

(03:48:02) [[Donald Trump]] threatened to cut off foreign aid to any country that voted in favor of a [[United Nations]] General Assembly resolution opposing U.S. recognition of [[Jerusalem]] as the capital of [[Israel]].

(04:00:42) [[Donald Trump]] had previously announced U.S. recognition of [[Jerusalem]] as [[Israel]]'s capital but simultaneously signed a waiver delaying the move of the [[United States]] embassy from [[Tel Aviv]], as required by a 1995 law.

(04:26:50) The [[United States]] provided [[Israel]] with approximately $3.8 billion in annual military aid, structured as loans that were subsequently forgiven and which [[Israel]] could use to purchase U.S. Treasury notes and earn interest.

(04:38:26) [[Osama bin Laden]] cited U.S. support for [[Israel]] as a primary grievance in his fatwa declaring war on the [[United States]], a justification also echoed by the perpetrator of the 1993 World Trade Center bombing.
