## Summary

The Social Security Administration sent "no match" letters to 570,000 employers regarding mismatched employee Social Security numbers. Poland refused a $300 billion restitution demand from Jewish organizations for World War II-era property losses. Jewish lobbying groups in the United States pressured the U.S. government to condition a permanent military base in Poland on restitution payments. Donald Trump signed the Justice for Uncompensated Survivors Today Act, requiring State Department reports on restitution compliance by 47 countries.

(00:04:20) The [[Social Security Administration]] sent "no match" letters to 570,000 employers, notifying them that the Social Security numbers of millions of their employees did not match government records, indicating the likely employment of undocumented workers.

(00:05:10) [[Poland]] refused to pay $300 billion in restitution demanded by Jewish organizations for property lost during [[World War II]], arguing it was also a victim of [[Nazi Germany]].

(00:06:00) Jewish lobbying groups in the [[United States]] pressured the [[United States|U.S.]] government to withhold the establishment of a permanent military base in [[Poland]] unless the Polish government paid the demanded restitution.

(00:08:00) A veteran Jewish communal official, quoted by the [[Washington Free Beacon]], stated that if [[Poland]] would not listen to Jewish demands, "they'll need to listen to us as Americans."

(00:12:20) [[Donald Trump]] signed the Justice for Uncompensated Survivors Today Act (Act 447), which requires the [[United States Department of State|State Department]] to report on the compliance of 47 countries, including [[Poland]], regarding the restitution of Jewish assets.

(00:17:40) The [[New York Times]] reported that the [[Trump administration]] had prepared plans for a potential 140,000-troop invasion of [[Iran]].

(00:20:40) [[Donald Trump]] expressed frustration with advisors [[John Bolton]] and [[Mike Pompeo]], believing they were rushing towards a military confrontation with [[Iran]] contrary to his desire for diplomatic talks, according to a [[The Washington Post|Washington Post]] article citing administration officials.

(00:23:40) [[Donald Trump]] hired [[John Bolton]] as National Security Advisor in April 2018, shortly before his summit with [[Kim Jong-un]] in June 2018.

(00:28:10) The [[Trump administration]] initiated a policy of sending Social Security "no match" letters to employers, a program previously suspended under the [[Barack Obama|Obama administration]].

(00:32:40) [[Donald Trump]] tweeted a message stating, "Next week ICE will begin the process of removing the millions of illegal aliens who have illicitly found their way into the United States."

(00:37:00) [[Kirstjen Nielsen]] was removed from her position as Secretary of the [[United States Department of Homeland Security|Department of Homeland Security]].
