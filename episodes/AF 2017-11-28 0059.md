## Summary

North Korea conducted an intercontinental ballistic missile test with a potential range of 13,000 miles, demonstrating a theoretical capability to strike the United States. Nicholas J. Fuentes argued the North Korean nuclear crisis was the dominant issue of the Trump presidency and placed culpability on former presidents Bill Clinton and George W. Bush. Justin Trudeau delivered a formal apology to the LGBTQ2 community in the Canadian House of Commons. Jordan Peterson and Kevin MacDonald engaged in a public dispute on Twitter regarding race, genetics, and identity.

(00:01:40) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced the start of an episode of his show, America First.

(00:10:50) [[North Korea]] conducted a test launch of an intercontinental ballistic missile (ICBM) from a mobile launcher in its western region.

(00:12:30) [[United States]] officials assessed the North Korean missile had a flight time of approximately 50 minutes and a potential range of 13,000 miles.

(00:14:10) The [[North Korea|North Korean]] ICBM test demonstrated a theoretical capability to strike any state within the [[United States]], though officials noted the range would be reduced with a heavier payload such as a nuclear warhead.
