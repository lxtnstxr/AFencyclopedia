## Summary

Donald Trump proposed a DACA deal in January 2018 and later announced a withdrawal from Syria in April 2018 before reconsidering. The United States launched missile strikes on Syria in April 2017 and April 2018 following alleged chemical weapons attacks in Douma. Bashar al-Assad's forces, aided by Russia and Iran, regained control of Syrian territory including East Ghouta in 2018.

---

(00:01:48) In January 2018, [[Donald Trump]] stated his intent to negotiate a deal granting legal status to DACA recipients in exchange for $1.6 billion in border wall funding, a proposal that was later abandoned in the omnibus spending bill.

(00:05:25) On April 7, 2018, an alleged chemical weapons attack occurred in Douma, a suburb of [[Damascus]], [[Syria]], reportedly killing 42 people.

(00:06:45) On April 7, 2017, the [[United States]] launched 59 Tomahawk missiles at an airfield in [[Homs]], [[Syria]], in retaliation for a chemical weapons attack allegedly launched from that location.

(00:07:18) The [[Syria|Syrian]] civil war, which began in 2011, is primarily a conflict between the government of [[Bashar al-Assad]] in [[Damascus]] and various anti-government rebel factions.

(00:09:18) In early April 2018, [[Donald Trump]] announced his intention to withdraw [[United States]] forces from [[Syria]], citing the defeat of [[ISIS]], but later conceded to pressure from [[Saudi Arabia]], [[Israel]], and the military establishment to remain.

(00:10:36) [[Nikki Haley]], the [[United States]] Ambassador to the [[United Nations]], stated in April 2017 that the role of the [[United States]] in [[Syria]] was not to depose [[Bashar al-Assad]], a policy shift from the prior [[Obama administration]].

(00:11:13) [[Bashar al-Assad]]'s forces, with the assistance of [[Russia|Russian]] airstrikes and [[Iran|Iranian]] militias, gradually regained control of [[Syria]] after 2016, culminating in a major victory in East Ghouta near [[Damascus]] in April 2018.

(00:15:34) Within 48 hours of the alleged chemical attack in Douma, the [[United States]] government accused [[Bashar al-Assad]], [[Russia]], and [[Iran]] of responsibility without presenting independent verification from the [[United Nations]].

(00:17:10) The [[United Nations]] announced on April 9, 2018, that it could not confirm a chemical weapons attack had occurred in Douma, [[Syria]], citing only reports of symptoms consistent with such an attack.

(00:18:23) The April 2017 [[United States]] missile strike on a [[Syria|Syrian]] airfield was tactically inconsequential, as the airfield was operational again within 24 hours, and [[Russia]] did not retaliate.

(00:19:55) On April 8, 2018, [[Israel]] conducted an airstrike on the T-4 military airfield in [[Syria]] by launching eight guided missiles from warplanes that had entered [[Lebanon|Lebanese]] airspace, five of which were shot down.

(00:21:23) [[Israel]] had previously struck the same T-4 airfield in [[Syria]] in February 2018 in response to an [[Iran|Iranian]] drone that violated [[Israel|Israeli]] airspace.

(00:22:24) Following the [[Israel|Israeli]] airstrike, [[Donald Trump]] promised a coordinated response with [[France|French]] President [[Emmanuel Macron]] to the alleged chemical attack in Douma.

(00:24:23) On April 9, 2018, [[John Bolton]] was sworn in as National Security Advisor to [[Donald Trump]], a figure known for his hawkish foreign policy views.

(00:25:44) In April 2018, the USS Donald Cook, a [[United States]] missile destroyer armed with 60 Tomahawk missiles, was reported to have moved to within 100 kilometers of the [[Syria|Syrian]] coast near Tartus.

(00:28:57) [[Mohammed bin Salman]], the Crown Prince of [[Saudi Arabia]], had been consolidating power and making disruptive reforms while strengthening ties with the [[United States]] and [[Europe]] in early 2018.

(00:30:29) In April 2017, [[Donald Trump]] ordered the missile strike on [[Syria]] while hosting [[China|Chinese]] President [[Xi Jinping]] at Mar-a-Lago, linking the action to broader diplomacy with [[China]] and [[North Korea]].

(00:31:58) [[Donald Trump]] was scheduled to hold the first-ever face-to-face meeting between a sitting [[United States]] president and a [[North Korea|North Korean]] head of state in April or May 2018.

(00:39:11) The civil war in [[Yemen]], fueled by [[Saudi Arabia]] and [[Iran]] with [[United States]] logistical support, was considered the world's worst humanitarian disaster in April 2018, featuring widespread famine and a historic cholera outbreak.

(00:40:33) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that the [[United States]] government is controlled by a permanent, transnational "deep state" within the [[Pentagon]], [[State Department]], and intelligence community, rather than by its elected civilian leadership.
