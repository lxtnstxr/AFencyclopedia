## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes characterized right-wing movements in the United States, France, the United Kingdom, and Italy as internally divided. He stated opposition to National Socialism due to intellectual conflicts with Catholic doctrine but expressed interest in other forms of fascism like Spanish Phalangism. Fuentes argued against free trade principles, citing national security risks from outsourcing to China and recommended Ian Fletcher's book *Free Trade Doesn't Work*. He also asserted that women are not political by nature and that interracial marriages lead to higher rates of domestic abuse and divorce.
