## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes announced that supporters planned to confront Charlie Kirk at a University of Iowa event. At a Colorado State University event, an attendee questioned Charlie Kirk about U.S. aid to Israel, which Kirk dismissed as a conspiracy theory. Donald Trump lifted sanctions on Turkey following a ceasefire brokered by Mike Pence and Mike Pompeo in northeastern Syria. Two University of Connecticut students were arrested and charged with a misdemeanor for shouting a racial slur.

(00:44:14) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced that two unidentified "Groypers" were preparing to confront [[Charlie Kirk]] with a question at an event at the [[University of Iowa]].

(01:13:58) [[Charlie Kirk]], the head of [[Turning Point USA]], was on a "Culture Wars" campus tour and held an event at [[Colorado State University]] with [[Donald Trump Jr.]] and [[Kimberly Guilfoyle]].

(01:29:48) An unidentified attendee at the [[Colorado State University]] event asked [[Charlie Kirk]] about the [[United States]] providing $3.8 billion annually in foreign aid to [[Israel]], contrasting it with aid to poorer regions and referencing the 1967 [[USS Liberty]] incident.

(02:07:26) A second unidentified attendee at the [[Colorado State University]] event challenged [[Charlie Kirk]] on [[Turning Point USA]]'s stance on immigration, arguing that both legal and illegal immigration would lead to [[Texas]] turning blue electorally.

(04:05:61) [[Donald Trump]] criticized political pundits for advocating further military intervention in [[Syria]] and linked such wars to policies of unlimited migration that import terrorism.

(04:25:43) Two students, Jared Carral and Ryan Mucaj, were arrested at the [[University of Connecticut]] and charged with a misdemeanor for shouting a racial slur in a parking lot, facing up to 30 days in jail.
