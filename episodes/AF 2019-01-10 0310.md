## Summary

Donald Trump's administration engaged in a government shutdown over border wall funding, reaching its 20th day on January 10, 2019. The United States Senate passed a bill to guarantee back pay for 800,000 furloughed federal workers affected by that shutdown. Republican Senator Lindsey Graham proposed a framework allocating $5.7 billion for a border wall in exchange for amnesty for 1.6 million DACA recipients. The New York Times writer Sarah Jeong had tweeted statements about white people, and the newspaper did not fire her after these tweets surfaced.
