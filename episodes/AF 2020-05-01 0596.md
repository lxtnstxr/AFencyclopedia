## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes announced the upcoming launch of a subscription archival website, NicholasJFuentes.com. Joe Biden formally denied a sexual assault allegation from former staffer Tara Reade during an appearance on MSNBC. Donald Trump threatened new tariffs on China and his administration crafted retaliatory measures for its handling of the COVID-19 pandemic.

(00:00:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced the upcoming launch of a subscription-based archival website, [[NicholasJFuentes.com]], containing nearly a terabyte of his past content including all episodes of America First, interviews, speeches, and gaming streams, set to go live the following Monday.

(00:01:34) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that the [[White House]] and [[Donald Trump]] were considering economic retaliation against [[China]] for its role in the origin and spread of the Coronavirus, which he asserted likely originated from a government laboratory in [[Wuhan]] rather than a wet market.

(00:03:53) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced he would discuss a sexual assault allegation against [[Joe Biden]] from a former staffer named Tara Reade, which he noted was gaining traction and had been corroborated by contemporaries.

(00:05:05) [[Joe Biden]] formally denied the sexual assault allegations during an appearance on [[MSNBC]], stating the incident "never happened" and questioning why the claim was raised after 27 years.

(00:17:55) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] read a statement from [[CNN]] reporting that [[Joe Biden]] said women making allegations deserve to be heard and treated with dignity, but that their stories should also be subject to scrutiny.

(00:22:59) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that the Democratic Party's "believe all women" standard, previously applied to accusations against [[Donald Trump]], [[Brett Kavanaugh]], and [[Roy Moore]], was now creating a contradiction for [[Joe Biden]] as the presumptive nominee.

(00:37:51) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] read a detailed account from [[CNN]] in which Tara Reade alleged that [[Joe Biden]] sexually assaulted her in a Capitol Hill corridor in 1993 while she worked as a staffer in his Senate office.

(00:56:01) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] read a report from the [[New York Times]] stating that [[Donald Trump]] said his trade deal with [[China]] was now secondary to the pandemic and that he threatened new tariffs as his administration crafted retaliatory measures.

(01:04:19) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] cited a [[Washington Post]] report that some [[United States]] officials had discussed canceling debt held by [[China]] as a retaliatory option for its handling of the COVID-19 pandemic, an idea later denied by Trump's top economic advisor.

(01:05:48) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that canceling [[United States]] debt held by [[China]] would undermine global faith in American debt and cause an economic meltdown, making tariffs and trade barriers a more likely form of retaliation.

(01:11:50) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that [[Donald Trump]] would benefit politically from retaliating against [[China]] for the Coronavirus, as the public was angry about the pandemic's disruption and placed blame on the Chinese government.
