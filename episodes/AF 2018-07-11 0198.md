## Summary

Donald Trump met with NATO members in Brussels and criticized Germany's Nord Stream 2 pipeline deal with Russia. Germany spent 1.2% of its GDP on defense while the United States spent 4.2%, and only five NATO members met the 2% spending target. Former German Chancellor Gerhard Schröder oversaw the Nord Stream 2 project after leaving office. NATO expanded eastward after the Soviet Union's 1991 collapse, and its Article 5 commits the United States to defend members like Estonia from Russian attack.
