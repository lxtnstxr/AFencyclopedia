## Summary

Robert Mueller testified before the House of Representatives that his report on Russian election interference represented his complete testimony. The House of Representatives passed House Resolution 246 by a vote of 398 to 17, expressing opposition to the Boycott, Divestment, and Sanctions movement targeting Israel. North Korea fired two projectiles from near Wonsan and stated it might lift its suspension of nuclear and missile tests in protest of expected U.S.-South Korea military drills. Donald Trump met with Kim Jong-un in Singapore and later stepped into North Korea, subsequently inviting Kim to the White House.

(00:04:44) [[Robert Mueller]] testified before the [[House of Representatives]] that his report on Russian election interference, released on May 29th, represented his complete testimony and that he would not summarize or describe its results differently.

(00:07:46) The [[House of Representatives]] passed House Resolution 246 by a vote of 398 to 17, a resolution expressing opposition to the Boycott, Divestment, and Sanctions (BDS) movement targeting [[Israel]].

(00:09:26) [[Ron DeSantis]], as Governor of [[Florida]], signed a state law earlier in the year while in [[Israel]] that could suspend students from public schools for supporting BDS.

(00:13:59) [[Sheldon Adelson]] reportedly went to the [[White House]] to demand the disinvitation of cartoonist [[Ben Garrison]] from a social media summit due to Garrison's commentary on figures like the [[Rothschild family|Rothschilds]] and [[George Soros]].

(00:17:32) [[Robert Mueller]] concluded over five hours of testimony before the [[House of Representatives]] Intelligence and Judiciary committees, often referring questions to his published report or declining to answer.

(00:20:04) During his testimony, [[Robert Mueller]] corrected a statement, clarifying that his investigation did not determine whether [[Donald Trump]] had committed a crime, after initially suggesting he would have sought an indictment if not for a Justice Department policy against charging a sitting president.

(00:21:13) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that the [[Central Intelligence Agency|CIA]], the media, and political figures like [[Nancy Pelosi]] conspired in an attempt to remove [[Donald Trump]] from office following his election.

(00:26:44) [[North Korea]] fired two unidentified projectiles from near the eastern city of [[Wonsan]], which flew approximately 267 miles before landing in the Sea of Japan, according to [[South Korea]]'s Joint Chiefs of Staff.

(00:27:03) [[North Korea]] stated it might lift its 20-month suspension of nuclear and missile tests in protest of expected military drills between the [[United States]] and [[South Korea]], which Pyongyang characterized as an invasion rehearsal.

(00:29:02) [[Donald Trump]] traveled to [[Singapore]] to meet with [[Kim Jong-un]] and later became the first sitting U.S. president to step into [[North Korea]], kickstarting a new round of negotiations.

(00:31:32) [[Kim Jong-un]] was invited by [[Donald Trump]] to visit the [[White House]] following their meeting, but subsequent missile tests from [[North Korea]] occurred shortly after this diplomatic overture.

(00:33:23) Following the U.S. invasion of [[Iraq]], countries including [[Libya]], [[Syria]], and [[Iran]] shut down their nuclear programs, according to [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]].

(00:39:11) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] appeared on the Kill Stream with [[Ethan Ralph]] for about an hour and fifteen minutes, discussing the [[Owen Benjamin]] controversy and the election.
