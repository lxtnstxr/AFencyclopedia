## Summary

Ruth Bader Ginsburg died on September 18, 2020, creating a Supreme Court vacancy less than six weeks before the presidential election. Mitch McConnell pledged a floor vote for Donald Trump's nominee, reversing his 2016 position regarding Barack Obama's nominee Merrick Garland. Several Republican senators, including Lisa Murkowski, Susan Collins, Chuck Grassley, and Mitt Romney, initially stated they would not vote to confirm a replacement before the election.
