## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes argued that U.S. military aid to Israel lacks constitutional basis and that Israel acts as a rogue state, citing espionage cases and its independent nuclear arsenal. Will Chamberlain contended that such aid deters a major Middle East war, provides crucial intelligence cooperation, and offers the United States strategic leverage. Fuentes asserted Israel's military self-sufficiency and its liability to U.S. interests, referencing the 1973 oil embargo and Israel's past technology transfers to China. Chamberlain warned that ending aid would risk regional invasion, remove restraints on Israeli settlements, and eliminate a key intelligence asset against groups like ISIS and Hezbollah.
