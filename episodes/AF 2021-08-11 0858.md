## Summary

The 2020 United States Census reported a decline in the white American population to nearly 60%, with the Hispanic share doubling to 20% and the Asian share doubling to 6%. United Nations Secretary-General António Guterres described a 2021 IPCC report as a "code red for humanity," calling for combined forces to avert climate catastrophe ahead of the COP26 summit in Glasgow. Nicholas J. Fuentes argued that IPCC climate policies aimed to establish global government rules that would reduce quality of life and that demographic changes would fundamentally alter the character of the United States.
