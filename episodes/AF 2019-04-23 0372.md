## Summary

Donald Trump met with Twitter CEO Jack Dorsey at the White House in 2019 to discuss election integrity and the opioid crisis. Jared Kushner consulted the George W. Bush Center and the Heritage Foundation while developing a merit-based immigration proposal for the president. Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu announced plans to name a Golan Heights settlement after Donald Trump following U.S. recognition of Israeli sovereignty there.

---

(00:04:43) [[Donald Trump]] met with [[Twitter]] chief executive [[Jack Dorsey]] at the White House to discuss the platform's commitment to protecting the health of public conversation ahead of the 2020 US elections and efforts to respond to the opioid crisis.

(00:05:10) [[Donald Trump]] accused [[Twitter]] of discriminatory behavior against conservative users, stating it was "hard for people to sign on" and that the company was "constantly taking people off the list," prompting him to suggest [[United States Congress|Congress]] should get involved.

(00:07:01) [[Patreon]] banned [[Robert Spencer]] of Jihad Watch after [[Mastercard]] reportedly threatened to stop handling the platform's transactions if he remained.

(00:10:19) An unverified report suggested [[Donald Trump]] called [[Jack Dorsey]] to the Oval Office to complain about losing [[Twitter]] followers, being stuck at approximately 59 million.

(00:18:34) [[Jared Kushner]], named one of [[Time (magazine)|Time]]'s 100 most influential people, stated in an interview that he was in charge of Middle East policy, immigration policy, and had handled the government shutdown and negotiated the [[United States–Mexico–Canada Agreement|USMCA]].

(00:20:26) [[Jared Kushner]] announced he would present a detailed immigration proposal to [[Donald Trump]] the following week, centered on improving border security, moving to a merit-based system, and maintaining humanitarian values.

(00:25:43) [[Jared Kushner]] consulted with the [[George W. Bush Presidential Center|George W. Bush Center]], the [[Heritage Foundation]], and the [[Koch family|Koch Brothers]] while developing his immigration proposal.

(00:27:33) The [[Supreme Court of the United States|Supreme Court]] heard arguments on including a citizenship question on the 2020 census, with [[John Roberts|Chief Justice John Roberts]] and [[Brett Kavanaugh|Justice Brett Kavanaugh]] directing most questions at lawyers challenging the question.

(00:28:55) [[Sonia Sotomayor|Justice Sonia Sotomayor]] argued that asking about citizenship on the census would depress response rates, a point immigration restrictionists viewed as beneficial for reducing representation and funding based on non-citizen counts.

(00:33:48) [[Donald Trump]] signed a presidential memorandum ordering the [[United States Department of State|State Department]] and [[United States Department of Homeland Security|Department of Homeland Security]] to develop recommendations within 120 days to reduce overstays of B1 and B2 visas from countries with rates exceeding 10 percent, targeting over 415,000 individuals.

(00:38:32) [[Benjamin Netanyahu|Bibi Netanyahu]] announced plans to name a new settlement in the [[Golan Heights]] after [[Donald Trump]], honoring the U.S. president's recognition of Israeli sovereignty over the region in March.

(00:39:35) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] criticized figures like [[Ben Shapiro]], [[Ari Mandel]], [[Elliott Hamilton]], and [[Harry Khachatrian]] for their focus on [[Israel]], suggesting their primary reason for being in the [[United States]] was financial, comparing it to a colonial relationship.

(00:41:15) [[Jared Kushner]] stated that [[Benjamin Netanyahu|Bibi Netanyahu]] stays in his high-rise building's penthouse suite during visits to the [[United States]].
