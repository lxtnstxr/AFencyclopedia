## Summary

Brett Kavanaugh was confirmed to the Supreme Court by a Senate vote of 50-48 on October 6, 2018, with Senator Susan Collins providing a decisive vote. Jair Bolsonaro of Brazil's Social Liberal Party won the first round of Brazil's presidential election on October 7, 2018, with 46% of the vote. Kavanaugh's confirmation established a strong conservative majority on the Supreme Court, and he became the first justice to hire an all-female cohort of law clerks.

(00:00:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] hosts his show on Columbus Day, October 8, 2018, following a weekend that included the confirmation of [[Brett Kavanaugh]] and the first-round presidential election victory of [[Jair Bolsonaro]] in [[Brazil]].

(00:01:00) On Saturday, October 6, 2018, [[Brett Kavanaugh]] was confirmed to the [[Supreme Court of the United States|Supreme Court]] by a Senate vote of 50-48, with [[Susan Collins]] providing the decisive vote after delivering a floor speech arguing he was a moderate who would not overturn *Roe v. Wade*.

(00:02:00) [[Susan Collins]] justified her vote for [[Brett Kavanaugh]] by publicly arguing he was a centrist who would not overturn *Roe v. Wade*, a characterization contradicted by analyses rating him as the second-most conservative justice behind [[Clarence Thomas]].

(00:15:00) [[Joe Manchin]] was the only Democratic senator to vote for [[Brett Kavanaugh]]'s confirmation, while [[Lisa Murkowski]] voted against it; [[Steve Daines]] missed the vote due to his daughter's wedding.

(00:20:00) [[Brett Kavanaugh]]'s confirmation secures a strong conservative majority on the [[Supreme Court of the United States|Supreme Court]] for a generation, with potential to uphold policies like the travel ban and the rescission of DACA.

(00:23:00) Following the confirmation vote, [[Christine Blasey Ford]] announced she had no further plans to pursue her allegations against [[Brett Kavanaugh]], after having raised millions of dollars through GoFundMe campaigns.

(00:28:00) [[Brett Kavanaugh]], upon joining the [[Supreme Court of the United States|Supreme Court]], became the first justice to hire an all-female cohort of law clerks.

(00:32:00) On October 7, 2018, [[Jair Bolsonaro]] of the [[Social Liberal Party (Brazil)|Social Liberal Party]] won the first round of [[Brazil]]'s presidential election with 46% of the vote, advancing to a runoff against Workers' Party candidate [[Fernando Haddad]].

(00:34:00) [[Jair Bolsonaro]]'s political coalition in [[Brazil]] includes young men, evangelical Christians, older women, and the military, united by his platform of traditional family values, anti-crime measures, gun rights, and opposition to abortion and drug liberalization.

(00:40:00) [[Jair Bolsonaro]]'s electoral success exemplifies a global trend of right-wing populist victories, seen also in [[Hungary]] with [[Viktor Orbán]], [[Italy]] with [[Matteo Salvini]], [[Russia]] with [[Vladimir Putin]], and [[Turkey]] with [[Recep Tayyip Erdoğan]].
