## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes argued that a key political litmus test should be the appeal to blue-collar Catholic white voters in Wisconsin, a demographic he saw as crucial for nationalist growth in several Midwestern states. Richard Spencer countered that this demographic had not shaped culture for a century, while Mike Enoch stated his aim was broad cultural transformation rather than electoral politics. Senator Jeff Flake announced he would not seek re-election in Arizona's 2018 GOP primary, citing the Trump administration's behavior, which Steve Bannon suggested signaled that "nobody's safe" for establishment Republicans. Donald Trump issued a memorandum banning refugee admissions from eleven countries unless they underwent enhanced screening, a policy Nicholas J. Fuentes cited among several Trump predictions he claimed were vindicated.
