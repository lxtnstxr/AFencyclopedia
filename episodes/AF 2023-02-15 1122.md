## Summary

Nikki Haley announced her candidacy for the Republican Party presidential nomination after serving as U.S. Ambassador to the United Nations and Governor of South Carolina. Following her governorship, Haley joined the board of Boeing, a company that had received hundreds of millions in state tax breaks during her administration. Nicholas J. Fuentes argued that Haley's campaign, which called for a "new generation of leadership," represented a Republican Party rebranding away from its core demographic.
