## Summary

Mike Pompeo pushed for a U.S. airstrike on an Iranian nuclear facility following tanker attacks in the Strait of Hormuz. The United States blamed Iran for the attacks, but the Japanese government requested concrete evidence and analysts suggested other possible actors. Donald Trump withdrew the United States from the Iran nuclear deal, reinstated full sanctions, and Iran subsequently announced it would increase uranium enrichment beyond agreed limits. Harvard University rescinded Kyle Kashuv's admission after the leak of private conversations containing racial slurs, prompting defense from commentators like Ben Shapiro and condemnation from others like Laura Loomer.

(00:02:30) [[Mike Pompeo]] pushed for an airstrike on [[Iran]] to destroy one of its nuclear facilities, according to a report from Israeli media citing diplomatic sources at the [[United Nations]].

(00:02:50) Two commercial ships, one belonging to [[Norway]] and one to [[Japan]], were attacked in the Strait of Hormuz, a critical channel for global oil shipments.

(00:03:00) The [[United States]] government immediately blamed [[Iran]] for the tanker attacks, while [[Iran]] denied responsibility.

(00:03:30) [[Mike Pompeo]] and the [[United States]] government released a video purporting to show [[Iran|Iranian]] personnel removing an undetonated mine from the Japanese tanker, though the Japanese owner of the ship disputed that a mine caused the damage.

(00:03:50) [[Kyle Kashuv]] had his admission to [[Harvard]] University rescinded following the leak of private conversations from when he was 16, in which he repeatedly used racial slurs.

(00:05:10) [[Ben Shapiro]], [[Dave Rubin]], and [[Candace Owens]] publicly came to the defense of [[Kyle Kashuv]] after [[Harvard]] rescinded his admission.

(00:05:20) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that [[Harvard]] University, as a private institution, had the right to rescind [[Kyle Kashuv]]'s admission.

(00:06:50) [[Steve Daines]] introduced a bill to [[United States Congress|Congress]] proposing a constitutional amendment to ban burning the [[United States]] flag, which [[Donald Trump]] endorsed on Twitter.

(00:09:30) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced he was considering live-streaming and reacting to a [[Donald Trump]] campaign rally in [[Orlando]], [[Florida]] scheduled for the following day.

(00:11:50) The [[BBC]] and the [[United Kingdom]] passed a new rule prohibiting advertisements that promote harmful gender stereotypes.

(00:12:10) A Father's Day advertisement from the sports retailer Champions depicted a white father with a black son, which [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] characterized as deliberate social programming.

(00:15:20) [[Kyle Kashuv]] publicly announced on Twitter that [[Harvard]] University had rescinded his admission offer.

(00:16:40) The leaked document that led to [[Kyle Kashuv]]'s admission being rescinded also contained a veiled threat of a school shooting, referencing building a map in CS:GO that resembled [[Parkland]].

(00:17:00) [[Kyle Kashuv]] became a political figure as the right-wing response to left-wing Parkland shooting survivors like [[David Hogg]], promoted by figures such as [[Ali Alexander]] and later [[Ben Shapiro]].

(00:18:10) [[Laura Loomer]] actively campaigned for [[Harvard]] to rescind [[Kyle Kashuv]]'s admission following the leak of his racist comments.

(00:20:00) [[Ben Shapiro]] condemned [[Steve King]] after the [[New York Times]] published comments taken out of context, and also condemned the [[Covington Catholic High School|Covington]] kids following a controversial media narrative.

(00:21:50) [[Ben Shapiro]] defended [[James Gunn]] when old tweets containing pedophilic jokes were revealed, and defended [[Sarah Jeong]] when her anti-white tweets were exposed.

(00:22:50) [[Ben Shapiro]] defended NFL players kneeling during the national anthem, a position opposed by [[Donald Trump]] and many conservatives at the time.

(00:25:20) [[Ben Shapiro]] tweeted a secretly recorded, out-of-context clip of [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] when he was 18, which [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] claimed contributed to him being ostracized and unable to attend [[Auburn University]].

(00:27:50) [[Donald Trump]] withdrew the [[United States]] from the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (Iran nuclear deal) and stated his goal was to negotiate a new deal addressing [[Iran]]'s nuclear program, missile development, and foreign interventions.

(00:29:00) The [[United States]] reinstated full sanctions on [[Iran]] and, the prior month, ended waivers that had allowed [[Iran]] to continue selling oil, placing its economy under severe strain.

(00:31:50) Intelligence analysts from the [[Center for Strategic and International Studies]] and the [[Japan|Japanese]] government expressed skepticism about the [[United States]] video evidence blaming [[Iran]] for the tanker attacks, suggesting other actors like [[ISIS]], [[Saudi Arabia]], or the [[United Arab Emirates]] could be responsible.

(00:32:30) The [[Japan|Japanese]] government formally requested concrete evidence from the [[United States]] to back its assertion that [[Iran]] was responsible for the tanker attacks and stated the provided video was not definitive proof.

(00:38:50) A report in Israeli media, citing [[United Nations]] diplomatic sources, claimed the [[United States]] was planning a "massive but limited" tactical airstrike on an [[Iran|Iranian]] nuclear facility in response to the tanker attacks.

(00:39:10) The same report stated [[Donald Trump]] was not enthusiastic about military action against [[Iran]] but had lost patience and would grant [[Mike Pompeo]], who was pushing for action, what he wanted.

(00:46:30) [[Iran]] announced it would increase its uranium enrichment levels beyond the 3.67% cap agreed to in the 2015 nuclear deal, moving toward 20% enrichment for use in reactors.

(00:48:50) [[Benjamin Netanyahu|Bibi Netanyahu]] stated at the [[United Nations]] nearly a decade prior that [[Iran]] was 95% of the way to a nuclear bomb, a weapon [[Iran]] still had not demonstrated as of the time of the monologue.

(00:50:30) A [[United States Congress|Congressional]] study indicated that even a full-scale [[United States]] bombing campaign destroying [[Iran]]'s entire known nuclear program would only delay its reconstitution by approximately six to nine months.

(01:00:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that if [[Israel]] or [[Saudi Arabia]] were concerned about [[Iran|Iranian]] nuclear proliferation, they should address it themselves, as both possess strong militaries and, in [[Israel]]'s case, a nuclear arsenal.
