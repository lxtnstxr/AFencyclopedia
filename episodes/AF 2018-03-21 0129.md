## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes announced a debate with Michael Tokes on civic versus ethnic nationalism. Jack Burkman offered a $130,000 bounty regarding the murder of DNC staffer Seth Rich and was later assaulted and shot by a private investigator he had hired. Donald Trump congratulated Vladimir Putin on his re-election, drawing criticism from John McCain and Lindsey Graham, while other Western leaders like Angela Merkel also offered congratulations. John McCain supported military interventions in numerous countries and was complicit in election interference in Ukraine.

---

(00:02:10) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced a debate with [[Michael Tokes]] on the topic of civic nationalism versus ethnic nationalism, scheduled for Friday at 7 p.m. Central on the Baked Alaska Experience, with a post-debate recap on his own show.

(00:08:00) [[Jack Burkman]], a lobbyist, offered a $130,000 bounty in 2017 for information leading to the arrest or resolution in the murder of [[Seth Rich]], a [[Democratic National Committee|DNC]] staffer killed on July 10, 2016 in Washington, D.C.

(00:09:30) [[Jack Burkman]] hired a private investigator, former Marine [[Kevin Doherty]], to look into the murder of [[Seth Rich]], but later fired him, leading to a dispute.

(00:10:40) [[Kevin Doherty]], posing as an informant, lured [[Jack Burkman]] to a parking garage with promises of emails about [[Andrew McCabe]], where he then shot Burkman twice and ran him over with an SUV.

(00:12:50) In January, [[Jack Burkman]] was assaulted outside his Virginia home by a masked individual who pepper-sprayed him before fleeing in a black sports sedan.

(00:20:30) [[Donald Trump]] called [[Vladimir Putin]] to congratulate him on winning a fourth term as President of [[Russia]], an action for which he was criticized by figures like [[John McCain]] and [[Lindsey Graham]].

(00:21:50) White House Press Secretary [[Sarah Huckabee Sanders]] stated, regarding [[Russia]]'s election, "We don't get to dictate how other countries operate."

(00:23:40) [[John McCain]] criticized [[Donald Trump]]'s call to [[Vladimir Putin]], stating, "An American president does not lead the free world by congratulating dictators on winning sham elections."

(00:25:20) [[John McCain]] advocated for U.S. military intervention in numerous countries including [[Iran]], [[Iraq]], [[Yemen]], [[Saudi Arabia]], [[Egypt]], [[North Korea]], [[China]], [[Russia]], [[Venezuela]], and [[Colombia]].

(00:25:40) [[John McCain]] was complicit in U.S. and [[European Union]] interference in [[Ukraine]]nian elections to oust a pro-Russian government in favor of a pro-Western one.

(00:27:40) [[John McCain]] supported NATO airstrikes that led to the deposition and death of [[Muammar Gaddafi]] in [[Libya]], contributing to the country's subsequent collapse into civil war and a failed state.

(00:29:20) [[Lindsey Graham]] argued that failing to expel Russian diplomats after [[Donald Trump]]'s call to [[Vladimir Putin]] would be seen by [[Russia]] as a sign of American weakness.

(00:31:50) [[Angela Merkel]], [[Emmanuel Macron]], and [[Jean-Claude Juncker]] of the [[European Union]] also congratulated [[Vladimir Putin]] on his election victory.

(00:32:30) In 2012, [[Barack Obama]] called [[Vladimir Putin]] to congratulate him on his election victory after [[Putin]] changed the constitution to allow for additional terms.

(00:34:00) [[John McCain]] fully supported [[Saudi Arabia]] and its war in [[Yemen]], as well as [[Israel]], despite criticisms of their human rights records and military conduct.

(00:39:30) Chicago Mayor [[Rahm Emanuel]] rolled out a municipal ID system that illegal immigrants could use, which also functioned as a voter registration card.

(00:47:40) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that commentators like [[Tucker Carlson]], [[Pat Buchanan]], and [[Ann Coulter]] are "substantially red-pilled" and further along in their views than publicly believed.

(00:58:50) In the Illinois Republican gubernatorial primary, [[Jeanne Ives]] came within four points of defeating incumbent [[Bruce Rauner]], who outspent her by tens of millions of dollars.

(00:59:40) [[J.B. Pritzker]], a billionaire, spent approximately $80 million of his own money on his successful campaign for the Democratic nomination for Governor of [[Illinois]].
