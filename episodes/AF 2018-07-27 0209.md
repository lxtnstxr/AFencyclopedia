## Summary

The Bronze Age Pervert defined the "Bronze Age Mindset" as a Western spirit embodied by historical figures like Achilles and Donald Trump. Michael Ma discussed plans to establish an off-the-grid community on 1,500 acres in Texas and expressed interest in the anarcho-primitivism of Ted Kaczynski. Nicholas J. Fuentes promoted the "#BringBackPapa" campaign to reinstate John Schnatter to Papa John's Pizza, for which Schnatter was suing the company.

(00:00:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] hosted a show featuring a special guest, [[Michael Ma]], who delivered a message from the [[Bronze Age Pervert]] regarding his new book *The Bronze Age Mindset*.

(00:02:50) [[Michael Ma]] stated he had received a scroll from the [[Bronze Age Pervert]] via the [[United States Postal Service]] and would relay its contents.

(00:03:35) The [[Bronze Age Pervert]], through [[Michael Ma]], defined the "Bronze Age Mindset" as the spirit of ancient heroes like [[Achilles]] and [[Odysseus]], a distinctly Western force that animated figures like [[Alexander the Great|Alexander]] and [[Julius Caesar|Caesar]], and which [[Donald Trump|Trump]] was said to embody.

(00:04:52) The [[Bronze Age Pervert]] argued that the "Bronze Age Mindset" was in danger of being snuffed out in the modern era and could not be revived through theory alone.

(00:08:45) The [[Bronze Age Pervert]] advised young men to have confidence in their own strength, form strong friendships, lift weights, read great books, and let historical figures like [[Julius Caesar|Caesar]], [[Alexander the Great|Alexander]], and [[Napoleon Bonaparte|Napoleon]] inspire them.

(00:09:30) The [[Bronze Age Pervert]] recommended studying the deep state in [[Turkey]], the rise of the [[Muslim Brotherhood]], and Saul Alinsky's *Rules for Radicals*.

(00:16:07) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] asked the [[Bronze Age Pervert]] about his book's stylistic choice of short, incomplete sentences and primitive language.

(00:16:43) The [[Bronze Age Pervert]] explained he wrote the book like he writes his "Twatter," attributing the style to being a "mantic from the Bronze Age" who has been alive for 3,500 years, and stated he aimed to show an intoxication with life through humor and outrageousness.

(00:17:20) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] and [[Michael Ma]] discussed how the medium of [[Twitter]] and social media affects thought formulation, leading to communication in short, staccato bursts.

(00:24:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] asked the [[Bronze Age Pervert]] for a message to "black pillers" who believe political action is futile.

(00:25:45) The [[Bronze Age Pervert]] told black pillers that situations were worse in the past, that there were hundreds of millions of native Europeans and Americans who would resist, and that their enemies were stupid and weak, citing [[Donald Trump|Trump]] and rising European nationalists as examples of inspiration.

(00:26:32) The [[Bronze Age Pervert]] stated that even in a worst-case demographic scenario, the situation could be corrected, using "the Fugeans" as an example, and advised chronic black pillers to change their diet and lift weights.

(00:27:02) The [[Bronze Age Pervert]] recalled the fun of trolling during the [[2016 United States presidential election|2016 election]] and wanted to bring back that spirit of being a prankster while working for political goals.

(00:36:04) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] asked the [[Bronze Age Pervert]] if he was surprised by the commercial success of his self-published book.

(00:36:34) The [[Bronze Age Pervert]] stated he was told he might sell only a few hundred books but instead outsold establishment-backed authors like [[James Clapper|Clapper]] and [[John McCain|McCain]] for much of June, reaching high rankings site-wide on [[Amazon]].

(00:37:11) The [[Bronze Age Pervert]] mentioned that the book's success led to conspiracy theories that he was coordinating with intelligence agencies or [[Vox Media|Vox]], but his only coordination was editing advice from [[Mena]] and [[Delicious Tacos]] and a cover illustration from [[Owen Cyclops]].
