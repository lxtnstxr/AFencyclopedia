## Summary

Donald Trump canceled a June 12 summit with North Korea in Singapore, citing hostile statements from the North. North Korea had released American hostages and planned to demolish a nuclear test site but canceled a separate meeting with South Korea after John Bolton referenced the "Libyan model" of denuclearization. Nicholas J. Fuentes characterized Trump's cancellation as a negotiating tactic and argued that entrenched interests constrain U.S. foreign policy, while also making claims about historical slavery and generational political shifts.
