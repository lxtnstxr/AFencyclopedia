## Summary

Donald Trump denied an Axios report alleging he had suggested using nuclear weapons against hurricanes. YouTube CEO Susan Wojcicki published a blog post outlining the platform's "Four Rs" policy framework following the banning of channels including James Allsup. Nicholas J. Fuentes contrasted YouTube's stated commitment to openness with its removal of creators like Allsup and "Soph." Donald Trump also tweeted praise for Italian Prime Minister Giuseppe Conte, a statement interpreted as opposition to Interior Minister Matteo Salvini.

---

(00:01:50) [[Donald Trump]] was the subject of a trending [[Axios]] story alleging he had repeatedly suggested to advisors the idea of using nuclear weapons to disrupt hurricanes forming off the coast of the [[United States]].

(00:02:23) [[Axios]], a media outlet, published a report citing unnamed White House sources claiming [[Donald Trump]] had discussed the possibility of ordering a nuclear strike on hurricanes.

(00:02:54) [[Donald Trump]] tweeted three times to deny the [[Axios]] story, calling it "ridiculous" and "fake news," and questioned the outlet's motives for publishing it.

(00:03:06) [[Donald Trump]] accused [[Axios]] of continuing to spread the hurricane story even after he returned from the [[G7]] summit in [[France]].

(00:04:05) [[Axios]] publicly replied to [[Donald Trump|Trump]]'s denial tweets, stating they stood by their reporting and linked to their original article.

(00:05:14) [[Donald Trump]] was criticized for a tweet that praised [[Giuseppe Conte]], the Prime Minister of [[Italy]], which was seen as a slight against the nationalist Interior Minister [[Matteo Salvini]].

(00:05:37) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced he would discuss a statement from [[YouTube]] CEO [[Susan Wojcicki]] issued 24 hours after the mass banning of channels including [[James Allsup]].

(00:05:51) [[Susan Wojcicki]], the CEO of [[YouTube]], tweeted about preserving [[YouTube]] as an open platform just one day after a purge that removed channels like [[James Allsup|James Alsop]], [[VDARE]], and [[American Identity Movement]].

(00:06:16) [[Susan Wojcicki]]'s blog post stated that preserving an open platform was more important than ever and involved balancing openness with responsibility, a statement [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] characterized as contradictory to [[YouTube]]'s recent banning actions.

(00:08:05) [[Susan Wojcicki]]'s blog post outlined [[YouTube]]'s "Four Rs" policy framework: Removal of violating content, Raising up authoritative voices, Reducing the spread of borderline content, and Rewarding trusted creators.

(00:11:19) [[James Allsup]] was banned from [[Twitter]] in December 2017, from [[Facebook]] and [[Instagram]] just prior to his [[YouTube]] ban, in a pattern [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] compared to the deplatforming of [[Alex Jones]].

(00:13:50) A study on online radicalization listed [[James Allsup]] as one of the most influential channels pushing ideas like race realism and criticism of [[Israel|Israeli]] influence, with his channel having accrued 62 million video views.

(00:14:21) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that [[James Allsup]]'s banning was a vindication of his effectiveness, as his channel was the largest pushing certain ideas and was successful at drawing viewers from more mainstream conservative creators like [[Steven Crowder]] and [[Ben Shapiro]].

(00:16:40) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] planned to host a stream with [[James Allsup]] on his [[DLive]] channel the following day to discuss the ban.

(00:17:47) [[Susan Wojcicki]]'s blog post claimed [[YouTube]] was committed to leaving up controversial or offensive content to foster a stronger society, which [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] contrasted with the banning of [[James Allsup]] and other creators.

(00:19:54) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] cited the prior banning of the channel "Soph" or "Lieutenant Corbus," a teenage creator, as another example contradicting [[Susan Wojcicki]]'s statement about [[YouTube]]'s tolerance.

(00:20:18) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] referenced the "Adpocalypse" in June, a period when [[James Allsup]] was demonetized and [[Steven Crowder]] faced restrictions, as part of a pattern of [[YouTube]] censorship.

(00:25:56) [[Susan Wojcicki]] stated that one of [[YouTube]]'s "Four Rs," "Raise up," involved elevating authoritative voices like mainstream media during breaking news events, which [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued buried alternative and right-wing perspectives.

(00:29:50) [[Susan Wojcicki]] stated that another of the "Four Rs," "Reduce," involved deliberately decreasing the spread of content that came close to violating policies, which [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued was used to suppress right-wing creators like [[Stefan Molyneux]] without actual rule violations.

(00:37:38) [[Axios]] reported, based on unnamed sources, that [[Donald Trump]] had suggested on multiple occasions the idea of a nuclear strike on hurricanes to his advisors and military personnel.

(00:38:07) [[Donald Trump]] tweeted a denial of the [[Axios]] hurricane story, calling it "phony" and stating it "never happened."

(00:38:38) [[Donald Trump]] tweeted a third time about the [[Axios]] story, sarcastically suggesting the outlet concocted the story to embarrass him and declaring the media "totally out of control."

(00:50:42) [[Donald Trump]] tweeted praise for [[Italy|Italian]] Prime Minister [[Giuseppe Conte]], calling him "highly respected" and "a very talented man who will hopefully remain prime minister," a statement interpreted as opposition to [[Matteo Salvini]]'s bid for the position.

(00:51:04) [[Matteo Salvini]], as Interior Minister of [[Italy]], was credited with reducing illegal African migrant arrivals into [[Europe]] by approximately 92% since taking office.

(00:51:53) [[Matteo Salvini]]'s party, [[Lega]], was polling at around 34% and he had initiated a vote of no confidence in the coalition government in a bid to become Prime Minister of [[Italy]].

(00:52:49) [[Donald Trump|Trump]]'s tweet praising [[Giuseppe Conte]] was seen by [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] as an implicit attack on and a betrayal of the nationalist movement represented by [[Matteo Salvini]].

(01:08:50) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] referenced a tweet from a [[Campus Reform]] contributor named "Roasty" who declared a boycott of anyone who cared about what [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] had to say, which prompted a flood of critical replies from his supporters.
