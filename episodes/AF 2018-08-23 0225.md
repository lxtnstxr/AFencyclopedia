## Summary

Donald Trump cited a Tucker Carlson report in a tweet asking Mike Pompeo to study land seizures and violence against farmers in South Africa. Nicholas J. Fuentes claimed the African National Congress was moving to seize land without compensation and cited AfriForum's statistics on farm attacks and murders. Jared Holt of Right Wing Watch and Christopher Mathias of the Huffington Post characterized claims of a "white genocide" in South Africa as white nationalist propaganda.

(00:01:17) [[Donald Trump]] tweeted about the "white genocide" in [[South Africa]], asking Secretary of State [[Mike Pompeo]] to study land seizures and the killing of farmers, citing a report from [[Tucker Carlson]].

(00:02:04) [[Tucker Carlson]] reported on [[Fox News]] that the [[South Africa|South African]] government was seizing land from white farmers.

(00:04:48) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that [[Donald Trump|Trump's]] tweet originated from a segment by [[Tucker Carlson]] on [[Fox News]].

(00:06:05) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] contrasted [[Donald Trump|Trump's]] stance with potential 2016 Republican nominees like [[Marco Rubio]], [[Ted Cruz]], and [[Jeb Bush]].

(00:07:50) [[Darren Beattie]], a speechwriter for [[Donald Trump]], was fired from the [[White House]] after it was revealed he had spoken at the H.L. Mencken Club conference, which also featured white nationalists.

(00:09:28) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that having figures like [[Donald Trump]] and [[Tucker Carlson]] in positions of influence, supported by a network of aligned individuals in Washington D.C., had a disproportionate effect on public discourse regarding [[South Africa]].

(00:14:05) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] criticized [[Vox]] for producing content attacking [[Tucker Carlson]] as "normalizing white nationalism," referencing a presenter he described as a "flamboyant homosexual."

(00:14:59) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] claimed that [[Jared Holt]] of Right Wing Watch would likely attack [[Tucker Carlson]] for being praised by figures like himself.

(00:16:06) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] described the historical context of [[South Africa]], stating it was a colony of [[Great Britain]] where a white minority controlled most of the land, a situation that continued through the apartheid era.

(00:17:17) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that after apartheid ended under [[Nelson Mandela]], the [[South Africa|South African]] government began buying land from white farmers to redistribute to black citizens, increasing black land ownership from roughly 13% in 1994 to about 27% by 2015.

(00:18:12) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] claimed the [[African National Congress]], the ruling party in [[South Africa]], was now moving to seize land from white farmers without compensation to appease its black constituents, encouraging racial animosity and violence.

(00:21:12) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] cited [[Lauren Southern]]'s documentary and accounts from attendees of an [[American Renaissance]] conference as evidence supporting the reality of violence against white farmers in [[South Africa]].

(00:24:48) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] cited statistics from [[AfriForum]], a [[South Africa|South African]] civil rights group, which reported 82 people killed in 423 farm attacks in 2017, and 109 attacks with over 15 murders so far in 2018.

(00:25:26) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] presented a murder rate for white farmers in [[South Africa]] of 150 per 100,000, comparing it to a general murder rate of 34.1 per 100,000 in [[South Africa]] and approximately 10 per 100,000 in the [[United States]].

(00:26:12) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] accused journalists [[Joe Bernstein]] and [[Jared Holt]] of dismissing the violence in [[South Africa]] as "white nationalist propaganda."

(00:27:15) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] recommended [[Lauren Southern]]'s documentary on [[South Africa]] for a detailed ground-level account.

(00:28:32) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] read a tweet from [[Christopher Mathias]] of the [[Huffington Post]] that stated, "don't invite people on your shows to argue that there is a white genocide in South Africa," calling it "white nationalist propaganda."

(00:29:28) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] cited a [[Russia Today]] report that the [[African National Congress]] parliamentary caucus deleted a tweet calling white people "murderers" and stating land and resources should be shared without consultation.
