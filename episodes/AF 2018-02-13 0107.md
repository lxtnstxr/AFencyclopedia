## Summary

The United States Senate debated DACA as part of a government funding agreement between Mitch McConnell and Chuck Schumer. Multiple federal courts ruled Donald Trump could not unilaterally rescind DACA, leading to a Supreme Court appeal. Trump's 2018 immigration proposal offered a pathway to citizenship for 1.8 million people in exchange for ending chain migration, the diversity visa lottery, and building a border wall. The 1965 Hart-Celler Immigration Act significantly shifted United States immigration sources from Europe to over 90% non-European.
