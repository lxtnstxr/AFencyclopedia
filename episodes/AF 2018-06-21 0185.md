## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes identified a "Charlottesville coalition" including himself, Faith Goldy, Millennial Matt, Baked Alaska, and James Bryden. Faith Goldy moved her funding platform from Patreon to Free Starter after being deplatformed and reported her YouTube channel had reached 60,000 subscribers. Faith Goldy argued the Republican Party botched messaging on a family separation executive order, which she stated gave human traffickers a "moral alibi" and noted Melania Trump visited detained immigrants wearing a jacket with the phrase "I really don't care." Nicholas J. Fuentes asserted Republican Party leadership wanted open borders as much as the Democratic Party, while Faith Goldy cited a Pew study showing more white people are dying than being born in the United States and stated Canada is experiencing the world's fastest rate of ethnic change.
