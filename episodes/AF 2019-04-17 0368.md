## Summary

France announced an international competition for architects to design a new spire for Notre Dame Cathedral. A Reuters poll indicated healthcare was the top 2020 election issue for Democrats, while immigration was the top issue for Republicans. Donald Trump vetoed a congressional resolution to end U.S. military involvement in the Saudi-led war in Yemen.

---

(00:30:00) [[France]] announced an international competition for architects to design a new spire for [[Notre Dame Cathedral]], with Prime Minister [[Edouard Philippe]] stating officials would consider whether to replicate the old spire or create an original design.

(00:53:00) [[Emmanuel Macron]] stated a goal to rebuild [[Notre Dame Cathedral]] within five years, while architectural experts estimated the reconstruction would take at least ten years.

(01:18:00) A [[Reuters]] poll indicated that for [[Democratic Party|Democrats]], healthcare was the top issue for the 2020 election at 30%, followed by the economy at 12% and the environment at 10%.

(01:18:00) The same [[Reuters]] poll found that for [[Republican Party|Republicans]], immigration was the top issue for the 2020 election at 40%, followed by healthcare at 13% and the economy at 9%.

(01:20:00) [[Donald Trump]]'s approval rating on healthcare was 36%, with a 56% disapproval rating, according to polling data cited.

(01:23:00) A [[CBS]] poll found that 74% of respondents said the new tax law had no impact on their tax bill or forced them to pay more.

(01:24:00) Research by the [[Pew Research Center]] indicated 36% of Americans had a favorable opinion of the tax cut, while 49% disapproved.

(01:24:00) A [[Monmouth University]] poll showed the tax cut had a 51% approval rating among [[Republican Party|Republicans]], a 35% approval rating among independents, and a 14% approval rating among [[Democratic Party|Democrats]].

(01:25:00) [[Donald Trump]]'s approval rating on immigration was 41%, with a 54% disapproval rating.

(01:25:00) [[Donald Trump]] began using the slogan "Keep America Great" as a potential campaign theme for the 2020 election.

(01:33:00) [[Donald Trump]] vetoed a congressional resolution to end U.S. military involvement in the [[Saudi Arabia|Saudi]]-led war in [[Yemen]], marking the second veto of his administration.

(01:35:00) The congressional resolution to end involvement in [[Yemen]] was drafted in response to the killing of journalist [[Jamal Khashoggi]].

(01:37:00) In a statement on the veto, [[Donald Trump]] said the resolution was "an unnecessary and dangerous attempt to weaken my constitutional authorities."

(01:39:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] reported that within the [[Trump administration]], [[Jared Kushner]] reviewed and approved all matters before they reached the president's desk, with Chief of Staff [[Mick Mulvaney]] deferring to him.

(01:40:00) [[William Barr]], as [[United States Attorney General|Attorney General]], overturned a lower court ruling to allow the detention of asylum seekers who establish a credible fear, though the ruling did not apply to families.

(01:43:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that the [[Republican Party]] lacked efficacy and "has to die," citing its failure to deliver on tax cuts and other promises despite controlling the White House and Congress.
