## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes launched the America First streaming platform using personal funds and volunteer developers after his ban from DLive. The second America First Political Action Conference in Orlando featured keynote speaker Representative Paul Gosar and was streamed on the movement's independent platform. Speakers at the event included Michelle Malkin, former Representative Steve King, and commentator John Miller.

(00:06:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] was banned from the streaming platform [[DLive]] on January 8th or 9th following the Capitol riot, forcing him to develop an independent streaming alternative.

(00:06:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] began developing a streaming platform alternative in January 2020, but the project was not ready for launch when he was deplatformed from [[DLive]].

(00:06:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] launched a temporary streaming solution on his website within a week of being banned from [[DLive]], initially using an embedded [[YouTube]] video player.

(00:06:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] and his development team, composed of volunteers, later updated the website's user interface and implemented a live chat function using [[Telegram]] for user authentication.

(00:06:00) The [[America First]] streaming platform, funded entirely by [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]]'s personal money and built by volunteer developers, aims to be a censorship-proof alternative to platforms like [[Twitch]], [[DLive]], and [[Trovo]].

(00:06:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] plans for the [[America First]] streaming platform to eventually host other streamers within the movement, such as [[Vince James]], [[Jaden McNeil]], and [[Michelle Malkin]].
