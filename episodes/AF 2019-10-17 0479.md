## Summary

Mike Pence and Mike Pompeo traveled to Turkey to broker a ceasefire, requiring Kurdish forces to withdraw from northeastern Syria. The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention reported a 56% increase in suicide rates for people aged 10 to 24 between 2007 and 2017. Ken Cuccinelli, a candidate for Homeland Security Secretary, cited an MIT study estimating up to 22 million illegal immigrants in the United States.

---

(00:01:12) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced the featured story for the evening would be a ceasefire agreement in [[Syria]] negotiated by [[Mike Pence]] and [[Mike Pompeo]] with [[Turkey]].

(00:01:28) [[Mike Pence]] and [[Mike Pompeo]] traveled to [[Turkey]] to meet with President [[Recep Tayyip Erdoğan|Erdoğan]] and broker a deal to temporarily halt [[Turkey]]'s military incursion into [[Syria]].

(00:01:39) The agreement between [[Turkey]] and the [[United States]] required the [[Syrian Democratic Forces|YPG]] and [[Syrian Democratic Forces|SDF]] Kurdish militant groups to withdraw from northeastern [[Syria]].

(00:02:06) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] characterized the ceasefire in [[Syria]] as an unequivocal victory for the [[Donald Trump|Trump]] administration, achieving a clean withdrawal of [[United States]] troops and preventing a clash between [[Turkey]] and [[Syria]].

(00:03:44) The [[Centers for Disease Control and Prevention|CDC]] reported a 56% increase in the suicide rate for people aged 10 to 24 between 2007 and 2017.

(00:04:24) [[Ken Cuccinelli]], a candidate to lead the [[United States Department of Homeland Security|Department of Homeland Security]], publicly stated the number of illegal immigrants in the [[United States]] could be as high as 22 million, citing a study from [[Massachusetts Institute of Technology|MIT]].

(00:04:34) [[Kevin McAleenan]] stepped down from his position, leading to [[Ken Cuccinelli]] being reconsidered for the role of [[United States Department of Homeland Security|DHS]] Secretary.

(00:13:30) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated the primary focus for his movement should be opposing "fake conservatives" at organizations like [[Turning Point USA]] and publications like the [[Washington Examiner]].

(00:13:36) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that the [[Donald Trump|Trump]] administration's new rule, which denies green cards to immigrants who cannot afford healthcare, represented a positive step on immigration policy.

(00:14:52) [[Ken Cuccinelli]] argued that for policy purposes, the [[United States]] should operate under the assumption of 22 million illegal immigrants to fully grasp the scale of the crisis.

(00:28:00) A report in the [[The Wall Street Journal|Wall Street Journal]] detailed that suicide had become the second leading cause of death for people aged 10 to 24, behind only unintentional injuries.

(00:42:47) [[Mike Pence]] announced that [[United States]] economic sanctions on [[Turkey]] would be lifted once its military offensive in [[Syria]] ended.

(00:42:56) [[Turkey]]'s Foreign Minister [[Mevlüt Çavuşoğlu]] clarified that [[Turkey]] was only suspending its military operation in [[Syria]] and would halt it permanently only after Kurdish forces completely withdrew from the border zone.

(00:45:52) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] noted that the initial agreement between [[Donald Trump]] and [[Recep Tayyip Erdoğan|Erdoğan]] allowed [[Turkey]] to establish a safe zone in [[Syria]] in exchange for taking responsibility for detained [[Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant|ISIS]] fighters.

(00:46:52) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] observed that the entire foreign policy establishment united against [[Donald Trump]] for pulling [[United States]] troops out of [[Syria]].

(00:49:09) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] criticized the [[United States]] for maintaining simultaneous alliances with both [[Turkey]], a [[NATO]] member, and the Kurdish [[Syrian Democratic Forces|YPG]], which [[Turkey]] considers a terrorist group linked to the [[Kurdistan Workers' Party|PKK]].

(00:50:09) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] suggested the [[United States]] should consider expelling [[Turkey]] from [[NATO]] due to its actions, including purchasing military equipment from [[Russia]] and threatening to send migrants into [[Europe]].
