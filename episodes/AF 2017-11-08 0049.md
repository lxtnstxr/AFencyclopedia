## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes and Stephen Bonnell debated the 1965 Hart-Celler Act's alignment with historical public opinion and founding intent. Fuentes argued the Act overturned 200-year immigration precedent without a national referendum and misled the public about its demographic consequences, while Bonnell cited contemporary polls and bipartisan congressional support for the law. Their debate extended to the U.S. Constitution's original intent, with Fuentes citing founders like Benjamin Franklin and Alexander Hamilton on racial homogeneity and Bonnell referencing John Quincy Adams and George Washington on assimilation.
