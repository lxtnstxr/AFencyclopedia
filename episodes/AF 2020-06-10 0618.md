## Summary

Following the death of George Floyd, widespread protests led to the removal or toppling of statues of Christopher Columbus and Confederate figures across the United States. Donald Trump rejected calls to rename military bases named for Confederate generals, while Army Secretary Ryan McCarthy and Defense Secretary Mark Esper had shown openness to the idea. Governor Andy Beshear of Kentucky proposed free health insurance for black residents, citing COVID-19 inequalities, and announced new implicit bias training for law enforcement. Nicholas J. Fuentes argued that such proposals and corporate donations to Black Lives Matter constituted a wealth transfer, and he defended Confederate monuments as heritage.
