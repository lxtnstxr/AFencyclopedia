## Summary

Nationalist parties in France, Italy, Hungary, and the United Kingdom performed well in the European Union Parliament elections. A Bloomberg article reported that foreign-born workers in Japan have expanded steadily since Shinzo Abe became Prime Minister in 2012. Nicholas J. Fuentes argued that Japan's unique culture and racial homogeneity are valuable and threatened by immigration.

(00:00:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] hosts the America First show, announcing coverage of the [[European Union]] Parliament election results and an article from [[Bloomberg]] about immigration in [[Japan]].

(00:02:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that nationalist parties in [[France]], [[Italy]], [[Hungary]], and the [[United Kingdom]] performed well in the [[European Union]] Parliament elections, but the results were more nuanced than initial appearances suggested.

(00:04:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced he would analyze a [[Bloomberg]] article detailing a new immigration program in [[Japan]] initiated under former Prime Minister [[Shinzo Abe]].

(00:07:40) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] criticized [[Jeff Bezos]]'s ex-wife, [[MacKenzie Scott]], for receiving a $37 billion divorce settlement and then being celebrated for pledging to give half of it to charity.

(00:11:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] gave an unironic salute to American troops and veterans in observance of Memorial Day.

(00:12:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] began analyzing a [[Bloomberg]] article which reported that [[Tokyo]] is becoming more ethnically diverse due to increased immigration and tourism, a trend the article framed as a positive development.

(00:15:30) The [[Bloomberg]] article stated that since [[Shinzo Abe]] became Prime Minister in 2012, the number of foreign-born workers in [[Japan]] has expanded steadily, marking a major shift from the country's historically restrictive policies.

(00:16:20) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] characterized the [[Bloomberg]] article's celebratory tone towards [[Tokyo]]'s increasing diversity as a "perverse and sick mentality" held by rootless cosmopolitans who want every city to resemble [[New York City]] or [[London]].

(00:18:50) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that [[Japan]]'s unique culture and racial homogeneity are inseparable and valuable, and that the push for immigration there sacrifices this cultural integrity for economic growth and propping up the welfare state.

(00:23:40) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] contended that both the political left and right, including figures like [[Bernie Sanders]] and [[Charlie Kirk]], prioritize economics and workers over preserving national culture, in contrast to nationalist movements.

(00:25:50) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that the immigration program in [[Japan]] is justified by elites as necessary to support the country's economy and welfare state, sacrificing thousands of years of cultural consistency for financial gain.

(00:29:30) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] expressed sadness that [[Japan]], which he viewed as a last vestige of a homogeneous culture, is also succumbing to mass immigration, though he held out hope that the Japanese would resist more effectively than Europeans.

(00:30:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] began his analysis of the [[European Union]] Parliament elections, noting he would provide a detailed breakdown on the premium show.

(00:31:50) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] explained that the [[European Union]] has a "democratic deficit" because the [[European Commission]], not the elected Parliament, drafts all legislation, rendering the elections largely symbolic.

(00:35:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] reported that in [[France]], [[Marine Le Pen]]'s [[National Rally (France)|National Rally]] party narrowly beat President [[Emmanuel Macron]]'s coalition by 23% to 22%, a victory he considered modest given Macron's deep unpopularity.

(00:38:40) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] reported that in [[Italy]], [[Matteo Salvini]]'s [[Lega (political party)|League]] party won a sweeping victory with 34% of the vote, making it one of the largest single parties in the [[European Union]] and prompting Salvini to call for new national elections.

(00:40:50) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] noted that as Interior Minister, [[Matteo Salvini]] reduced illegal immigration into [[Italy]] by 92% since 2015.

(00:41:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] reported that in [[Hungary]], Prime Minister [[Viktor Orbán]]'s [[Fidesz]] party won an outright majority with over 52% of the vote.

(00:41:20) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated there was potential for [[Marine Le Pen]], [[Matteo Salvini]], and [[Viktor Orbán]] to form a Eurosceptic, nationalist coalition within the [[European Union]] Parliament.

(00:42:40) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] concluded that while nationalist gains in the [[European Union]] elections were modest, the significant takeaway was the collapse of the centrist establishment parties, which lost 70 seats and saw their combined share drop from 54% to 43%.
