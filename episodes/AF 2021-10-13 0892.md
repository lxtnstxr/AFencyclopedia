## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes launched a show on the beta platform Cozy.TV. Texas Governor Greg Abbott issued an executive order banning all COVID-19 vaccine mandates by any entity in the state. The Biden administration proposed an IRS rule to monitor bank accounts with over $600 in annual flow, which Treasury Secretary Janet Yellen defended as targeting tax avoidance.

(00:00:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] launched the inaugural stream of his show on a new beta streaming platform called Cozy.TV, which he described as the only censorship-proof, independent live-streaming site on the internet.

(00:01:44) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced that [[Texas]], under Governor [[Greg Abbott]], had officially banned all vaccine mandates by any entity in the state.

(00:02:38) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] reported that the [[Biden administration]] was attempting to pass a new regulation allowing the [[Internal Revenue Service|IRS]] to monitor checking account balances and transactions for accounts with over $600 in annual deposits or withdrawals to audit for tax compliance.

(00:04:56) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that [[Janet Yellen]], the [[United States]] Secretary of the Treasury, defended the proposed [[Internal Revenue Service|IRS]] rule during a hearing, claiming it was designed to crack down on tax avoidance by the ultra-wealthy and close a $7 trillion tax gap.

(00:21:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] cited a report from the [[New York Times]] detailing that the [[Biden administration]]'s proposed bank reporting rule was sparking an uproar among banks and [[Republican Party|Republican]] lawmakers who viewed it as a breach of privacy and government overreach.

(00:27:50) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that the [[Internal Revenue Service|IRS]] surveillance rule, alongside recent powers granted to the [[United States Department of Justice|Department of Justice]] for keyword search warrants and the [[United States Department of Homeland Security|Department of Homeland Security]] to place informants in private chats, constituted a consolidation of totalitarian power by a regime hostile to its political adversaries.

(00:50:21) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] reported that [[Greg Abbott]], the Governor of [[Texas]], issued an executive order banning any entity, including private businesses, from requiring COVID-19 vaccinations for employees or customers, reversing his previous stance.

(00:51:59) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] noted that [[Greg Abbott]]'s order was motivated partly by [[Joe Biden]]'s September mandate requiring all employers with over 100 workers to mandate vaccines or weekly testing.

(00:57:50) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] expressed skepticism that [[Greg Abbott]] and [[Ron DeSantis]] would seriously enforce their anti-mandate and anti-censorship policies, citing [[Greg Abbott]]'s retreat on securing the [[Texas]] border and [[Ron DeSantis]]'s ineffective anti-tech censorship bill.

(01:02:44) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] criticized [[Greg Abbott]] for stating the COVID-19 vaccine was "safe and effective" while opposing the mandate, arguing that being against the vaccine itself is a necessary part of opposing the mandate.
