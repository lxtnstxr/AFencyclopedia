## Summary

Donald Trump announced a United States troop withdrawal from northeastern Syria, allowing Turkey to conduct military operations there. The Syrian Democratic Forces, led by the Kurdish YPG militia, were considered a terrorist organization by the Turkish government. Several U.S. politicians, including Lindsey Graham, Nancy Pelosi, and Mitt Romney, criticized the decision as a betrayal of Kurdish allies and a benefit to Russia, Iran, and ISIS. New York City passed a law imposing fines for using the terms "illegal alien" or "illegals" with intent to demean.
