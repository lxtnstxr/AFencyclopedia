## Summary

Donald Trump tweeted support for Covington Catholic High School student Nick Sandmann and invited the students to the White House. Nathan Phillips, a participant in the confrontation with the students, falsely claimed to be a Vietnam War combat veteran. The White House confirmed the State of the Union address would proceed despite Nancy Pelosi's request for a postponement. Kamala Harris and Elizabeth Warren announced early steps in their campaigns for the Democratic presidential nomination.

(00:25:42) During a confrontation at the Lincoln Memorial, students from [[Covington Catholic High School]] were confronted by [[Nathan Phillips]] and other Native American activists, who directed hostile remarks at them.

(00:47:34) [[Nathan Phillips]] falsely claimed to be a Marine combat veteran of the [[Vietnam War]], but military records show he served from 1972 onward, after the last [[United States|U.S.]] Marines had left [[Vietnam]].

(00:58:88) Following the confrontation at the Lincoln Memorial, [[Nathan Phillips]] and a group of approximately 20 demonstrators attempted to enter the [[National Shrine of the Immaculate Conception]] in [[Washington, D.C.]] during a Saturday evening Mass, prompting shrine security to lock the doors.

(01:11:30) [[Nick Sandmann]], a student from [[Covington Catholic High School]] who was filmed during the Lincoln Memorial incident, was interviewed by [[The Today Show]], where the interviewer pressured him to apologize for his actions.

(01:17:08) [[Donald Trump]] tweeted in support of [[Nick Sandmann]] and the [[Covington Catholic High School]] students, stating they were treated unfairly and smeared by the media, and that new footage proved the initial media narrative wrong.

(01:18:26) [[Donald Trump]] invited the students from [[Covington Catholic High School]] to the [[White House]], with plans for them to visit as early as the following day.

(01:19:58) During his 2016 presidential campaign, [[Donald Trump]] defended his campaign manager [[Corey Lewandowski]] against allegations of assaulting journalist [[Michelle Fields]], refusing to dismiss him despite pressure from figures like [[Ben Shapiro]].

(02:02:66) [[Bill Kristol]] tweeted condemning the [[Covington Catholic High School]] students as "MAGA brats" and praising [[Nathan Phillips]] as a "noble Native American," but later deleted the tweet without an apology after the full context of the incident emerged.

(02:03:24) A writer for [[National Review]] published an article falsely accusing the [[Covington Catholic High School]] students of spitting on a cross, later issuing a half-hearted apology for the claim.

(02:03:48) [[Ben Shapiro]] retweeted [[Jonathan Capehart]]'s statement of support for [[Nathan Phillips]] during the [[Covington Catholic High School]] controversy, but later deleted the retweet and did not apologize.

(02:04:57) On his podcast, [[Ben Shapiro]] criticized others for jumping to conclusions about the [[Covington Catholic High School]] incident, despite having initially retweeted condemnatory statements about the students himself.

(03:15:96) [[Nancy Pelosi]] sent a letter to [[Donald Trump]] suggesting the [[State of the Union]] address scheduled for January 29th be postponed due to the ongoing government shutdown, citing security concerns.

(03:17:12) The [[White House]] confirmed the [[State of the Union]] address would proceed on January 29th but was considering alternative venues, including a potential rally location, if the traditional address in the [[United States Capitol]] was not possible.

(03:24:56) [[Donald Trump]] maintained a government shutdown for over a month in an effort to secure funding for a border wall, a stance that solidified support among his base despite criticism.
