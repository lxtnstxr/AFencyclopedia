## Summary

Donald Trump withdrew the United States from the 2015 Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action, an Iran nuclear deal negotiated by the Barack Obama administration and signed by the P5 nations, Germany, and Iran. The International Atomic Energy Agency had confirmed Iran's compliance with the deal multiple times since 2015. Following the withdrawal, Iran's President Hassan Rouhani indicated preparations to restart uranium enrichment, while the United Kingdom, France, Germany, and the European Union jointly expressed regret over the U.S. decision. Israel and Saudi Arabia supported the U.S. withdrawal, and Israel conducted military strikes in Syria against Iranian assets shortly afterward.
