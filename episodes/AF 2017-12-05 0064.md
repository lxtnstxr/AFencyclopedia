## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes argued that the traditional family unit with mothers at home is central to his America First traditionalist and nationalist movement. He reported that Donald Trump informed Palestinian and Jordanian leaders of the U.S. embassy move to Jerusalem, a decision which drew criticism from leaders in Saudi Arabia, Egypt, Jordan, Turkey, France, and Germany. Fuentes suggested Trump’s action might align with a proposal from Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman and Jared Kushner that offered Palestinians no sovereign state. He also argued that a Supreme Court ruling against a Colorado baker in a same-sex wedding cake case would highlight a double standard regarding private sector discrimination.
