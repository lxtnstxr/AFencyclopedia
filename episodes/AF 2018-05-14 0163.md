## Summary

The 1995 Jerusalem Embassy Act mandated the United States move its embassy from Tel Aviv to Jerusalem and recognize Jerusalem as Israel's capital. Donald Trump executed the law in 2017, with the embassy opening in May 2018. On the opening day, Hamas-organized protests in the Gaza Strip led to Israeli forces killing 55 demonstrators. Official U.S. policy maintained Jerusalem should be shared between Israel and Palestine, a position contradicting Benjamin Netanyahu's annexation policies.
