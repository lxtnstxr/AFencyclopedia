## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes announced a temporary cessation of the "Groyper War" against Turning Point USA events. Ann Coulter's speech at Berkeley drew hundreds of counter-demonstrators and led to arrests. The House Judiciary Committee considered drafting four articles of impeachment against Donald Trump for abuse of power, bribery, contempt of Congress, and obstruction of justice.

---

(00:10:54) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced that the "Groyper War, phase one" had temporarily ceased after a series of disruptions at [[Turning Point USA]] events on college campuses.

(00:18:84) [[Turning Point USA]] sent an email to its mailing list declaring its recent "culture war tour" a "huge victory" and a "triumph on the college campus," a claim [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] characterized as lacking self-awareness.

(00:23:12) [[Ann Coulter]] gave a speech at [[University of California, Berkeley|Berkeley]] that drew hundreds of counter-demonstrators who harassed attendees and led to arrests, an event [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] contrasted with the more orderly conduct of his supporters.

(00:25:14) [[Ann Coulter]] wrote in a 2007 article that if the demographic change imposed by current immigration law "were legally imposed on any group other than white Americans, it would be called genocide."

(00:45:30) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that the Democratic Party's impeachment effort against [[Donald Trump]] was a rejection of the 2016 election results and a "coup" designed to bog down the administration with investigations.

(00:56:90) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that the fifth 2020 Democratic presidential primary debate featured [[Amy Klobuchar]], [[Cory Booker]], [[Elizabeth Warren]], [[Andrew Yang]], [[Kamala Harris]], [[Joe Biden]], [[Bernie Sanders]], [[Pete Buttigieg]], [[Tom Steyer]], and [[Tulsi Gabbard]].

(00:57:98) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] characterized the fifth Democratic debate as "painfully boring" and noted the live audience was largely silent, even when [[Kamala Harris]] called [[Donald Trump]] a "criminal."

(00:59:94) [[Tulsi Gabbard]] engaged in a notable exchange with [[Pete Buttigieg]] over foreign policy during the debate, criticizing regime change wars and the military-industrial complex.

(01:00:16) [[Joe Biden]] committed several gaffes during the debate, including saying the country needed to keep "punching at" the problem of domestic abuse and incorrectly claiming he was endorsed by "the only black female senator in the U.S. Senate."

(01:00:29) [[Cory Booker]] scored points against [[Joe Biden]] by calling him out for his past opposition to marijuana legalization, drawing laughter from the debate audience.

(01:03:64) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] observed that [[Joe Biden]] had remained the Democratic frontrunner in polls despite poor debate performances, while [[Elizabeth Warren]] had surged but stagnated, and [[Bernie Sanders]] had also stagnated.

(01:06:02) The House Judiciary Committee was considering drafting four articles of impeachment against [[Donald Trump]]: abuse of power, bribery, contempt of Congress, and obstruction of justice.

(01:08:28) [[Kamala Harris]], [[Amy Klobuchar]], [[Elizabeth Warren]], and [[Bernie Sanders]], all senators and Democratic presidential candidates, would need to divert campaign time to a potential Senate impeachment trial if the House voted to impeach.

(01:09:92) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued the impeachment case against [[Donald Trump]] centered on a phone call with the Ukrainian president where there was no explicit *quid pro quo* mentioned in the released transcript.

(01:10:31) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] characterized the charges of obstruction of justice and contempt of Congress as pertaining to the investigation itself, not an underlying crime, drawing a parallel to the earlier [[Russia]] investigation.

(01:13:52) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that the worst consequence of Democrats winning the House in the 2018 midterms was their ability to "run out the clock" by bogging down the Trump administration with investigations like impeachment until the 2020 election.

(01:15:74) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] compared partisan political coverage focusing on figures like [[Nancy Pelosi]] and [[Mitch McConnell]] to commentary on professional wrestling, calling it "fake and staged."

(01:17:76) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] dismissed an article in *The American Conservative* that trashed him but conceded he had "a very good point" on demographics and national identity, criticizing the publication for attacking his style while agreeing with his substance.
