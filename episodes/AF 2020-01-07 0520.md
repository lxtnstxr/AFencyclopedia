## Summary

Iran launched ballistic missiles at U.S. bases in Al-Assad and Erbil, Iraq, in retaliation for the U.S. assassination of Qasem Soleimani, which followed a Hezbollah demonstration at the U.S. embassy in Baghdad. Initial assessments showed no U.S. casualties from the strikes, and Iran warned against retaliation while threatening Israel and Dubai. Nicholas J. Fuentes argued Iran's response showed restraint and characterized the state as a rational actor, while also asserting that U.S. Middle East policy was influenced by Israel, the military-industrial complex, and the petrodollar.
