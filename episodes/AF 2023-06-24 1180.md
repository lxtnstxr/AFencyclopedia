## Summary

The episode centered on the 2023 rebellion by the Wagner Group against the Russian Ministry of Defense. It detailed the group's origins in Ukraine, its role in the 2022 invasion, and the escalating conflict between its leader, Yevgeny Prigozhin, and Russian Defense Minister Sergei Shoigu, culminating in the armed march on Rostov-on-Don. Commentary also argued that U.S. policy in Ukraine since 2014 contributed to the conditions that led to the rebellion and risked broader instability.

(00:22:04) In 2014, the [[CIA]] and the [[United States|U.S.]] State Department backed a coup in [[Ukraine]] that deposed the pro-Russian president and installed a pro-Western government.

(00:42:58) In January 2023, leaked intelligence reported by the [[Washington Post]] revealed that [[Yevgeny Prigozhin]] offered [[Ukraine]] information on Russian troop positions in exchange for a reprieve for his [[Wagner Group]] forces in [[Bakhmut]].

(00:57:50) On June 23, 2023, [[Yevgeny Prigozhin]] accused [[Sergei Shoigu]] of ordering a missile strike on a [[Wagner Group]] camp and declared an armed rebellion, framing it as a "march of justice" against the military leadership rather than a coup against [[Vladimir Putin]].
