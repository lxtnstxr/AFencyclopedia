## Summary

Donald Trump announced the 1776 Commission to promote a patriotic view of U.S. history in education, framing it as a response to the 1619 Project. The U.S. Department of Education opened an investigation into Princeton University for potential civil rights violations following its president's statements on institutional racism. Trump planned to announce a Supreme Court nominee to replace Ruth Bader Ginsburg before the presidential election, with Mitt Romney stating he would vote to confirm a nominee. Nicholas J. Fuentes argued the 1776 Commission represented a pivot by Trump and the Republican Party towards cultural conservatism and identitarianism.
