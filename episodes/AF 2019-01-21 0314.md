(00:06:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced the return of his show for its first full week and previewed topics including a presidential announcement on the government shutdown, the National March for Life rally, and the controversy involving [[Covington Catholic High School]] students.

(00:30:00) [[Donald Trump]] proposed a compromise to end the government shutdown, offering $5.7 billion for a border wall, 250 miles of new barrier, additional border patrol agents and immigration judges, and three years of deportation protection for approximately one million DACA and TPS recipients.

(00:35:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that [[Donald Trump]]'s immigration proposal was a "sensible deal" and a "down payment on the wall" intended to either be accepted by Democrats or serve as a pretext to declare a national emergency and build the wall without congressional approval.

(01:00:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] claimed the [[Covington Catholic High School]] incident, where students wearing MAGA hats faced a [[Native American]] activist and [[Black Hebrew Israelites]], was a misrepresented media narrative and that the students were provoked, not the instigators.

(01:04:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] asserted that the media and left-wing figures, including a Hollywood producer, called for violence against the [[Covington Catholic High School]] students, with threats ranging from doxing and unemployment to being thrown into a woodchipper.

(01:28:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] characterized the backlash against the [[Covington Catholic High School]] students as a racially motivated attack by a "non-white coalition" and "Jewish media" against confident white youth, contrasting it with the defense of black youths like [[Trayvon Martin]] and [[Michael Brown]].

(01:30:00) [[Nicholas J. (01:36:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that [[Ben Shapiro]], another Jewish commentator, writes for [[National Review]].

(01:39:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that the conservative movement is under a "hostile foreign occupation" and must reclaim the American right from leaders who are not aligned with white Christian interests.

(01:42:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] recounted that [[Ben Shapiro]] used similar tactics against him when he was younger.
