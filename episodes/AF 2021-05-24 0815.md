(00:59:53) The [[United States Department of Agriculture]] implemented a $4 billion debt relief program in 2021 specifically for Black, Hispanic, Indigenous, and other non-white farmers who had endured historical discrimination, as part of the $1.9 trillion COVID-19 Relief Act.

(01:00:59) White conservative farmers in states including [[Florida]], [[Texas]], and the Midwest filed lawsuits to block the USDA's race-based farm debt relief program, arguing it constituted illegal discrimination against white farmers.

(01:01:51) [[Tom Vilsack]], the U.S. Secretary of Agriculture, defended the race-based farm debt relief program at a White House briefing, stating that earlier coronavirus relief had disproportionately benefited white farmers and that the program addressed years of racial discrimination by the USDA.

(01:10:31) The [[Supreme Court of the United States]] ruled in 1995 that race-based federal programs are permissible only if they are "narrowly tailored" to accomplish a "compelling governmental interest," such as remedying an institution's own past discrimination.
