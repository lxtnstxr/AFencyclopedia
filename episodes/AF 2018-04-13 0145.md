## Summary

Youssef, a conservative-progressive computer science major, debated Steven Crowder on socialism and argued for a working-class coalition spanning the far right and left. Youssef criticized Democratic Party immigration policies and internal corruption while supporting Donald Trump's 2016 economic populism, though he found Trump's presidency insufficient on healthcare and immigration enforcement. Nicholas J. Fuentes praised Youssef's performance and later fielded calls on Second Amendment extremism and Senate Joint Resolution 54, a measure to restrict U.S. involvement in Yemen that the Pentagon opposed.
