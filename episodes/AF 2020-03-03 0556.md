## Summary

Joe Biden won the Democratic primaries in Alabama, North Carolina, and Virginia on Super Tuesday, while Bernie Sanders secured a victory in Vermont. Following Biden's South Carolina primary win, Pete Buttigieg and Amy Klobuchar withdrew from the presidential race and endorsed him. Bernie Sanders required a majority of pledged delegates to secure the nomination outright, as superdelegates would only vote on a second ballot at a contested convention. Mike Bloomberg appeared on the ballot for the first time on Super Tuesday after spending heavily on advertising in those states.
