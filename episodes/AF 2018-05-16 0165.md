## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes announced an upcoming appearance by BG Combi on his show. In 2018, progressive candidate Kara Eastman defeated Brad Ashford in Nebraska's 2nd congressional district Democratic primary. Will Nardi authored a Vice.com article criticizing Turning Point USA for its association with figures like Crystal Clanton and Lucian Wintrich. Nicholas J. (00:05:47) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced that [[BG Combi]] would be appearing as a guest on his show later that week.

(00:08:46) In the 2018 Nebraska 2nd congressional district Democratic primary, progressive candidate [[Kara Eastman]] defeated establishment-backed pragmatist [[Brad Ashford]] by less than three percentage points.

(00:09:14) The Nebraska 2nd congressional district was previously won by [[Barack Obama]] and was represented by Democrat [[Brad Ashford]] in 2014 before being won by [[Donald Trump]] in 2016.

(00:10:34) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that Democrat [[Conor Lamb]] won a special election in Pennsylvania's 18th district by presenting himself as an optimal candidate who rejected [[Nancy Pelosi]], avoided commenting on [[Donald Trump]], and focused on local issues like the opioid epidemic.

(00:13:03) The trend of progressive candidates outperforming pragmatists in Democratic primaries was also observed in the 2018 Idaho governor's race and several elections in [[Pennsylvania]].

(00:13:32) A generic ballot poll released on the day of the show indicated the Democratic Party's lead had shrunk to three points, within the margin of error.

(00:14:11) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that the FiveThirtyEight polling average showed Democrats with less than a six-point lead, below the estimated six to eleven-point margin analysts believed they needed to be competitive in House races due to Republican structural advantages.

(00:16:58) [[Will Nardi]] authored an article for Vice.com titled "TPUSA is a safe space for the worst of campus conservatism," which criticized [[Turning Point USA]].

(00:19:07) [[Will Nardi]] had previously written a Vice.com article about his "strange college rivalry" with [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]], which [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] described as portraying him as a villain, neo-Nazi, and white supremacist.

(00:22:26) [[Will Nardi]]'s Vice.com article characterized [[Turning Point USA]] as having deteriorated from a free market advocacy group into a "breeding ground of the alt-light."

(00:23:01) [[Will Nardi]]'s article cited the controversy surrounding former [[Turning Point USA]] field director [[Crystal Clanton]], who was ousted after a text message saying "I hate black people" was made public.

(00:23:38) [[Will Nardi]]'s article criticized [[Turning Point USA]] for promoting chants like "lock her up" and propaganda such as "fossil fuel saves lives," and for maintaining a "McCarthyite" Professor Watchlist database of left-leaning college professors.

(00:25:49) [[Will Nardi]]'s article cited [[Turning Point USA]]'s invitation of figures like [[Lucian Wintrich]], founder of "Twinks for Trump," and [[Milo Yiannopoulos]] as examples of the group's extremism.

(00:26:46) [[Will Nardi]]'s article praised [[Young Americans for Freedom]] as a preferable group, noting it was founded by [[William F. Buckley]] and memorialized the legacy of [[Ronald Reagan]], and had hosted [[Mike Pence]].

(00:27:11) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] criticized [[Ronald Reagan]] for failing to conserve the country's demographics, manufacturing base, and gun rights in [[California]], a state that never voted Republican for president again after his tenure.

(00:30:29) Vice.com published a photo spread titled "The Fabulous Kids of RuPaul's Drag Convention," which [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] condemned as the systematic sexualization and abuse of innocent children.

(00:42:56) [[Patrick Little]], a California veteran running for governor, was explicitly and constantly "calling out the JQ" (Jewish Question), according to a viewer comment read by [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]].

(00:48:31) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] cited [[Paul Nehlen]]'s failed electoral strategy and subsequent removal from platforms as evidence that directly addressing the "Jewish Question" is an ineffective way to win elections.
