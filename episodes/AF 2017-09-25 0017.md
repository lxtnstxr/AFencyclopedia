## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes and Steven Bonnell debated the 1965 Hart-Celler Act's demographic consequences. Fuentes cited statistics showing immigration shifted from predominantly European to Latin American and Asian sources after the act's passage. Steven Bonnell argued the act's morality depended on legislative intent and its passage reflected the democratic process. Fuentes contended the reform constituted an injustice by acting against the sovereign will of the American people.
