## Summary

Akayedullah detonated an improvised pipe bomb at the Port Authority bus terminal in New York City. Vladimir Putin visited Syria to announce a Russian troop withdrawal following declarations of the Islamic State's defeat, whose territory had shrunk to 3% of Iraq and 5% of Syria. Donald Trump ordered the U.S. military to defeat the Islamic State, leading to its near-total territorial defeat within a year. A special Senate election in Alabama featured Republican Roy Moore against Democrat Doug Jones, with a Fox News poll showing Jones leading by 10 points despite Alabama's strong Republican electoral history.

(00:03:42) A 27-year-old Bangladeshi immigrant named Akayedullah, who entered the [[United States]] seven years ago via chain migration, detonated an improvised pipe bomb attached to his person at the Port Authority bus terminal in [[New York City]].

(00:28:88) [[Vladimir Putin]] made an unannounced visit to [[Syria]] to announce the withdrawal of [[Russia|Russian]] troops, following declarations by the governments of [[Syria]] and [[Iraq]] that the Islamic State had been defeated.

(00:28:88) The Islamic State's territorial control had been reduced to 3% of [[Iraq]] and 5% of [[Syria]] as of that weekend, a rapid decline from controlling an area the size of [[West Virginia]] and 8 million people when [[Donald Trump]] took office.

(00:29:16) [[Donald Trump]] ordered the U.S. military to defeat the Islamic State, changed the rules of engagement, and delegated authority to the [[United States Department of Defense|Defense Department]], leading to the group's near-total defeat in less than a year.

(00:35:00) A special Senate election for the seat of former Senator [[Jeff Sessions]] in [[Alabama]] was scheduled for the following day, featuring Republican candidate [[Roy Moore]] against Democrat [[Doug Jones]].

(00:35:40) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced he would host live coverage of the [[Alabama]] Senate election results at 7 p.m. Eastern Time, featuring a panel including [[James Allsup]], [[Alex Witoslawski]], [[Shane Trejo]], and [[Jazzhands McFeels]].

(00:35:56) A [[Fox News]] poll released that day showed [[Doug Jones]] leading [[Roy Moore]] by 10 points, though its methodology sampled Republicans and Democrats in [[Alabama]] with only a 2% difference, a figure [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued was inaccurate given the state's strong Republican electoral history.

(00:37:52) In the previous Senate election in [[Alabama]], the Republican candidate won by 44 points, and [[Donald Trump]] carried the state by 28 points in the 2016 presidential election.

(00:39:08) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that a victory for [[Roy Moore]] would demonstrate that a candidate could succeed without support from the media, his own political party, or the "international establishment," citing the examples of [[Donald Trump]] and other America First-aligned candidates in special elections.
