## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes argued that a United States war with Iran would be nearly unwinnable and that sanctions are less costly than military engagement. Jacob Wohl advocated for regime change in Iran using all available tools, including potential military force, to prevent it from acquiring nuclear weapons. Fuentes traced advocacy for wars against Iraq, Syria, and Iran to a 1996 "Clean Break" memo written for Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu. Wohl asserted that deposing the Iranian regime is in the United States' interest to prevent a nuclear-armed state that sponsors groups like Hamas and Hezbollah.
