## Summary

Joe Biden announced his 2020 presidential candidacy with a campaign video focusing on the 2017 Charlottesville rally and Donald Trump's response. Nicholas J. Fuentes argued that the Democratic Party, represented by Biden, venerated a new set of "founding fathers" including Martin Luther King Jr., framing America as a "nation of immigrants." Jared Kushner prepared an immigration proposal for Donald Trump that included a shift to merit-based immigration, while Agriculture Secretary Sonny Perdue advocated for more foreign agricultural workers within that plan. Early national polling averages showed Joe Biden leading the Democratic primary field with 28% support, followed by Bernie Sanders at 20%.
