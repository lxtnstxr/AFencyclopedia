## Summary

The United States depleted its stockpile of long-range artillery and missiles due to military aid provided to Ukraine, halting further transfers to preserve its own national defense reserves. Tens of thousands of protesters in Prague, Czech Republic, demonstrated against government support for sanctions on Russia and the war in Ukraine, blaming these policies for high energy costs. NATO member states maintained only peacetime military production levels, which were inadequate to replenish arsenals depleted by the conflict, with U.S. 155mm artillery production covering only two weeks of Ukrainian expenditure. Russia annexed Crimea and supported separatists in Ukraine's Donbass region following the 2014 Maidan revolution, which deposed pro-Russian leader Viktor Yanukovych.
