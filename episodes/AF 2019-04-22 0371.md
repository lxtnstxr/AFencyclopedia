## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes participated in a stream with Brittany Venti and reported increased viewership for his video on a debate between Jordan Peterson and Slavoj Žižek. A series of explosions in Sri Lanka killed at least 207 people, which the Sri Lankan government blamed on the National Thowheeth Jama'ath. Hillary Clinton, Barack Obama, and Julian Castro each issued statements condemning the attacks in Sri Lanka and offering prayers for the victims. The World Jewish Congress, headquartered in New York City and led by President Ronald S. Lauder, criticized a Polish Easter tradition involving an effigy of Judas Iscariot.

(00:10:58) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] participated in a stream earlier in the day with [[Brittany Venti]], relating to her as a fellow streamer and "Charlottesville alumnus."

(00:19:58) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] reported that his video covering the debate between [[Jordan Peterson]] and [[Slavoj Žižek]] had reached approximately 45,000 views, a significant increase over his usual viewership.

(00:46:58) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] expressed anxiety that increased visibility for his show could lead to organizations like the [[Anti-Defamation League|ADL]] or the [[Southern Poverty Law Center|SPLC]] attempting to have him "shut down."

(00:52:68) A series of eight explosions in [[Sri Lanka]] on Easter Sunday killed at least 207 people and injured hundreds more, targeting churches and high-end hotels including the Cinnamon Grand, Shangri-La, and the Kingsbury.

(01:02:46) The [[Sri Lanka|Sri Lankan]] government blamed the attacks on the National Thowheeth Jama'ath, a Muslim militant group, and noted the attacks had international coordination.

(01:07:71) The attacks in [[Sri Lanka]] primarily affected the country's Christian minority, approximately 83% of whom are Catholic, constituting an anti-Christian and anti-Catholic attack on Easter Sunday.

(01:08:18) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] contrasted the global response to the [[Sri Lanka]] bombings with the reaction to the [[Christchurch mosque shootings|New Zealand mosque shooting]], noting calls to shut down websites and ban content after the latter.

(01:09:38) [[Hillary Clinton]] stated on Twitter that the world must "stand united against hatred and violence" and offered prayers for those affected by the "horrific attacks on Easter worshipers and travelers in [[Sri Lanka]]."

(01:11:12) [[Barack Obama]] described the attacks in [[Sri Lanka]] as "an attack on humanity" and offered prayers for the victims, standing with the people of [[Sri Lanka]].

(01:12:43) [[Julian Castro]] called the attacks on "Easter worshipers and tourists in [[Sri Lanka]]" deeply saddening and offered prayers for the dead, injured, and their families.

(01:15:16) A [[The Washington Post|Washington Post]] article published the morning after the attacks was headlined "Sri Lanka church bombings stoke far-right anger in the West," and noted that analysts say the majority of global terrorism victims are Muslims.

(01:16:28) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] referenced a retweet by [[Ben Shapiro]] following the [[Notre-Dame de Paris|Notre Dame Cathedral]] fire that stated, "nobody cries when a synagogue burns down."

(01:17:18) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] cited statistics indicating the number of Christians killed for their faith rose from 1,200 in 2012 to 4,136 in 2018, according to Open Doors' World Watch List.

(01:21:10) [[Emmanuel Macron|President Macron]] of [[France]] postponed an address to the yellow vest protesters for a week following the fire at [[Notre-Dame de Paris|Notre Dame Cathedral]].

(01:21:28) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] noted that yellow vest protests involving thousands of people smashing windows, burning cars, and fighting riot police had been ongoing in [[Paris]] for five months, but were not widely covered in American media.

(01:36:45) A [[BBC]] article reported that the [[World Jewish Congress]] was upset by a Polish Easter tradition where an effigy of Judas Iscariot, depicted with a big red nose, black hat, and Orthodox-style ringlets, is beaten and dragged through the streets.

(01:39:21) The [[World Jewish Congress]], founded in [[Geneva]] in 1936, is an international federation of Jewish communities and organizations with headquarters in [[New York City]] and offices in [[Brussels]], [[Jerusalem]], [[Paris]], [[Moscow]], [[Buenos Aires]], and [[Geneva]].

(01:39:26) The president of the [[World Jewish Congress]] is [[Ronald S. Lauder]], heir to the [[Estée Lauder Companies]], and the chief executive officer is [[David de Rothschild]], head of the Rothschild banks in [[France]].
