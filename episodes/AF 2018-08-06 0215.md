## Summary

Apple, Facebook, YouTube, Spotify, Stitcher, and Pinterest removed all InfoWars content, leaving Twitter as its primary host. Donald Trump appeared on InfoWars during his 2016 presidential campaign, which was propelled in part by alternative media. Following the Charlottesville rally, figures like James Alsup and Millennial Matt were removed from services like Uber and Airbnb. The New York Times retained columnist Sarah Jeong despite her tweets expressing animosity toward white people.
