## Summary

Christine Blasey Ford alleged Brett Kavanaugh sexually assaulted her in the early 1980s, though her account contained discrepancies regarding the location and number of people present. The Republican Party delayed Kavanaugh's Supreme Court confirmation vote to schedule a public hearing for Ford, who demanded a full FBI investigation before testifying. Nicholas J. Fuentes argued the allegations were a politically motivated fabrication and characterized establishment politicians as self-interested actors serving donor interests. Fuentes also recounted his political evolution to an "America First" advocate, which led Ben Shapiro to call him an anti-Semite and resulted in his ostracization from certain conservative circles.
