## Summary

Gary Cohn resigned as chief economic adviser to President Donald Trump following Trump's comments about the Charlottesville rally and due to his opposition to proposed tariffs on China. North Korea communicated a willingness to give up its nuclear program for summit talks, which President Trump described as promising while the White House maintained denuclearization as a precondition. Donald Trump ordered a missile strike on a Syrian airfield and prepared further military options against the Assad government in response to chemical weapons attacks. Nicholas J. Fuentes argued China's trade practices were responsible for a majority of the United States' trade deficit.
