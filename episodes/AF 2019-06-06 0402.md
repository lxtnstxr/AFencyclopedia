## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes characterized World War II as a geopolitical contest between Germany and the United Kingdom. He introduced a bill by Missouri Senator Josh Hawley aimed at forcing video-sharing websites to disable recommendation systems for videos featuring children. Fuentes cited Breitbart reports that illegal immigration at the U.S.-Mexico border in May reached a two-decade high and that Border Patrol apprehended over 500 migrants from Africa in one week. He reported that Donald Trump threatened escalating tariffs on Mexico, which White House Press Secretary Sarah Sanders and Vice President Mike Pence stated were still moving forward.

(00:12:52) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced that his show would cover immigration, a new bill by [[Josh Hawley]], and the 75th anniversary of D-Day.

(00:38:28) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated he was required by [[Ezra Levant]] and his producers to express excitement about hosting his show.

(01:01:04) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] described D-Day on June 6, 1944, as the largest amphibious assault in world history, an Allied invasion of [[France]] at the beaches of Normandy that turned the tide of World War II against [[Germany]].

(01:08:48) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that public figures like [[Ben Shapiro]] frequently invoke the imagery of storming the beaches of Normandy in political rhetoric, which has watered down the significance of D-Day.

(01:23:44) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] characterized World War II as a contest for geopolitical power between [[Germany]] and the [[United Kingdom]], rather than a grand struggle between good and evil.

(01:59:08) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] concluded that the lesson of D-Day is that subsequent generations, particularly baby boomers, failed in their stewardship of the civilization that the Greatest Generation fought to preserve.

(02:05:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] introduced a new bill by Senator [[Josh Hawley]] of [[Missouri]] aimed at forcing video-sharing websites like YouTube to disable recommendation systems for videos featuring children, following a report that the algorithm helped spread such content among predators.

(02:58:16) [[Josh Hawley]] stated, in a quote reported by [[Fox News]], that YouTube was pushing videos of children to pedophiles and needed to place children's safety above profits.

(03:31:52) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] cited a [[Breitbart]] report stating that illegal immigration at the U.S.-Mexico border in May reached a two-decade high, with over 132,000 apprehensions, a level not seen since the presidency of [[Bill Clinton]].

(03:45:82) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] cited projections from researcher Stephen Kopitz that over 1 million border apprehensions and 500,000 undetected illegal crossings were expected at the U.S.-Mexico border in 2019.

(03:52:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] cited a [[Breitbart]] report that U.S. Border Patrol agents in the Del Rio Sector apprehended more than 500 migrants from the continent of [[Africa]] in one week, the first large group solely from Central and South Africa recorded in border patrol history.

(04:40:56) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] reported that [[Donald Trump]] had threatened to impose tariffs on [[Mexico]], starting at 5% on June 10th and potentially rising to 25% by October 1st, if Mexico did not address immigration issues.

(04:58:16) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] reported that White House Press Secretary [[Sarah Sanders]] and Vice President [[Mike Pence]] stated the U.S. was still moving forward with tariffs on [[Mexico]], with negotiations ongoing but Mexican proposals deemed insufficient.

(05:15:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] outlined that proposed provisions in a deal with [[Mexico]] included deploying 6,000 Mexican soldiers to its southern border and implementing a "Third Safe Country" agreement to require asylum seekers to apply in the first safe country they enter, rather than the U.S.

(06:13:02) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated he had privated all videos on his YouTube channel as a precautionary measure against aggressive enforcement of new policies, but that all shows were mirrored on the alternative video hosting website [[BitChute]].
