## Summary

Donald Trump announced the United States would not respond militarily to Iran's rocket strikes but would impose additional economic sanctions. Iran refused to provide the black box from a crashed Ukraine International Airlines Boeing 737 to Boeing or the United States. Trump characterized Iran's missile strikes on Iraqi bases housing U.S. troops, which caused no American casualties, as a de-escalatory signal. Nicholas J. Fuentes argued that Iran provided Iraq with advance warning of the strikes to avoid American fatalities and a major U.S. military response.

(00:01:39) [[Donald Trump]] announced the [[United States]] would not respond militarily to [[Iran]]'s rocket strikes but would instead impose additional economic sanctions on the Iranian regime.

(00:08:45) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] reported that a [[Boeing]] 737 jet operated by [[Ukraine International Airlines]] crashed shortly after takeoff from [[Tehran]]'s international airport, killing all 176 people on board, including citizens of [[Iran]], [[Canada]], [[Ukraine]], [[Sweden]], [[Afghanistan]], [[Germany]], and the [[United Kingdom]].

(00:10:52) [[Iran]] refused to turn over the aircraft's black box data recorder to [[Boeing]] or the [[United States]], complicating the investigation into the cause of the crash.

(00:15:19) [[Donald Trump]] delivered a statement from the [[White House]] in response to [[Iran]]'s missile attacks on two [[Iraq|Iraqi]] bases housing [[United States|U.S.]] troops, which resulted in no American casualties.

(00:17:24) [[Donald Trump]] characterized [[Iran]]'s missile strikes, which caused no American or [[NATO]] fatalities, as a sign that [[Tehran]] was seeking to de-escalate tensions following the [[United States|U.S.]] killing of [[Qasem Soleimani]].

(00:18:55) The [[United States]] military did not attempt to intercept [[Iran]]'s ballistic missiles because [[Patriot]] and [[Avenger]] anti-missile defense systems were not deployed to the [[Al-Assad]] and [[Erbil]] bases that were targeted.

(00:20:07) [[Qasem Soleimani]] was the commander of the [[Quds Force]], an elite division of the [[Iranian Revolutionary Guard Corps]] that reported directly to Supreme Leader [[Ali Khamenei]] and was responsible for [[Iran]]'s military operations across the [[Middle East]].

(00:22:59) In April 2017, [[Donald Trump]] ordered a missile strike on a Syrian airfield after [[Bashar al-Assad]] was accused of using chemical weapons, a move framed as enforcing a "red line" that [[Barack Obama]] had previously declined to enforce in 2013.

(00:24:37) [[Iran]] provided [[Iraq]] with advance warning of its missile strikes on bases housing [[United States|U.S.]] troops, which [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued was a deliberate move to ensure no American casualties and avoid triggering a major military response.

(00:27:18) In his White House address, [[Donald Trump]] declared, "as long as I am president of the United States, Iran will never be allowed to have a nuclear weapon," establishing the prevention of an Iranian nuclear weapon as the central tenet of his administration's Iran policy.

(00:29:29) [[Donald Trump]] announced the [[United States]] would "immediately impose additional punishing economic sanctions" on [[Iran]] and called for the [[United Kingdom]], [[Germany]], [[France]], [[Russia]], and [[China]] to abandon the [[Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action|JCPOA]] (Iran nuclear deal).

(00:30:49) [[Barack Obama]]'s administration argued that "snapback sanctions" could be reimposed if [[Iran]] violated the [[Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action|JCPOA]], but critics contended that rebuilding an international sanctions regime involving [[Russia]] and [[China]] would be virtually impossible after the deal lifted all sanctions.

(00:32:25) [[Donald Trump]] stated that the "very defective" [[Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action|JCPOA]] was nearing its expiration and provided [[Iran]] a "quick path to nuclear breakout," while calling for a new deal that would allow [[Iran]] to prosper peacefully if it abandoned its nuclear ambitions.

(00:37:17) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] contrasted [[Donald Trump]]'s stated desire for a deal that would allow [[Iran]] to "thrive and prosper" with the position of [[Benjamin Netanyahu|Bibi]] and [[Israel]], which he argued seeks the destruction of [[Iran]] and regime change.

(00:38:12) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that [[Donald Trump]]'s foreign policy strategy with [[Iran]] mirrored his approach to [[North Korea]], combining "fire and fury" rhetoric and military posturing with an explicit offer to negotiate a deal, a strategy he termed the "Trump doctrine."

(00:39:12) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] noted that [[Israel]], [[Saudi Arabia]], and the [[United Arab Emirates]] were actively agitating for war with [[Iran]], unlike the situation with [[North Korea]], which he identified as a key difference in the geopolitical pressure on [[Donald Trump]].

(00:40:23) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] asserted that while figures like [[Mike Pompeo]], [[Sheldon Adelson]], the [[United States Department of Defense|Pentagon]], and [[John Bolton]] wanted war with [[Iran]], he believed [[Donald Trump]] personally did not want to start a new conflict.

(00:41:49) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] credited [[Donald Trump]] with showing restraint and avoiding new wars, contrasting this with the [[Barack Obama|Obama]] administration, which he said initiated interventions in [[Libya]] and [[Syria]] despite campaigning to end the wars in [[Iraq]] and [[Afghanistan]].

(00:44:14) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] dismissed as unrealistic the conspiracy theory that the [[Iran|Iranian regime]] secretly wanted [[Qasem Soleimani]] dead and colluded with the [[United States]] to orchestrate his killing, citing a lack of trust and cooperation between the two governments.

(00:45:59) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] suggested that the killing of [[Qasem Soleimani]] and the subsequent friction with the [[Iraq|Iraqi]] government, which passed a non-binding resolution to expel [[United States|U.S.]] troops, could create a pathway for the [[United States]] to withdraw its military from [[Iraq]].
