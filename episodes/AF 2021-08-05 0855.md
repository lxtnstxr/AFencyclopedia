## Summary

The White House considered a COVID-19 vaccination mandate for all foreign travelers to the United States. Ron DeSantis refused lockdowns in Florida and fought vaccine mandates by companies like Carnival Cruise Line. Nicholas J. Fuentes argued multiracial working-class populism was "Trumpism without Trump" and criticized proposed vaccine systems as permanent state control. JD Vance received $15 million for his campaign from Peter Thiel, and his campaign paid Ryan Grodusky's super PAC $15,000 monthly.

(00:20:12) The [[White House]] was deliberating on whether to mandate COVID-19 vaccination for all foreign travelers entering the [[United States]].

(00:44:30) [[Ron DeSantis]] responded to criticism from [[Joe Biden]] by stating, "Why don't you do your job and get your border secure? And until you do that, I don't want to hear a blip about COVID from you."

(00:53:08) [[Ron DeSantis]] had refused to implement lockdowns or mask mandates in [[Florida]] and was fighting against private companies, including [[Carnival Cruise Line]], that attempted to impose their own vaccine mandates.

(01:18:46) [[Ron DeSantis]] had previously used state power to target [[Ben & Jerry's]] after the company stopped selling its ice cream in the [[West Bank]], an action taken at the behest of the Jewish ambassador to the [[United States]].

(01:19:28) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that the multiracial working-class populism agenda, pioneered by figures like [[Steve Bannon]], would not achieve its goals, describing it as "Trumpism without Trump."

(01:23:47) [[Ron DeSantis]] was considered the most popular option to lead the [[Republican Party]] if [[Donald Trump]] did not run in the 2024 presidential election, according to CPAC straw polls and other polling.

(01:25:22) [[Ron DeSantis]] had earlier in the year encouraged people to get vaccinated, stating it was great and worked, though he said he would not enforce a mandate.

(01:41:20) [[JD Vance]] received $15 million from [[Peter Thiel]] for his campaign and had previously worked at the [[American Enterprise Institute]], graduated from [[Yale Law School]], and was a contributor for [[CNN]].

(01:42:58) [[Ryan Grodusky]]'s super PAC was paid $15,000 a month by the [[JD Vance]] campaign, a financial relationship that was not initially disclosed to his audience.

(01:57:34) The [[White House]] announced it was strongly considering requiring all foreign visitors to the [[United States]] to be fully vaccinated against COVID-19, as stated by press secretary [[Jen Psaki]].

(02:06:17) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] characterized the proposed national vaccine registry and passport system as a permanent mechanism of state control that would outlive the coronavirus pandemic, comparing it to the expansion of the [[Department of Homeland Security]]'s mandate after 9/11.

(02:08:52) [[Apple]] announced a new update that would scan photos on users' phones for child sexual abuse material, establishing a precedent for scanning personal devices.
