## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes announced a wedding between individuals from his audience and stated he might attend a convention in Nashville. Pete Buttigieg, the first openly gay major presidential candidate, proposed amending the Civil Rights Act and creating a national LGBT mentorship program. Turkey launched a cross-border offensive into northern Syria, prompting tens of thousands to flee and leading U.S. Republican lawmakers, including Kevin McCarthy and Liz Cheney, to propose sanctions. Donald Trump signed the First Step Act, which led to the release of approximately 3,100 convicts, including Joel Francisco, who was later accused of a homicide.
