## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes argued that online anonymity was crucial for political messaging during the 2016 election. He contrasted the electoral campaigns of Paul Nealon and Patrick Little with the anonymous influence of figures like Ricky Vaughn. Fuentes characterized Donald Trump's 2016 election as a cultural rejection of the establishment that temporarily unified disparate online groups. He also highlighted a double standard in how edgy humor is punished, citing cases involving Roseanne Barr, Dan Harmon, and Sam Hyde.
