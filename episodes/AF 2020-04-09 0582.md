## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes reported 468,000 confirmed coronavirus cases and 16,663 deaths in the United States. The Trump administration began laying groundwork to reopen parts of the U.S. economy in May, as Treasury Secretary Steve Mnuchin told CNBC companies could restart if medical conditions allowed. International Monetary Fund Managing Director Kristalina Georgieva stated the pandemic would cause the worst global economic crisis since the Great Depression. Anthony Fauci and Deborah Birx noted social distancing had reduced the projected U.S. death count to about 60,000.

(00:17:00) [[Kristalina Georgieva]], the Managing Director of the [[International Monetary Fund|IMF]], stated that the coronavirus pandemic would cause the worst global economic crisis since the Great Depression of the 1930s.

(00:40:00) The [[Trump administration]] began laying the groundwork to push for a reopening of parts of the [[United States]] economy as early as the beginning of May, amid rising unemployment numbers.

(01:38:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] accused [[China]] of lying about the severity of the coronavirus, paying off the [[World Health Organization|WHO]], and failing to shut down international travel early in the outbreak.

(03:06:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that a sustainable economic reopening required widespread testing, contact-tracing capabilities, and precautionary measures like masks to prevent a new wave of infections, as immunity from a vaccine or natural infection did not yet exist.
