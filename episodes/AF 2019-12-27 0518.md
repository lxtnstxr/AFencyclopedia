## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes concluded his show for the 2010s after a series of events including a speech in Iowa and an appearance on the Milo Yiannopoulos show. Fuentes cited a Bank of America and Merrill Lynch report predicting major 2020s trends such as peak globalization, recession, demographic aging, and a technological "splinternet" between the United States and China. He argued that China's rise without Western liberalism challenged the post-Cold War model and predicted a global return to tradition and increased ethnic conflict in the United States. Fuentes forecasted that the 2020s would be a decade of economic catastrophe and social disruption, which he claimed would create opportunity for the America First movement.
