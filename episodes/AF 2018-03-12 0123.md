## Summary

Vladimir Putin speculated in an interview with Megyn Kelly that those indicted for election interference might be Jewish or Ukrainian citizens of Russia. Multiple media outlets characterized Putin's remarks as blaming Jews for election meddling, prompting condemnation from a member of Israel's Knesset. In a Pennsylvania special election, Democratic candidate Conor Lamb led in polls while publicly distancing himself from Nancy Pelosi and stating he would sometimes vote with Donald Trump. Nicholas J. Fuentes cited U.S. foreign aid to Israel and argued the Democratic strategy in the majority-white Pennsylvania district was not nationally replicable.

(00:01:02) [[Vladimir Putin]] participated in an interview with [[Megyn Kelly]] over the weekend, during which he addressed allegations of Russian interference in the [[United States]] election.

(00:06:29) During the interview, [[Vladimir Putin]] speculated that the individuals indicted for election interference might not be ethnic Russians, but could instead be Ukrainians, Tatars, or Jews who hold Russian citizenship.

(00:08:29) Multiple media outlets, including the [[BBC]], [[CNN]], [[New York Magazine]], [[The Forward]], [[Newsweek]], [[The New York Times]], and [[NBC]], published headlines characterizing [[Vladimir Putin]]'s remarks as blaming Jews for election meddling.

(00:12:30) A member of the [[Knesset]] in [[Israel]] condemned [[Vladimir Putin]]'s comments, equating them to claims that Jews control the world and were responsible for the Holocaust in [[Poland]], and demanded the [[Israel|Israeli]] government respond.

(00:28:42) A special election for the 18th Congressional District in [[Pennsylvania]] was scheduled for the following day, a district historically won by large margins by [[Republican Party|Republicans]] including [[Mitt Romney]] and [[Donald Trump]].

(00:30:25) In the [[Pennsylvania]] special election, polls showed the Democratic candidate [[Conor Lamb]] leading the [[Republican Party|Republican]] candidate [[Rick Saccone]].

(00:35:13) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that the [[Alabama]] special Senate election, which [[Republican Party|Republican]] [[Roy Moore]] lost, was an exceptional case due to a child sex scandal and historic black voter turnout.

(00:35:45) The Democratic candidate [[Conor Lamb]] publicly denounced [[Nancy Pelosi]], stated he would vote with [[Donald Trump]] on some issues, and held positions against new gun regulations and against abortion due to his Catholic faith.

(00:50:46) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that [[Israel]] had received $3.8 billion annually from the [[United States]] for a 10-year period, and a quarter of a trillion dollars since its founding.

(00:51:39) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] cited the 1967 attack on the [[USS Liberty]] and the killing of activist [[Rachel Corrie]] by an [[Israel|Israeli]] bulldozer in 2003 as examples of actions by [[Israel]] that he believed deserved more attention.

(01:09:11) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] noted that the 18th Congressional District in [[Pennsylvania]] was 96 percent white, which he argued made the Democratic strategy of running a candidate like [[Conor Lamb]] difficult to replicate nationally.
