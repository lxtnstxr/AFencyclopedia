## Summary

Donald Trump and Vladimir Putin held a summit in Helsinki, Finland, discussing Ukraine, Iran, and accusations of Russian election meddling. Figures like John McCain, Lindsey Graham, and Chuck Schumer criticized Trump's performance at the subsequent press conference. Nicholas J. Fuentes argued that the media's focus on election meddling was a strategy to delegitimize Trump and his populist policies.

---

(00:00:08) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] began the episode of his show, [[America First]], by announcing a focus on the recently concluded [[Donald Trump|Trump]]-[[Russia]] summit in [[Helsinki]].

(00:00:30) [[Donald Trump|Trump]] held a summit with [[Vladimir Putin|Putin]] in [[Helsinki]], the capital of [[Finland]], following a European trip that included stops in [[Brussels]] for a [[NATO]] summit, the [[United Kingdom]], and [[Scotland]].

(00:00:44) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that the [[Donald Trump|Trump]]-[[Vladimir Putin|Putin]] summit represented a positive trajectory for [[United States|U.S.]]-[[Russia]] relations, which he considered vital given both nations' status as major nuclear and conventional military powers.

(00:01:36) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that he had received numerous messages from people apologizing for their criticism of him during the [[Syria]] strike controversy and asking to be unblocked on social media.

(00:03:50) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that [[Donald Trump]] represented a future for nationalist and right-wing politics within the system, providing hope that was absent with previous pro-amnesty and free trade candidates.

(00:05:45) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] contended that premature declarations of [[Donald Trump]] as a sellout after setbacks communicated a pernicious message to right-wing voters to disengage from the political process.

(00:06:46) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] compared criticisms of the [[Donald Trump|Trump]]-[[Vladimir Putin|Putin]] summit in [[Helsinki]] to those of the [[Donald Trump|Trump]]-[[Kim Jong-un]] summit in [[Singapore]], noting both were accused of legitimizing dictators but were preferable to military conflict.

(00:07:31) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] identified [[John McCain]], [[Lindsey Graham]], and [[Ben Sasse]] as "chicken hawks" who advocated for aggressive posturing against [[Russia]] but would not personally fight in a resulting war.

(00:08:19) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] mentioned conducting a Periscope stream critiquing [[Christian Picciolini]], who runs the organization [[Life After Hate]] and claims to be a former neo-Nazi turned liberal humanist.

(00:12:43) [[Donald Trump|Trump]] and [[Vladimir Putin|Putin]] held a one-on-one meeting for two hours, followed by a working lunch and a joint press conference in [[Helsinki]], their first such press conference since [[Barack Obama]] held one with [[Vladimir Putin|Putin]] in 2010.

(00:13:50) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] observed that [[Vladimir Putin|Putin]] employed power plays during the summit, including arriving an hour late and delivering a longer opening statement than [[Donald Trump|Trump]].
