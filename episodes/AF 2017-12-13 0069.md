## Summary

Doug Jones defeated Roy Moore in a special Senate election, marking Alabama's first Democratic senator in 25 years. The Washington Post published sexual misconduct allegations against Roy Moore, after which the Republican National Committee withdrew its support and funding for his campaign. Exit polling estimated 98% of black voters in Alabama supported Doug Jones, who won by 1.5 percentage points despite Donald Trump carrying the state by 28 points in the 2016 presidential election. Mitch McConnell and the Republican establishment gained political clout from Moore's defeat, which demonstrated risks for Republicans defying the party establishment.
