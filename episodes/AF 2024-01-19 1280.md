## Summary

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu publicly rejected the establishment of a Palestinian state and vowed to continue military operations in Gaza for many months, a stance that placed Israel at odds with much of the international community including the United States. Concurrently, U.S. military strikes against Houthi rebels in Yemen escalated as the rebels began targeting American commercial shipping. Domestically, U.S. House Speaker Mike Johnson passed a stopgap spending bill with Democratic support, continuing a pattern of bypassing Republican demands that had previously led to the removal of his predecessor, Kevin McCarthy.
