## Summary

A Texas hospital announced it would ration critical care by prioritizing vaccinated COVID-19 patients. The United States Department of Justice has used keyword search warrants requiring Google to identify users who searched for specific terms related to crimes. UC Health requires organ transplant recipients and donors to be vaccinated against COVID-19, citing high mortality rates for infected recipients.

(00:02:08) The [[COVID-19]] pandemic and the "vaccine holocaust" were the featured topics of the show.

(00:03:10) A hospital in [[Texas]] announced it would begin rationing critical care based on COVID-19 vaccination status, prioritizing vaccinated patients over unvaccinated ones.

(00:04:16) A woman was removed from an organ transplant waiting list because she was not vaccinated against COVID-19, with doctors citing a lower chance of survival if she contracted the virus.

(00:05:08) A leaked memo from the [[United States Department of Justice|Justice Department]] revealed the use of "keyword search warrants" issued to search engines like [[Google]].

(00:05:43) These warrants required [[Google]] to identify the usernames and IP addresses of anyone who searched for specific keywords, such as a victim's name or address, over a defined period.

(00:14:44) The warrant in a [[Wisconsin]] kidnapping case ordered [[Google]] to provide data on users who searched for three names, a phone number, or an address related to the victim over 16 days.

(00:15:44) In a separate case related to the [[R. Kelly]] trial, police used a keyword warrant to have [[Google]] share IP addresses linked to searches for an arson victim's address.

(00:16:16) The [[American Civil Liberties Union|ACLU]] expressed concern that keyword and "geofence" warrants were being requested in secret, potentially violating First Amendment rights.

(00:18:38) Geofence warrants, which allow law enforcement to collect digital activity data within a specific geographical area, were used to identify individuals inside the [[United States Capitol|Capitol]] on [[January 6]]th.

(00:40:06) [[UC Health]] confirmed a policy requiring almost all organ transplant recipients and living donors to be vaccinated against COVID-19.

(00:40:32) [[UC Health]] stated the policy was driven by studies showing transplant recipients who test positive for COVID-19 have a mortality rate between 18% and 32%.

(00:43:50) The policy was publicly confirmed after patient Leilani Lutali posted a letter on [[Twitter]] stating she was made inactive on a kidney transplant waiting list for being unvaccinated.

(00:55:16) Content creator Classical Theist argued on [[Twitter]] that each person who complies with a vaccine mandate makes it more difficult for others to resist.

(01:00:47) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that the only way to stop the enforcement of vaccine mandates was for people to collectively refuse compliance, as the system could not punish everyone.
