## Summary

The U.S. government averted a shutdown after a continuing resolution passed the House, a move that prompted Representative Matt Gaetz to file a motion to vacate the chair against Speaker Kevin McCarthy. The killing of Philadelphia journalist Josh Kruger was discussed, with claims linking his death to broader criticisms of urban crime policies and the recent elimination of cash bail in Illinois. Figures including Chicago Mayor Brandon Johnson and Cook County Board President Toni Preckwinkle were cited as examples of leadership allegedly failing to address crime, which was characterized as a form of racial reparations.
