## Summary

Donald Trump announced a coalition military strike against Syria on April 13, 2018, framing it as a deterrent against chemical weapons use and not seeking regime change. The United States, United Kingdom, and France launched 105 missiles targeting three facilities in Syria, with the U.S. notifying Russia beforehand. Following the strikes, the Assad government's forces captured Eastern Ghouta, a key rebel stronghold near Damascus. Nicholas J. Fuentes argued the strike demonstrated U.S. military willingness to create leverage in negotiations with North Korea and Iran.
