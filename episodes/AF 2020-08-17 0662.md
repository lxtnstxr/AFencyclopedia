## Summary

The 2020 Democratic National Convention opened with a recitation of the Preamble and a prayer from Reverend Gabriel Salguero. Speakers like Philonise Floyd, Kristin Urquiza, and Mayor Muriel Bowser criticized Donald Trump's leadership on racial justice and the COVID-19 pandemic. Former Republican officials including Governor John Kasich endorsed Joe Biden, who was formally nominated with Kamala Harris as his running mate. Michelle Obama delivered the convention's keynote address, stating Donald Trump was the wrong president for the country.

---

(00:00:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced he would be covering the first night of the 2020 [[Democratic National Convention]] live on his show, noting the event was being held virtually from [[Milwaukee]] due to the pandemic and would feature speeches by [[Michelle Obama]] and [[Bernie Sanders]].

(00:20:00) The 2020 [[Democratic National Convention]] opened with a recitation of the Preamble to the U.S. Constitution by a diverse group of individuals, followed by the Pledge of Allegiance led by a nurse.

(00:27:00) [[Reverend Gabriel Salguero]] delivered the opening prayer for the [[Democratic National Convention]], asking for divine blessing on [[Republicans]], independents, and [[Democrats]] alike and for guidance through the pandemic, economic uncertainty, and civil discord.

(00:42:00) [[Eva Longoria]], serving as the convention's moderator, introduced a series of video check-ins with Americans, including small business owner Scott from [[Swarthmore, Pennsylvania]], 15-year-old literary activist Marley Dias from [[West Orange, New Jersey]], farmer Rick from [[Volant, Pennsylvania]], and school nurse Michelle from [[El Paso, Texas]].

(00:56:00) Farmer Rick from [[Pennsylvania]] stated that the trade wars had a devastating effect on his family farm and that he was supporting [[Joe Biden]] because he believed current leadership would make him the last generation to farm it.

(01:06:00) [[Congresswoman Gwen Moore]] of [[Milwaukee]] welcomed viewers to the convention, highlighting the city's history of labor rights and social justice, and formally nominated [[Joe Biden]] to be the 46th President with [[Kamala Harris]] as his running mate.

(01:27:00) [[Mayor Muriel Bowser]] of [[Washington, D.C.]] recounted how [[Donald Trump]] deployed troops and used tear gas against peaceful protesters near the White House, leading her to create Black Lives Matter Plaza, and stated she did this so her daughter would not grow up under a president who fans the flames of racism.

(01:29:00) [[Philonise Floyd]], the brother of [[George Floyd]], addressed the [[Democratic National Convention]], stating that his brother should be alive today along with [[Breonna Taylor]], [[Ahmaud Arbery]], and others, and called for continued action for justice.

(01:31:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] verbally refused to participate in or respect a moment of silence for [[George Floyd]] during the [[Democratic National Convention]], calling Floyd a criminal and insulting the speakers.

(01:34:00) Singer [[Leon Bridges]] performed his song "Sweeter," which was written after the murder of [[George Floyd]].

(01:35:00) [[Joe Biden]], in a pre-recorded segment, stated the nation was in a "battle for the soul of America" and argued that the moment had come to deal with systemic racism and economic inequality.

(01:36:00) In a pre-recorded conversation, activist [[Jamira Burley]], [[Mayor Mike Duggan]] of [[Detroit]], and voting rights advocate [[Stacey Abrams]] discussed collective responsibility, economic empowerment, and the need for national voting rights legislation and policing standards.

(01:39:00) [[Gwen Carr]], the mother of [[Eric Garner]], told [[Joe Biden]] in a conversation that politicians' feet must be held to the fire to achieve lasting change on police brutality.

(01:42:00) [[Congressman Jim Clyburn]] of [[South Carolina]] spoke from [[Charleston]], tying his endorsement of [[Joe Biden]] to the city's history of slavery and the 2015 [[Emanuel AME Church]] shooting, and argued that Biden was a unifier who understood profound loss.

(01:46:00) Former Marine and TAPS mentor [[Kevin K.]] shared a story about [[Joe Biden]] unexpectedly visiting a gathering of military families, which he cited as an example of Biden's genuine character and compassion.

(01:47:00) A speaker detailed how [[Joe Biden]] helped stop the spread of [[Ebola]] in 2014 and, with [[Barack Obama]], assembled a pandemic playbook, which the [[Trump]] administration later disbanded.

(01:48:00) [[Governor Andrew Cuomo]] of [[New York]] gave a speech arguing that [[COVID-19]] exposed a weakened American body politic due to divisions and an incompetent federal government, and endorsed [[Joe Biden]] as a leader who could unify the nation.

(01:53:00) [[Kristin Urquiza]] blamed the death of her father, [[Mark Anthony Urquiza]], on [[COVID-19]] on [[Donald Trump]]'s misinformation, stating his "only preexisting condition was trusting Donald Trump."

(01:56:00) [[Megan Rapinoe]] hosted a conversation with frontline healthcare workers [[Dr. Bradley Dreifuss]], [[Aldo Martinez]], [[Michelle Boyle]], and [[Chung Lee]], who described the emotional toll, lack of PPE, and inadequate federal response to the [[COVID-19]] pandemic.

(02:01:00) Multiple speakers, including a veteran with multiple myeloma and [[U.S. Senate]] candidate [[Sara Gideon]] of [[Maine]], criticized [[Donald Trump]] for undermining the [[United States Postal Service]] and advocated for voting by mail.

(02:02:00) Singer [[Maggie Rogers]] performed her song "Back in My Body" from [[Maine]] after an introduction by [[Sara Gideon]].

(02:04:00) [[NBC News]] anchors [[Lester Holt]] and [[Savannah Guthrie]] began their network's live coverage of the [[Democratic National Convention]], noting that [[Senator Bernie Sanders]] and [[Michelle Obama]] would be the featured speakers.

(02:06:00) [[Governor Gretchen Whitmer]] of [[Michigan]] spoke from a [[UAW]] hall in [[Lansing]], crediting the [[Obama-Biden]] administration with saving the auto industry and criticizing [[Donald Trump]]'s pandemic response, while arguing essential workers were the nation's MVPs.

(02:12:00) Former [[Republican]] officials including [[Governor John Kasich]] of [[Ohio]], former [[Hewlett-Packard]] CEO [[Meg Whitman]], and former [[Congresswoman Susan Molinari]] of [[New York]] endorsed [[Joe Biden]], with Kasich stating his duty to country outweighed party attachment in unprecedented times.

(02:19:00) [[Senator Doug Jones]] of [[Alabama]] spoke from a memorial for the four girls killed in the 1963 [[Birmingham]] church bombing, citing the state's civil rights history as evidence that deep divisions can be overcome and endorsing [[Joe Biden]] as a unifier.

(02:22:00) [[Senator Catherine Cortez Masto]] of [[Nevada]] defended vote-by-mail systems and accused [[Donald Trump]] of trying to suppress votes by threatening funding and suing her state, urging viewers to help flip the [[U.S. Senate]].

(02:25:00) [[Senator Amy Klobuchar]] of [[Minnesota]] argued that unity was a means to an end and endorsed [[Joe Biden]] as a president for all of America, referencing the death of [[George Floyd]] in her state.

(02:29:00) A montage featured former Democratic presidential candidates including [[Pete Buttigieg]], [[Amy Klobuchar]], [[Beto O'Rourke]], [[Cory Booker]], [[Elizabeth Warren]], [[Andrew Yang]], and [[Bernie Sanders]] explaining why they endorsed [[Joe Biden]] and criticizing [[Donald Trump]].

(02:34:00) [[Representative Cedric Richmond]] of [[Louisiana]], a co-chair of the [[Biden-Harris]] campaign, argued that [[Joe Biden]] understood the dignity of work and would rebuild the economy with working people first, unlike [[Donald Trump]].

(02:37:00) A video segment featured [[Amtrak]] conductors who knew [[Joe Biden]] from his daily commute, recounting how he treated everyone with equal respect and kept in touch, including calling one conductor after a heart attack.

(02:39:00) [[Senator Bernie Sanders]] of [[Vermont]] gave a speech asserting that [[Donald Trump]] was leading the country down the path of authoritarianism and that the election was about preserving democracy, while outlining policy areas where he and [[Joe Biden]] agreed, such as a $15 minimum wage and expanding healthcare.

(02:47:00) [[Michelle Obama]] delivered the keynote address for the first night of the [[Democratic National Convention]], stating that [[Donald Trump]] was "the wrong president for our country," incapable of meeting the moment, and urging viewers to vote for [[Joe Biden]] "like our lives depend on it."

(03:08:00) Performers [[Billy Porter]] and [[Stephen Stills]] closed the first night of the [[Democratic National Convention]] with a performance of the song "For What It's Worth."
