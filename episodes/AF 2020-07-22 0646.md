## Summary

Donald Trump deployed federal police to Portland and announced plans to expand the surge to Chicago, Kansas City, and Albuquerque under Operation Legend. The Trump administration's policies, including a travel ban and the public charge rule, were projected to cut legal immigration by nearly 50% between fiscal years 2016 and 2021. Nicholas J. Fuentes opposed immigration based on its contribution to multiracialism and the demographic displacement of the white American population.

(00:01:04) [[Donald Trump]] deployed 150 federal police under the [[Department of Homeland Security]] to [[Portland]] to restore order amid widespread unrest.

(00:02:14) [[Donald Trump]] announced plans to send federal police into [[Chicago]] and [[Kansas City]] as part of an expansion of the federal law enforcement surge, with talks of extending the program to [[Albuquerque]].

(00:03:45) A report from [[Forbes]] projected that [[Donald Trump]] would have cut annual legal immigration by close to 50% between fiscal year 2016 and fiscal year 2021.

(00:09:50) The [[National Foundation for American Policy]] projected that the number of legal immigrants would decline by 49%, or 581,845 individuals, between fiscal year 2016 and fiscal year 2021 due to policies of the [[Donald Trump|Trump]] administration.

(00:12:03) Three policies cited as most responsible for a projected over 50% decline in the "immediate relatives of U.S. citizens" immigration category were a travel ban against predominantly Muslim countries, an April 22, 2020 presidential proclamation preventing the entry of parents of U.S. citizens, and the public charge rule.

(00:15:51) The [[Donald Trump|Trump]] administration established a refugee admissions ceiling for fiscal year 2020 that was 84% lower than the final year of the [[Barack Obama|Obama]] administration, reducing the cap from 110,000 to 18,000 per year.

(00:19:25) [[Joe Biden]]'s immigration plan included granting amnesty and a pathway to citizenship to 11 million illegal immigrants, suspending all deportations for 100 days, and raising the refugee admissions cap to 125,000 per year.

(00:20:37) [[Donald Trump]] had constructed approximately 230 miles of an 18-foot concrete and steel border fence and negotiated with [[Mexico]] to make illegal immigration more difficult.

(00:25:04) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated his opposition to immigration was based on its contribution to multiracialism and the demographic dispossession and replacement of the native European, Christian, white American population.

(00:30:45) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that only white people could perpetuate Western civilization and the United States as it had existed for the past 240 years, stating that other populations, even if professing Western values, could not create or sustain the same civilization.

(00:37:06) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] noted that up until the 1960s, the [[United States]] was approximately 90% white, with the only significant non-white population being blacks who did not possess full legal equality at the time.

(00:41:02) [[Donald Trump]] initiated a surge of federal security forces into U.S. cities, specifically targeting [[Chicago]], [[Kansas City]], and [[Albuquerque]], as part of a crime crackdown named Operation Legend.

(00:41:59) Operation Legend was named after four-year-old Legend Talifero, who was fatally shot in [[Kansas City]] in June; the boy's mother joined [[Donald Trump]] at the operation's announcement.

(00:43:13) [[William Barr|U.S. Attorney General William Barr]] stated the operation would involve sending about 200 federal agents to [[Kansas City]], a comparable number to [[Chicago]], and about 35 to [[Albuquerque]], with over $60 million in federal grants for hiring more police officers.

(00:52:50) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] characterized the problems within black communities, such as fatherlessness, poverty, and crime, as largely self-inflicted and not caused by white people, racism, or systemic issues.
