## Summary

Donald Trump issued a 48-hour ultimatum to Iran to open the Strait of Hormuz or face an all-out attack on its energy infrastructure by the United States, Israel, and Saudi Arabia. Iran stated it would retaliate by bombing energy infrastructure in the Gulf and desalination plants in the United Arab Emirates. Trump postponed the attack by five days on Monday morning, citing ongoing talks, and later claimed Iran had agreed to share the Strait of Hormuz with America. Iran demanded sovereignty over the Strait of Hormuz, the withdrawal of all U.S. military bases from the Middle East, and reparations, while the U.S. demanded the dismantling of Iran's nuclear complex and suspension of its ballistic missile program.
