## Summary

Republican state legislators in Florida proposed a bill imposing severe penalties on big tech companies that deplatform candidates for statewide office. Governor Ron DeSantis announced support for a related measure, the Transparency and Technology Act, which would levy a $100,000 daily fine for such deplatforming. Big League Politics reported that Elon University professor Megan Squire, a Southern Poverty Law Center fellow, maintained a database of individuals and had links to groups like the John Brown Gun Club. Nicholas J. Fuentes stated his intention to seek a restraining order against Squire and to query Elon University regarding her employment.
