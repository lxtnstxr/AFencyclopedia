## Summary

The Supreme Court of the United States ruled 6-3 that the Civil Rights Act of 1964 prohibits workplace discrimination based on sexual orientation and gender identity, with Justice Neil Gorsuch authoring the majority opinion. Justices Neil Gorsuch and John Roberts joined the court's liberal justices in the majority, while Justices Clarence Thomas, Samuel Alito, and Brett Kavanaugh dissented. Rayshard Brooks was shot and killed by Atlanta police after resisting arrest and taking an officer's taser outside a Wendy's restaurant. Ben Shapiro, Benny Johnson, Charlie Kirk, Ted Cruz, and Marco Rubio all publicly supported Donald Trump's presidential campaign.
