## Summary

The United States assassinated Qasem Soleimani, head of the Quds Force of Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps, in a January 2020 airstrike at Baghdad International Airport. Iran subsequently announced it would no longer abide by the limits of the 2015 Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action, a nuclear agreement from which the Trump administration had withdrawn the United States in 2018. Following the assassination, the United States deployed an additional 3,000 troops to the Middle East and Donald Trump threatened strikes on 52 Iranian sites if Iran retaliated.
