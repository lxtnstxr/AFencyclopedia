## Summary

The episode discussed the historic October 3, 2023, vote in the U.S. House of Representatives that removed Kevin McCarthy as Speaker, analyzing the preceding events including his debt ceiling deal and a stopgap spending measure passed with Democratic support. It detailed the role of Representative Matt Gaetz in initiating the removal and examined the stated positions of representatives including Marjorie Taylor Greene and Paul Gosar. Commentator Nicholas J. Fuentes offered criticism of several Republican figures and discussed his movement's activities and predictions for the 2024 election cycle.

(00:45:14) On October 3, 2023, the [[United States House of Representatives]] voted to remove [[Kevin McCarthy]] from the position of Speaker of the House, marking the first such removal in American history and giving him the shortest tenure in the role.

(00:45:14) [[Matt Gaetz]] of Florida invoked a motion to vacate the chair, a rule change agreed to in January 2023, which forced the vote that resulted in [[Kevin McCarthy]]'s removal as Speaker.

(00:45:14) The vote to remove [[Kevin McCarthy]] succeeded with eight [[Republican Party|Republican]] representatives, including [[Andy Biggs]], [[Ken Buck]], [[Tim Burchett]], [[Eli Crane]], [[Bob Good]], [[Nancy Mace]], and [[Matt Rosendale]], joining all voting [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] representatives.

(01:05:22) [[Paul Gosar]] voted to keep [[Kevin McCarthy]] as Speaker, a decision [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] cited as a reason for withdrawing his support for the representative.

(01:09:36) [[Marjorie Taylor Greene]] voted to keep [[Kevin McCarthy]] as Speaker and argued that significant change could not occur until [[Donald Trump]] won back the White House.

(01:14:32) In the deal to become Speaker in January 2023, [[Kevin McCarthy]] promised conservative holdouts the release of January 6th surveillance footage, the impeachment of [[Joe Biden]], individual spending bills, the ability to defund federal officials, investigative committees, spending cuts, and a rule allowing any single member to force a vote on his removal.

(01:14:32) Between January and June 2023, [[Kevin McCarthy]] failed to deliver on most of the promises he made to conservative members to secure the speakership, including releasing January 6th footage and moving to impeach [[Joe Biden]].

(01:14:32) In May 2023, [[Kevin McCarthy]] negotiated a deal with the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]]-controlled Senate and the [[Joe Biden|Biden]] administration to suspend the debt ceiling until January 2025 without securing significant spending concessions for [[Republican Party|Republicans]].

(01:14:32) [[Marjorie Taylor Greene]] helped [[Kevin McCarthy]] whip votes to pass the debt ceiling suspension deal in May 2023.

(01:14:32) In late September 2023, [[Matt Gaetz]] and other conservative holdouts refused to support a stopgap spending bill unless it included the HR2 immigration bill and larger spending cuts, leading to a standoff ahead of a potential government shutdown.

(01:14:32) On September 30, 2023, [[Kevin McCarthy]] bypassed conservative holdouts and worked with [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] representatives to pass a 45-day continuing resolution to fund the government with no policy concessions, averting a shutdown.

(01:14:32) Following [[Kevin McCarthy]]'s deal with [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrats]], [[Matt Gaetz]] followed through on his threat and invoked the motion to vacate the chair on October 2, 2023, leading to the successful vote to remove McCarthy on October 3.
