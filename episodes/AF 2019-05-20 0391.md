(00:10:19) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced that [[E. Michael Jones]] would be a guest on his show on Friday, May 24th.

(00:10:02) [[Bloomberg]] published an article stating that the fertility rate in the [[United States]] had fallen to 1.7 children per woman, well below the 2.1 replacement rate, and argued that the only solution to the resulting demographic and economic challenges was increased immigration.

(00:20:36) [[Donald Trump|Trump]] was planning to invoke the Insurrection Act to use the [[United States]] military and National Guard to remove illegal immigrants from the country, according to senior administration officials cited by [[The Daily Caller]].

(00:23:32) In an interview with [[Steve Hilton]] of [[Fox News]], [[Donald Trump|Trump]] expressed reluctance to implement mandatory E-Verify, arguing it was "so tough" and "not equipped" for farmers, stating it "works too well" and would harm Republican agricultural interests.

(00:42:32) During the European Union election campaign, [[Nigel Farage]] was doused with a milkshake in [[Newcastle]], following similar attacks on [[Carl Benjamin]] and [[Tommy Robinson]].

(00:44:59) [[Burger King]]'s UK Twitter account responded to a police request for a [[McDonald's]] in [[Edinburgh]], [[Scotland]] to stop selling milkshakes during a [[Nigel Farage]] event by tweeting, "Dear people of Scotland, we're selling milkshakes all weekend. Have fun. Love BK."

(00:48:45) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that major corporations like [[Burger King]], [[Disney]], and [[Goldman Sachs]], as well as donors like the [[Koch brothers]], consistently promote left-wing social causes and mass immigration in pursuit of profit, aligning with progressive activists against nationalist politicians.
