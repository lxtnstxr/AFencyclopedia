## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes was invited by the Young Americans for Liberty club to speak on immigration at Rose-Hulman College in Indiana. The 19 hijackers responsible for the September 11, 2001, attacks were foreign nationals who entered the United States legally on temporary visas, primarily from Saudi Arabia. Following the attacks, the United States launched wars in Afghanistan in 2001 and Iraq in 2003, and later became involved in the Syrian civil war. Authors of the 1996 "Clean Break" memo for Israel's Likud party, Douglas Feith, David Wurmser, and Richard Perle, later held positions in the Pentagon under the George W. Bush administration.
