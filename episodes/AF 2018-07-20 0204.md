## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes hosted leftist guest David Sherratt, who described himself as a Dorset-based leftist shitposter. David Sherratt argued that payment processors like PayPal and Patreon have no legal obligation to serve individuals they deem harmful and that such restrictions are a natural outcome of capitalism. Nicholas J. Fuentes countered that essential financial services engaging in viewpoint discrimination raises a question of public interest, citing the legal concept of money as speech. Sherratt rejected the idea that money constitutes speech and asserted that figures like Donald Trump and Tucker Carlson gain popularity by downplaying more extreme views.
