## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes produced over 400 episodes of his program "America First" from February 2017. YouTube content-restricted an episode of "America First" following comments by Ilhan Omar about the American Israel Public Affairs Committee. Congressional leaders Nancy Pelosi, Paul Ryan, and Mitch McConnell issued a joint letter demanding a retraction after Omar's criticism. Donald Trump announced an indefinite delay to planned tariff increases on China and proposed a summit with Xi Jinping at Mar-a-Lago.

---

(00:05:20) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] began hosting his nightly political commentary program, "America First," in February 2017, producing over 400 episodes across more than two years.

(00:05:56) [[YouTube]] content-restricted an episode of "America First" titled "The Truth About the Israel Lobby," disabling comments, view counts, likes, and searchability for the video.

(00:09:16) The content-restricted episode by [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] was produced following comments by [[Ilhan Omar]] about the political influence of [[American Israel Public Affairs Committee|AIPAC]].

(00:10:23) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that the content restriction of his video demonstrated the disproportionate power of Zionist organizations like the [[Anti-Defamation League|ADL]] and the [[Southern Poverty Law Center|SPLC]], which he claimed actively work to suppress criticism of [[Israel]].

(00:12:18) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that after [[Ilhan Omar]]'s comments about [[American Israel Public Affairs Committee|AIPAC]] trended, pro-[[Israel]] organizations deployed "trusted flaggers" to report and restrict related online content.

(00:13:33) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] noted that after [[Ilhan Omar]] criticized [[American Israel Public Affairs Committee|AIPAC]], congressional leaders including [[Nancy Pelosi]], [[Paul Ryan]], and [[Mitch McConnell]] issued a joint letter demanding a retraction.

(00:16:39) During the week of the monologue, [[Donald Trump]] was in [[North Korea]] and [[Mike Pence]] was traveling to [[Venezuela]], leaving [[Nancy Pelosi]] as the highest-ranking [[United States]] official on American soil.

(00:17:27) Following the previous year's "#OscarsSoWhite" controversy, the Academy Awards featured 29 non-white presenters out of 52 and awarded top prizes to films focused on racial issues.

(00:19:18) [[Alva Johnson]]'s lawsuit claimed witnesses including Florida Attorney General [[Pam Bondi]] and state director [[Karen Giorno]] saw the incident, though both later denied it occurred.

(00:21:54) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] characterized the allegations by [[Alva Johnson]] as part of a pattern of deceptive hoaxes, linking it to the recent case involving [[Jussie Smollett]].

(00:26:02) The second summit between [[Donald Trump]] and [[Kim Jong-un]] was scheduled to take place in [[Hanoi]], following their first historic meeting in [[Singapore]] in June of the previous year.
