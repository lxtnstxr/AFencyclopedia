## Summary

The U.S. Senate acquitted Donald Trump in his second impeachment trial, after which Mitch McConnell condemned Trump as responsible for the January 6th events but voted to acquit. The Republican Party faced internal censures, with state parties in Louisiana and North Carolina rebuking Senators Bill Cassidy and Richard Burr for their conviction votes. The United Kingdom government admitted it was considering domestic vaccine passports, with former Prime Minister Tony Blair pushing for a global standard through the World Economic Forum's Common Pass initiative. Nicholas J. Fuentes announced the America First Political Action Conference had reserved all tickets and warned that immunity passports were being pushed by a global coalition as part of a "Great Reset."
