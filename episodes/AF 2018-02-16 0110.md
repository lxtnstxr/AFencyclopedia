## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes rejected the concept of a white ethno-state, arguing the entire United States belongs to his movement. Fuentes cited the Civil Rights Act of 1964 as a precedent for regulating social media platforms like Twitter and Facebook to apply First Amendment protections. He expressed disappointment in Paul Nehlen's political direction, citing Nehlen's association with David Duke and his removal from the Wisconsin Republican Party. Fuentes advocated for the movement to appropriate the label "America First conservatives" to advocate for ending foreign aid to Israel and focusing on demographic issues.
