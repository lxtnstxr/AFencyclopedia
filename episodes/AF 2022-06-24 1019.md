## Summary

The United States Supreme Court overturned the 1973 Roe v. Wade ruling on June 24, 2022, ending the federal constitutional right to abortion. Justice Samuel Alito delivered the majority opinion, joined by Justices Clarence Thomas, Neil Gorsuch, Brett Kavanaugh, and Amy Coney Barrett. Clarence Thomas's concurring opinion suggested applying the same legal logic to reconsider precedents on sodomy, gay marriage, and contraception. Nicholas J. Fuentes characterized the ruling as a decisive spiritual victory and credited Donald Trump's judicial appointments for enabling it.
