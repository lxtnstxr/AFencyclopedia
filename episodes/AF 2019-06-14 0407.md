## Summary

Two oil tankers, one Norwegian and one Japanese, exploded in the Gulf of Oman near the Strait of Hormuz. The United States government and Mike Pompeo asserted Iranian responsibility, citing a Central Command video, while the Japanese tanker owner and Iran's UN mission contradicted the claim. The Democratic Party scheduled two primary debates for June 2019, with the first featuring Cory Booker and Elizabeth Warren and the second featuring Bernie Sanders and Joe Biden, who held a strong lead in early state polling. The United Kingdom's Advertising Standards Authority implemented a new rule banning advertisements featuring harmful gender stereotypes.

(00:31:32) Two oil tankers, one belonging to [[Norway]] and one belonging to [[Japan]], exploded in the Gulf of Oman near the Strait of Hormuz, a critical passage for 30% of the world's seaborne crude oil.

(00:36:42) [[Shinzo Abe]], the Prime Minister of [[Japan]], was visiting [[Iran]] for diplomatic talks aimed at improving relations between [[Washington]] and [[Tehran]] at the time of the oil tanker incident.

(00:38:21) [[U.S. Central Command]] released a video purporting to show Iranian sailors removing an unexploded mine from the hull of the Japanese-owned tanker Kokura Courageous, which the [[United States]] cited as evidence of [[Iran]]'s involvement.

(00:39:56) The Japanese owner of the attacked oil tanker stated that sailors reported the damage was caused by a flying projectile, not a sea mine, and that the impact was well above the waterline, contradicting the [[United States]] government's initial claim.

(00:40:52) [[Mike Pompeo]] and the [[United States]] government asserted they were "100% certain" [[Iran]] was behind the attacks on the oil tankers in the Strait of Hormuz.

(00:41:42) [[Iran]]'s UN mission issued a statement categorically rejecting the [[United States]]' allegations regarding the June 13th oil tanker incidents and condemning them in the strongest possible terms.

(00:42:04) [[Iran]]'s foreign minister stated that the reported attacks on Japan-related tankers were "suspicious" as they occurred while Prime Minister [[Shinzo Abe]] was meeting with Ayatollah [[Ali Khamenei]] for extensive and friendly talks.

(00:42:52) According to a [[CNN]] report, an American MQ-9 drone observed Iranian vessels closing in on the tankers hours before the attack, but the drone was later shot down by an Iranian surface-to-air missile, preventing the collection of further video evidence.

(01:22:46) The [[United Kingdom]]'s Advertising Standards Authority (ASA) implemented a new rule banning advertisements featuring "harmful gender stereotypes," including those that emphasize distinctions between boys and girls or suggest women are solely responsible for cleaning.

(01:22:56) The ASA stated the rule was prompted by a study concluding stereotypes could restrict people's choices and prevent them from fulfilling their potential, but would not prevent ads featuring only one gender or using stereotypes to challenge their negative effects.
