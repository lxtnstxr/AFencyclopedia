## Summary

The University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign rejected Steven Crowder's "Change My Mind" event, forcing him to conduct it outside. Nicholas J. Fuentes argued that the modern conservative tradition is wrongly equated with capitalism and libertarianism rather than traditional right-wing values. Fuentes traced the term "alt-right" to Richard Spencer in 2008 and later called for a reassessment of right-wing identity in 2018. A guest named Sean proposed the term "dissident right" had replaced "alt-right" and noted MIT had rated an anonymous figure known as "Ricky Vaughn" among the top election influencers.
