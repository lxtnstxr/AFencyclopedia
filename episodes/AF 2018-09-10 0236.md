## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes focused his program on competitor Tucker Carlson and the media reaction to Carlson's segment questioning the national motto "diversity is our strength." Media outlets including the Washington Post and Huffington Post accused Tucker Carlson of racism, while the organization Sleeping Giants launched an advertising boycott campaign against his show. Nicholas J. Fuentes expressed admiration for nationalist leaders like Bashar al-Assad and Vladimir Putin and argued that liberal democratic elites envision a future for America resembling Brazil.

(00:00:05) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] opened his program by announcing his return to regular streaming and previewed a show focused on a rival commentator and the media reaction to a recent segment.

(00:03:09) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that the focus of his episode would be on competitor [[Tucker Carlson]], whose show many people reportedly watch instead of his own.

(00:03:31) [[Tucker Carlson]] aired a segment questioning the national motto "diversity is our strength," asking how precisely diversity strengthens institutions like marriage or military units and why questioning it leads to censorship.

(00:06:49) In his segment, [[Tucker Carlson]] directly challenged proponents of diversity to explain how a lack of common language or values improves cohesion among neighbors or coworkers.

(00:09:10) Following [[Tucker Carlson]]'s segment, media outlets including the [[Washington Post]], [[GQ]], [[Huffington Post]], and [[Esquire]] published headlines accusing him of racism and hate-filled rhetoric for questioning diversity.

(00:09:44) The organization [[Sleeping Giants]], which [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] identified as a radical far-left group responsible for the deplatforming of [[Alex Jones]], launched a campaign sending an open letter to corporations advertising on [[Tucker Carlson]]'s show.

(00:10:14) The [[Sleeping Giants]] letter to advertisers argued that [[Tucker Carlson]]'s questions about diversity were inappropriate for corporations that sell to consumers of all races and backgrounds.

(00:14:50) The [[Sleeping Giants]] letter claimed [[Tucker Carlson]]'s points mirrored arguments found on neo-Nazi websites like the [[Daily Stormer]] and noted his past praise from prominent white nationalists like [[David Duke]].

(00:16:30) The [[Sleeping Giants]] letter also cited [[Tucker Carlson]]'s past comments about [[Latin American]] countries changing U.S. election outcomes through demographic change as further evidence of his problematic views.

(00:16:30) [[Tucker Carlson]] had previously argued that [[Latin American]] countries were forcing demographic change on the [[United States]] at a rate American voters do not want.

(00:28:52) Former Missouri Secretary of State [[Jason Kander]] responded on Twitter, asserting that [[Tucker Carlson]]'s view that diversity hurts the military is ignorant because he never served, and labeled his view on marriage as racist.

(00:31:18) Writer [[Noah Smith]] argued on Twitter that [[America]] embraces diversity not because it is easy but because it is hard, comparing it to the challenge of landing on the moon, though he acknowledged the multiracial experiment might fail.

(00:35:23) [[Tucker Carlson]] responded forcefully to the criticism, stating the organized left was lying about his segment and that the fundamental questions about what holds a nation of 325 million people together are being silenced by leaders who benefit from division.

(00:39:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that outlets like the [[Daily Wire]] and [[National Review]], along with [[Sleeping Giants]], promote a false hope that people can be convinced to see past racial and religious identities, a belief he claimed only white people are willing to adopt.

(00:47:10) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] characterized the elite consensus displayed at Senator [[John McCain]]'s funeral, attended by figures like [[George Bush]] and [[Barack Obama]], as a commitment to a managerial, globalist system opposed by populist nationalism.

(00:49:43) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] commented on actor [[David Henrie]]'s arrest for bringing a loaded gun to [[LAX]], noting he is a practicing Roman Catholic who attends Latin Mass and could be ideologically aligned.
