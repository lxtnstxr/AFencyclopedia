## Summary

The Trump administration launched a global campaign to decriminalize homosexuality, led by Ambassador Richard Grenell. Bernie Sanders announced his candidacy for the 2020 United States presidential election as a Democrat. Nicholas J. Fuentes argued the future of the American right was a battle for succession between establishment figures and media personalities.

---

(00:00:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] returned to his show after being ill the previous day, noting he had a migraine, sore throat, and runny nose.

(00:01:05) The [[Donald Trump|Trump]] administration launched a global campaign to decriminalize homosexuality in approximately 70 countries, primarily in the [[Caribbean]], the [[Middle East]], and [[Africa]].

(00:01:30) [[Ivanka Trump]] led a separate White House initiative called the Women's Global Development and Prosperity Initiative, aimed at developing economic stability for 50 million women in developing countries.

(00:04:10) [[Richard Grenell]], the U.S. Ambassador to [[Germany]] and the highest-profile openly gay person in the [[Donald Trump|Trump]] administration, led the global decriminalization effort, which began with a strategy dinner at the U.S. embassy in [[Berlin]] with LGBT activists from across [[Europe]].

(00:06:06) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that the foundation of conservatism is essentialism, derived from [[Aristotle]]'s teleology, which holds that everything has a goal-directed function, and that activities like homosexuality and marijuana use are wrong because they frustrate the essential purposes of procreation and rationality.

(00:08:19) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] cited the [[Donald Trump|Trump]] administration's failed government shutdown and subsequent capitulation on immigration as evidence of misplaced priorities, contrasting them with the successful advancement of socially liberal global initiatives.

(00:11:11) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] questioned what it meant for [[Donald Trump]] to be a conservative, given his socially liberal background from [[New York City]] and his administration's advancement of liberal policies despite failures on immigration.

(00:22:29) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that being called racist, anti-Semitic, sexist, homophobic, or transphobic by the left is a sign that one is telling the truth, as these labels are used to browbeat opponents into accepting a revolutionary transformation of [[America]].
