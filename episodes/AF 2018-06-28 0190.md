## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes hosts the program *America First*. A gunman killed five people at the Capital Gazette building in Annapolis, Maryland. Following the shooting, journalists linked the violence to rhetoric from Donald Trump and others. Various public figures, including Maxine Waters, Peter Fonda, and Madonna, made controversial statements advocating harassment or violence against political opponents. The Inspector General reported a close relationship between the press and U.S. intelligence agencies. Anthony Kennedy retired from the Supreme Court, and Donald Trump prepared to select a nominee from a pre-released list of candidates.
