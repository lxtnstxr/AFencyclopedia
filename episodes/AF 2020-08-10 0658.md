## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes reported on widespread looting in Chicago following an officer-involved shooting in the Englewood neighborhood. Chicago Mayor Lori Lightfoot condemned the downtown looting as "abject criminal behavior," while alderman Brian Hopkins criticized her for lacking an effective strategy. Black Lives Matter Chicago expressed skepticism about the police account of the Englewood shooting and planned a protest. Fuentes also cited Evanston School District 65's reopening plan, which granted priority for in-person instruction to black and brown students, among other categories.
