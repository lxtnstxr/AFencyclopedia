## Summary

Donald Trump pleaded not guilty to 34 felony charges of falsifying business records in New York, stemming from alleged hush money payments to Stormy Daniels and Karen McDougal. Manhattan District Attorney Alvin Bragg accused Trump of a "catch and kill" scheme to conceal violations of election law, though federal prosecutors had previously declined to bring charges. Commentator Nicholas J. Fuentes characterized the indictment as political "lawfare," while CNN legal analysts described the case as legally complicated and underwhelming.
