## Summary

Donald Trump imposed a 35-day government shutdown over border security funding, the longest in U.S. history. A continuing resolution reopened the government for 21 days after Trump's proposed immigration compromise was rejected by Democrats. An ABC News/Washington Post poll found 53% of registered voters blamed Trump and congressional Republicans for the shutdown.

(00:05:20) [[Donald Trump]] imposed a government shutdown on December 21st over border security funding, which became the longest shutdown in United States history at 35 days.
