## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes argued the United States government's primary duty is to protect its citizens, not foreign nationals, and that the Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals program was an unconstitutional executive action by President Barack Obama. Will Nardi conceded the program's unconstitutionality but argued deporting its 800,000 recipients would be a cruel and unrealistic fiscal expense, citing deportation costs exceeding $10,000 per person. Fuentes cited the case of DACA recipient Emanuel Rangel, who murdered four American citizens, as an example of preventable crime and stated 93% of recipients are Hispanic, largely from Mexico. Nardi cited research from the Urban Institute's Maria Ekenaji and Stanford University's Adam Davidson, arguing immigrants increase economic productivity and job creation without directly competing with U.S.-born workers.
