## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes announced a premium membership offering access to exclusive podcasts and a Discord server role. Donald Trump implemented an immigration crackdown and changed a rule in April 2018 that led to child separations at the border. The House considered two immigration bills, with the Paul Ryan bill proposing a pathway to citizenship for 1.8 million DACA recipients. White House communications framed the child separation issue as a legislative problem created by Democratic laws.

(00:10:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced the return of premium content on his website, NicholasJFuentes.com, offering an America First premium membership for five dollars a month.

(00:10:00) The premium membership included access to the weekly "America First World Report" foreign affairs podcast, the weekly "2018 Election HQ" election podcast, an audio-only version of the daily show, and a special role in the Discord server.

(00:59:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced upcoming guests for the week, including [[Jared Taylor]] on Tuesday, [[Faith Goldy]] on Thursday, and "JF" on Friday.

(01:00:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that a new episode of "America First World Report" had been uploaded to the premium page, focusing on the history of denuclearization in [[South Africa]], [[Ukraine]], [[Libya]], [[Belarus]], and [[Kazakhstan]].

(08:48:00) [[Donald Trump]] backed [[Paul Ryan]] into a corner on immigration ahead of the midterms, offering a choice between reasonable border security with DACA protections or no deal at all.

(09:52:00) Since January, [[Donald Trump]] had been winning the immigration conversation, implementing a crackdown that increased arrests and tightened restrictions on visas, green cards, refugees, and asylum seekers.

(11:34:00) The Democratic Party's focus on child separation arose after the Mueller investigation was delegitimized by an IG report, [[North Korea]] negotiations were going successfully, the economy was doing well, and an immigration fix was on the table.

(12:02:00) During the 2016 election, the Democratic National Convention featured the [[Khan family]], a Gold Star family who criticized [[Donald Trump]], leading him to attack them for about a week.

(19:05:00) In April 2018, [[Donald Trump]] changed a rule to prosecute all individuals crossing the border illegally, which led to the separation of children from parents who were detained.

(31:45:00) [[Donald Trump]] told GOP congressional leaders in a huddle on Capitol Hill that he wanted a legislative fix incorporated into an immigration bill to address child separation.

(32:23:00) [[Ted Cruz]] proposed a stand-alone bill to address child separation by making it more lenient for children to stay with parents in expanded detention facilities while parents were on trial.

(33:03:00) Two immigration bills were on the floor of the House: the Goodlatte bill and the [[Paul Ryan]] bill, with the Goodlatte bill lacking the necessary votes to pass.

(33:44:00) The [[Paul Ryan]] bill offered a pathway to citizenship for 1.8 million DACA recipients, ended the diversity visa lottery, provided a promissory note for $25 billion for a wall over future Congresses, and made minor adjustments to chain migration.

(36:10:00) A report in [[Politico]] stated that [[Stephen Miller]] and other White House aides were aggressively prosecuting illegal immigrants and cutting down on immigration to present progress on enforcement to voters by September.

(38:00:00) White House communications, including from [[Sarah Huckabee Sanders]] and Homeland Security Secretary [[Kirstjen Nielsen]], framed the child separation issue as a problem created by Democratic laws that Democrats needed to change.

(43:43:00) [[Donald Trump]]'s rhetoric on immigration countered the child separation narrative by focusing on MS-13, drug dealers, and the negative consequences of immigration seen in Europe, presenting a positive vision of law enforcement and border security.
