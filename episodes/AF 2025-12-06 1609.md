## Summary

The newly released national defense strategy, authored by Elbridge Colby, outlined a foreign policy centered on an "America First" doctrine and hemispheric security. It characterized Europe as facing a "civilizational crisis" and "erasure" due to mass migration and political censorship, claims it framed as an acknowledgment of "white genocide," while encouraging the rise of right-wing populist parties. The strategy also advocated for using the U.S. military to secure the nation's borders and called for allies in Asia and Europe to bear more of their own defense costs.
