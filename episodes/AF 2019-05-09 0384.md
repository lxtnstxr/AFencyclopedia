## Summary

The United States seized the North Korean cargo ship *Wise Honest* for sanctions violations, prompting North Korea to launch short-range ballistic missiles. Donald Trump postponed tariff increases on China in 2018, but negotiations collapsed after China reneged on commitments in a draft agreement. Facebook banned figures including Laura Loomer, Milo Yiannopoulos, Paul Joseph Watson, and Alex Jones. The Trump administration secured a donation of HIV prevention drugs from Gilead Sciences for the uninsured.

---

(00:01:55) The [[United States]] seized a North Korean cargo ship, the *Wise Honest*, for violating international sanctions by exporting coal and importing heavy machinery, marking the first such seizure by the U.S. government.

(00:02:10) In retaliation for the ship seizure, [[North Korea]] launched two short-range ballistic missiles, its second such test since November 2017, representing a significant escalation in tensions.

(00:03:30) In December 2018, at a G8 summit, [[Donald Trump|Trump]] postponed planned tariff increases on [[China]] in exchange for a promise from [[Xi Jinping]] to negotiate a comprehensive trade deal.

(00:03:46) The trade negotiations between the [[United States]] and [[China]] totally collapsed after [[China]] reneged on dozens of commitments in a 155-page draft agreement, deleting promises to change laws on intellectual property theft, forced technology transfers, and currency manipulation.

(00:07:09) In a politically motivated purge, [[Facebook]] banned a series of figures including [[Laura Loomer]], [[Milo Yiannopoulos]], [[Paul Joseph Watson]], and [[Alex Jones]].

(00:08:13) Despite [[Donald Trump|Trump]] and his campaign officials like [[Brad Parscale]] and [[Donald Trump Jr.|Don Jr.]] tweeting about the social media bans, the [[Trump administration]] took no substantive action to address the issue over the following week.

(00:09:53) Major media outlets coordinated to suppress coverage of a Colorado school shooting because the perpetrators were a transgender girl and a homosexual boy, which did not fit the preferred narrative on gun violence.

(00:14:46) During a 9/11 remembrance ceremony in 2016, [[Hillary Clinton]] collapsed, but the media universally dismissed concerns about her health as a "conspiracy theory" and "fake news."

(00:15:36) [[Alex Jones]] and [[InfoWars]] were targeted for deplatforming not solely for controversial content, but because they could reach tens of millions of people without being under the control of the mainstream media status quo.

(00:16:29) The [[Trump administration]] secured a historic donation of HIV prevention drugs (PrEP) from [[Gilead Sciences|Gilead]] for the uninsured, a policy [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] characterized as a betrayal of the administration's base in favor of homosexual advocacy groups.

(00:20:33) The U.S. Justice Department stated that the seized North Korean ship, the *Wise Honest*, was part of a scheme to export "tons of high-grade coal to foreign buyers by concealing the origin of their ship."

(00:22:02) The diplomatic progress with [[North Korea]] under [[Donald Trump|Trump]] was predicated on securing [[China]]'s cooperation to enforce sanctions, particularly on coal exports, which are North Korea's economic lifeline.

(00:24:17) [[Vladimir Putin]] met with [[Kim Jong-un]] the previous week, and both [[Russia]] and [[China]] saw an opening to resume trade with [[North Korea]] as a wedge against [[United States|American]] pressure in other geopolitical areas like [[Syria]] and [[Ukraine]].

(00:25:32) Following the missile tests, [[Donald Trump|Trump]] stated, "We're looking at it very seriously right now. Nobody's happy about it," but maintained that the relationship continues and that North Korea "wants to negotiate."

(00:26:04) The high-water mark of U.S.-North Korea diplomacy was the June 2018 summit in [[Singapore]], where [[Kim Jong-un]] and [[Donald Trump|Trump]] met for the first time and North Korea committed to "complete, verifiable, irreversible denuclearization."

(00:28:40) A second summit in [[Hanoi]] in February 2019 ended early with no agreement after [[Donald Trump|Trump]] concluded North Korea was not serious about a deal, dashing hopes for a formal end to the [[Korean War]].

(00:34:17) The [[United States]] had imposed a 10% tariff on $87 billion of Chinese goods, extended to $200 billion worth of goods, with a planned increase to 25% on all Chinese imports, which [[Donald Trump|Trump]] postponed in December 2018.

(00:34:48) [[China]]'s economy, reflected in the Shanghai Composite Stock Market Index, had suffered significantly from the U.S. tariffs, leading Chinese businesses to fear the planned 25% tariff on all $500+ billion of imports.

(00:38:49) A diplomatic cable from [[Beijing]] arrived in [[Washington, D.C.|Washington]] late on a Friday with systematic edits to the draft trade deal, reversing core U.S. demands and blowing up months of negotiations.

(00:41:56) In response to [[China]]'s reversal, [[Donald Trump|Trump]] announced the U.S. would raise tariffs on $200 billion of Chinese goods to 25% and impose a new 25% tariff on the remaining $235 billion of untariffed Chinese imports.

(00:43:38) Figures like [[Peter Thiel]] and [[Michael Anton]] noted that the American establishment reluctantly came around to [[Donald Trump|Trump]]'s confrontational stance on [[China]], recognizing the critical need to counter its rise as a regional hegemon.

(00:46:37) [[Donald Trump|Trump]]'s ability to sustain a trade war with [[China]] was underpinned by strong U.S. economic indicators, including 3.2% GDP growth and 3.6% unemployment.

(00:47:52) [[Steve Bannon]] argued that the purpose of the 2017 tax cuts was to reorient supply chains back to [[North America]] and make the U.S. competitive against [[China]], providing long-term economic leverage.
