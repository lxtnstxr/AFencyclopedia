## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes argued the Republican Party requires an economic nationalist platform to win presidential elections, citing Steve Bannon as a proponent. He identified academia as a societal influence pipeline from universities like Princeton and Harvard to Washington, D.C. think tanks. Fuentes described himself as a distributist who supports regulated markets with tariffs, citing historical examples from Germany and Francisco Franco's Spain. He advocated for reforming the Republican Party through institutional infiltration rather than secession or street activism.
