## Summary

Nicolás Maduro accused the United States of sponsoring a coup in Venezuela and later cut diplomatic ties after Donald Trump recognized opposition leader Juan Guaidó as interim president. Nancy Pelosi and Donald Trump engaged in a procedural dispute over the scheduling and location of the State of the Union address during a government shutdown. Donald Trump proposed a $5.7 billion immigration compromise for wall funding in exchange for temporary protections for DACA and TPS holders. Nicholas J. Fuentes argued against women serving on the Supreme Court, citing a belief in different ethical frameworks.

---

(00:06:46) [[Nicolás Maduro]] accused the [[United States]] of sponsoring a coup in [[Venezuela]] after the U.S. backed the opposition candidate and threatened military intervention.

(00:07:55) [[Nancy Pelosi]], as Speaker of the [[United States House of Representatives]], invited [[Donald Trump]] on January 3rd to deliver the State of the Union address on January 29th, a standard procedural formality.

(00:08:30) [[Nancy Pelosi]] later sent a letter requesting [[Donald Trump]] postpone the State of the Union address, citing an inability to provide proper security due to the government shutdown and suggesting it be delivered in writing instead.

(00:09:19) [[Donald Trump]] responded to [[Nancy Pelosi]]'s letter by canceling a congressional foreign trip to [[Afghanistan]], [[Brussels]], and potentially [[Egypt]] for [[Nancy Pelosi]] and other Congressional Democrats.

(00:09:33) [[Donald Trump]] formally replied to [[Nancy Pelosi]] with a letter stating his intent to deliver the State of the Union address "on time, on schedule, and very importantly, on location."

(00:10:15) [[Nancy Pelosi]] responded to [[Donald Trump]]'s letter by informing him the [[United States House of Representatives]] would not consider a resolution authorizing the State of the Union address in the House Chamber until the government reopened.

(00:11:49) Following [[Nancy Pelosi]]'s blockade, [[Donald Trump]] announced he would give the State of the Union address on Tuesday at an alternative location to be announced, with speculation including the border, a MAGA rally, the [[United States Senate]], or [[West Virginia]].

(00:12:49) [[Donald Trump]] proposed an immigration compromise seeking $5.7 billion in wall funding in exchange for three-year protections for approximately 700,000 DACA recipients and 300,000 TPS holders.

(00:14:44) A [[Fox News]] poll published on the day of the show indicated [[Donald Trump]]'s approval rating had fallen to 43%, down from 46% before the government shutdown, with notable declines among GOP women and evangelicals.

(00:16:51) Congressional Democratic leadership in the [[United States House of Representatives]] stated they were prepared to offer [[Donald Trump]] $5.7 billion for border security, but explicitly none for a wall, and only if he agreed to reopen the government.

(00:21:59) [[Donald Trump]] formally recognized Venezuelan opposition leader Juan Guaidó as the rightful interim president of [[Venezuela]], a move also supported by [[Paraguay]], [[Brazil]], [[Chile]], [[Peru]], [[Ecuador]], [[Colombia]], [[Argentina]], [[Costa Rica]], [[Guatemala]], and [[Canada]].

(00:22:50) [[Nicolás Maduro]] responded to [[Donald Trump]]'s recognition of Juan Guaidó by cutting off diplomatic ties with the [[United States]] and giving American diplomats 72 hours to leave [[Venezuela]].

(00:25:49) [[Mexico]], [[Bolivia]], and [[Cuba]] maintained diplomatic recognition of [[Nicolás Maduro]] as the legitimate president of [[Venezuela]].

(00:26:56) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] observed a pattern of U.S. military actions, such as strikes in [[Syria]], coinciding with diplomatic timetables involving [[North Korea]] and [[China]], suggesting a potential strategic linkage.

(00:50:33) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] speculated that a recent focus on controversies involving Christian schools, [[Mike Pence]]'s wife, and the Knights of Columbus could be a coordinated attack on Catholics, anticipating a future Supreme Court nomination of [[Amy Coney Barrett]] following [[Ruth Bader Ginsburg]]'s potential departure.

(00:59:30) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated he did not believe women belong on the [[Supreme Court of the United States|Supreme Court]], arguing that women have a different ethical framework based on caretaking and belong in the home, not in government.
