## Summary

Donald Trump was offered a bipartisan border security deal of $1.37 billion for fencing to avoid a government shutdown. Chicago police found no camera footage corroborating Jussie Smollett's account of an alleged attack and reported he refused to provide his phone records. Mitch McConnell announced a Senate vote on the Green New Deal resolution associated with Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez. Nicholas J. Fuentes argued the firing of Steve Bannon left Stephen Miller as the sole prominent America First advocate in the White House.

(00:47:00) A bipartisan deal proposed to [[Donald Trump]] by [[Richard Shelby|Shelby]] and other senators aimed to avoid a government shutdown by providing $1.37 billion for 55 miles of border fencing.

(00:58:00) During a prior government shutdown, [[Donald Trump]] offered a compromise of $5.7 billion for a border wall in exchange for temporary protections for [[DACA]] and [[Temporary Protected Status]] immigrants, which [[Democratic Party|Democrats]] rejected.

(01:05:00) [[CNN]] reported, citing sources who had spoken directly with [[Donald Trump]], that the President intended to sign the border security deal to avoid another partial government shutdown.

(01:10:00) At a rally in [[El Paso]], [[Donald Trump]] referred to the congressional border deal as "setting the table" and did not explicitly reject it.

(01:11:00) [[Lindsey Graham]], [[Richard Shelby|Shelby]], and [[Mitch McConnell]] reportedly believed [[Donald Trump]] was being persuaded to accept the bipartisan border security deal.

(01:12:00) In Fall 2017, a memorandum from the office of [[Chuck Schumer]] claimed [[Donald Trump]] would protect [[DACA]] through congressional legislation without seeking wall funding in exchange, a claim the White House denied within 48 hours.

(01:13:00) In January 2018, [[Donald Trump]] told a bipartisan meeting at the White House he would sign any deal with border wall funding, leading to reports he would accept $1.6 billion, but he ultimately did not sign such a deal.

(01:14:00) In December 2018, [[The Washington Post]] reported [[Donald Trump]] was offering $2 billion to avert a shutdown, but he did not sign the bill that reached his desk.

(01:14:00) [[Donald Trump]] had the option to declare a national emergency under U.S. Code 2808 to access $3.6 billion in military construction funds for a border wall.

(01:15:00) [[Donald Trump]] could also use U.S. Code 2293 to access $3 billion in Army Corps Civil Works funds for a border wall, which would also require an emergency declaration.

(01:16:00) [[Ann Coulter]] and others had publicly urged [[Donald Trump]] to declare a national emergency to build the border wall.

(01:16:00) White House official [[Darren Beattie|Darren Beatty]] tweeted that the national emergency option for wall funding would likely face insurmountable legal challenges from [[Democratic Party|Democrats]], the judiciary, and border property owners.

(01:17:00) [[Donald Trump]] could use U.S. Code 284 to divert $2 billion in Pentagon counter-narcotics funds for border security without declaring a national emergency.

(01:18:00) [[Donald Trump]] could access approximately $680 million in Treasury forfeiture funds for border security without declaring a national emergency.

(01:24:00) [[Jussie Smollett]], a gay actor on the show *Empire*, told [[Chicago]] police he was attacked by two men yelling anti-gay and anti-black slurs and "This is MAGA country" on January 29th.

(01:25:00) According to a report by the [[New York Post]], [[Jussie Smollett]] claimed his attackers poured bleach on him and put a rope around his neck.

(01:29:00) A patron at Lizzie McNeil's Irish Pub near the alleged attack site and neighbor Agin Mohammed expressed skepticism about [[Jussie Smollett]]'s story, citing the liberal nature of the neighborhood and the lack of camera footage.

(01:30:00) [[Chicago]] police reviewed thousands of hours of camera footage from the area of the alleged attack on [[Jussie Smollett]] and found no evidence corroborating his account.

(01:31:00) [[Jussie Smollett]] told police he was on the phone with his manager during the attack, who claimed to have heard the incident, but [[Jussie Smollett]] refused to turn over his phone and provided only heavily redacted records.

(01:32:00) [[Jussie Smollett]] asked the responding police officers to turn off their body cameras during their initial interview at his apartment.

(01:36:00) [[Kenan Thompson]] expressed support for [[Jussie Smollett]] during an appearance on *The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon*.

(01:39:00) [[Mitch McConnell]] announced he would bring the [[Green New Deal]], a nonbinding resolution associated with [[Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez|Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez (AOC)]], to a vote in the [[United States Senate]] to force [[Democratic Party|Democrats]] to go on record before the 2020 election.

(01:41:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that [[Mitch McConnell]] should have used a similar tactic to force a vote on border wall funding before the 2018 midterm elections but did not, knowing many [[Republican Party|Republicans]] like [[Marco Rubio]] would side with donors over voters.

(01:51:00) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that the firing of [[Steve Bannon]] was a substantial loss for the [[Donald Trump|Trump]] administration, leaving [[Stephen Miller]] as the only remaining prominent advocate for the America First agenda in the White House.
