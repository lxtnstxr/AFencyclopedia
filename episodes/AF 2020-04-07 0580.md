## Summary

Donald Trump threatened to withhold funding from the World Health Organization, criticizing its early opposition to travel restrictions on China. The United States recorded nearly 13,000 total coronavirus deaths and 400,000 confirmed cases, while New York reported 731 deaths in a single day. Nicholas J. Fuentes accused the World Health Organization of colluding with China to downplay the pandemic and advocated for the United States to prioritize a vaccine for its own citizens.

(00:02:50) [[Donald Trump]] may cut funding to the [[World Health Organization]] in response to its early opposition to his travel restrictions on [[China]] during the coronavirus outbreak.

(00:11:30) The global total of confirmed coronavirus cases reached 1.4 million, with the [[United States]] accounting for nearly 400,000 of those cases and 30,000 new infections in a 24-hour period.

(00:11:50) [[Spain]] reported 140,000 coronavirus cases, [[Italy]] reported 135,000, [[France]] reported 109,000, [[Germany]] reported 107,000, [[China]] reported 83,000, [[Iran]] reported 62,000, and the [[United Kingdom]] reported 55,000.

(00:12:30) The [[United States]] recorded approximately 1,800 new coronavirus deaths in a single day, bringing the total to nearly 13,000, a figure approaching the 14,000 deaths attributed to H1N1.

(00:23:05) [[Donald Trump]] threatened to withhold funding from the [[World Health Organization]], accusing it of having "called it wrong" and criticizing its January statement opposing his travel restrictions on [[China]].

(00:24:50) The [[World Health Organization]] initially refused to classify the coronavirus outbreak as a pandemic and advised against travel bans, a position [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] characterized as carrying water for [[China]].

(00:28:50) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued that the [[World Health Organization]] colluded with [[China]] to downplay the severity of the coronavirus, including its origin and spread, and misled the public for months.

(00:30:50) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] asserted that the [[Donald Trump|Trump]] administration's coronavirus response, including nationwide shelter-in-place orders and a multi-trillion dollar stimulus, had been remarkably effective despite initial misinformation from [[China]] and the [[World Health Organization]].

(00:46:50) The [[United States]] contributed $111 million in assessed dues and an additional $401 million in voluntary contributions to the [[World Health Organization]] in 2017, according to figures cited by [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]].

(00:47:30) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] advocated for the [[United States]] to develop a coronavirus vaccine and withhold it from other countries, arguing that America should prioritize its own citizens over international aid.

(00:58:50) [[Andrew Cuomo]] announced that [[New York]] recorded 731 coronavirus deaths in a single day, its highest one-day total, while [[New Jersey]] reported 232 deaths, also a new daily high.

(01:00:30) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] analyzed that coronavirus death rates in [[New York]] and [[New Jersey]] were lagging behind declining hospitalization rates, suggesting the peak in fatalities would follow the peak in hospitalizations.
