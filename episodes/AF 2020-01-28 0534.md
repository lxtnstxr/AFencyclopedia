## Summary

Donald Trump and Benjamin Netanyahu presented a 181-page Israeli-Palestinian peace plan developed by Jared Kushner. The plan allowed Israel to annex West Bank settlements and proposed a demilitarized Palestinian state with a capital in eastern Jerusalem. Nicholas J. Fuentes created the America First Political Action Conference after being banned from CPAC. A Sabra hummus Super Bowl advertisement featured drag queens Kim Chi and Ms. Cracker, while a Brooklyn elementary school scheduled a Drag Queen Story Hour.

(00:01:05) [[Donald Trump]] and [[Benjamin Netanyahu|Bibi]] jointly presented a 181-page Israeli-Palestinian peace plan at the White House, a proposal that had been in development for years and was originally slated for announcement in 2019.

(00:01:24) [[Jared Kushner]], the son-in-law of [[Donald Trump]], was the primary architect of the peace plan, working in coalition with the [[Israel|Israeli]] government.

(00:02:17) For the first time, an advertisement featuring drag queens was scheduled to air during the Super Bowl, a television event watched by over 100 million people.

(00:02:55) A public elementary school in [[Brooklyn]], the Maurice Sendak Community School, planned to host a Drag Queen Story Hour for first-grade students on February 24th to discuss gender fluidity.

(00:05:32) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] was banned from attending the Conservative Political Action Conference (CPAC) in the previous year, along with [[Patrick Casey]] and other America First conservatives who were disinvited or blacklisted.

(00:06:02) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced the creation of the America First Political Action Conference (AFPAC), a counter-event to CPAC scheduled for February 28th in [[Washington, D.C.]].

(00:07:54) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] cited the successful execution of the Groyper Leadership Summit in December, which coincided with the Turning Point USA Student Action Summit, as evidence that his movement could organize secure and professional events.

(00:13:29) The Super Bowl advertisement for Sabra hummus featured drag queens [[Kim Chi]] and [[Ms. Cracker]] from *RuPaul's Drag Race*, as well as rapper [[T-Pain]] and *Real Housewives of New Jersey* stars [[Teresa Giudice]] and [[Caroline Manzo]].

(00:14:18) [[Jason Levine]], the chief marketing officer for [[Sabra]], stated the advertisement aimed to demonstrate how "versatile, relevant, and relatable" hummus is by featuring a diverse group of personalities.

(00:15:09) Marketing strategist [[Bob Wittek]], who specializes in LGBTQ audiences, told [[NBC News]] the drag queen advertisement was "revolutionary" and would bring queer messaging into millions of homes worldwide.

(00:25:21) Op-ed editor [[Sohrab Ahmari]] of the *[[New York Post]]* reported that the Maurice Sendak Community School in [[Brooklyn]] had hosted Drag Queen Story Hour in previous years and planned to do so again to enhance "inclusiveness and diversity."

(00:36:26) The Israeli-Palestinian peace plan, dubbed the "deal of the century," was developed over years by [[Jared Kushner]] in collaboration with the [[Israel|Israeli]] government under [[Benjamin Netanyahu|Bibi]].

(00:37:36) [[Donald Trump]] stated that [[Jerusalem]] would remain [[Israel]]'s "undivided capital" under the peace plan, but that a future Palestinian state could have its capital in eastern [[Jerusalem]].

(00:38:48) The Trump administration's plan gave [[Israel]] a green light to annex Israeli settlements in the [[West Bank]] and mandated a four-year freeze on further settlement expansion in exchange for U.S. recognition of Israeli sovereignty over those settlements.

(00:39:30) [[Donald Trump]] said that neither Palestinians nor Israelis would be uprooted from their homes under the plan, a provision interpreted as allowing illegal Israeli settlements to remain.

(00:41:16) [[Benjamin Netanyahu|Bibi]] announced he would move to take the first steps in annexing [[West Bank]] settlements on the Sunday following the plan's unveiling.

(00:41:32) The peace plan demanded that Palestinians give up claims to nearly 30% of the [[West Bank]], while a map indicated [[Israel]] was prepared to provide land in the Israeli desert for a future Palestinian state.

(00:42:05) The unveiling event at the White House featured [[Donald Trump]] and [[Benjamin Netanyahu|Bibi]] flanked by dozens of right-wing supporters of [[Israel]], including donor [[Sheldon Adelson]] and GOP members of Congress.

(00:47:30) A map of the proposed peace plan showed a series of Palestinian cantons pockmarked with Israeli settlements, connected by thin corridors of roads, bridges, and tunnels that commentators noted were easily severed.

(00:50:20) The proposed Palestinian state under the plan would not be entitled to have a military or paramilitary, and [[Israel]] would retain the right to regain security control at any time.

(00:51:08) The peace plan was released on the same day a formal indictment was issued against [[Benjamin Netanyahu|Bibi]] in [[Israel]] for bribery and corruption, and one month before an Israeli election where his prime ministership was at stake.

(00:51:42) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] argued the purpose of the one-sided peace plan was to be a non-starter for Palestinians, thereby allowing [[Israel]] to claim it wanted peace while continuing to annex territory after Palestinian rejection.

(00:58:06) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] characterized the relationship between the [[United States]] and [[Israel]] as parasitic, arguing that U.S. foreign policy had been hijacked by [[Jared Kushner]] and the [[Israel|Israeli]] government to benefit [[Israel]] at America's expense.

(01:00:00) [[Nicholas J. (01:03:15) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] asserted that figures like [[Sheldon Adelson]], [[Benjamin Netanyahu|Bibi]], and [[Jared Kushner]] were "running the show" in U.S. foreign policy regarding [[Israel]], which he considered a disgrace.
