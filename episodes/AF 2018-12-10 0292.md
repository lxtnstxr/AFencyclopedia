## Summary

Nicholas J. Fuentes announced a computer upgrade for his show and released a premium episode rebutting "Trump skepticism." Donald Trump appointed William Barr as Attorney General, replacing Jeff Sessions. Gavin McInnes was fired from CRTV and banned from YouTube, Twitter, Facebook, and Instagram, following the FBI's classification of his founded group, the Proud Boys, as extremist. Nicholas J. Fuentes criticized McInnes's ideology and identified combating tech censorship as a critical political priority, while also outlining personnel and funding criteria for the Trump administration.

(00:03:20) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] announced the upgrade of the show's computer system with a new graphics card to improve stream quality and facilitate more gaming content.

(00:03:40) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] stated that the premium episode titled "Day the Black Pill" contained a substantial rebuttal to recent "Trump skepticism" within certain online circles, arguing for continued support of the [[Donald Trump|Trump]] movement.

(00:04:00) [[Gavin McInnes]] was fired from his job at [[CRTV]] over the weekend and subsequently banned from [[YouTube]], losing a channel with 220,000 subscribers, as part of a broader deplatforming campaign.

(00:04:20) [[William Barr]] was appointed as the new Attorney General by [[Donald Trump]] over the weekend, replacing [[Jeff Sessions]].

(00:04:40) [[Gavin McInnes]] had previously been a prominent media figure with appearances on [[Fox News]], [[Compound Media]] with [[Anthony Cumia]], and a show on [[CRTV]], before being systematically removed from major platforms.

(00:05:00) [[Gavin McInnes]] was banned from [[Twitter]] in August because the [[Proud Boys]], a group he founded, was classified as a gang by the platform.

(00:05:20) Following his removal from [[Twitter]], [[Gavin McInnes]] was also banned from [[Facebook]] and [[Instagram]], and was recently denied a visa to enter [[Australia]].

(00:06:00) The merger between [[CRTV]] and [[Glenn Beck]]'s [[The Blaze TV]] occurred shortly before [[Gavin McInnes]] was fired from [[CRTV]], though [[Glenn Beck]] publicly denied involvement in the decision.

(00:07:20) [[YouTube]] cited copyright infringement as the official reason for banning [[Gavin McInnes]]'s channel, leading to speculation that [[CRTV]] may have flagged his content after terminating his employment.

(00:10:40) [[Nicholas J. Fuentes]] criticized [[Gavin McInnes]] for using his platform to advance what he termed "basic bitch conservatism" instead of addressing issues like criticism of technology, egalitarianism, and the free market.
